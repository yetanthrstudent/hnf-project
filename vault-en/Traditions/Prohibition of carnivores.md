# Prohibition of carnivores (نهي عن كل ذي ناب من السباع)

Hadith prohibiting the consumption of any fanged animal (carnivore) and any taloned bird. It has become a pillar of Islamic dietary law, yet was **unknown to the scholars of the Ḥijāz** until its transmission by judges and scholars of al-Shām.

## The text

> « نَهَى رَسُولُ اللهِ ﷺ عَنْ أَكْلِ كُلِّ ذِي نَابٍ مِنَ السِّبَاعِ »
>
> "The Messenger of God prohibited eating any fanged animal among carnivores."

## [[Persons/al-Zuhrī|Al-Zuhrī]]'s admission

Al-Zuhrī himself acknowledges in Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (n° 1932, [p. 5956](https://shamela.ws/book/711/5956)):

> « وَلَمْ أَسْمَعْ ذَلِكَ مِنْ عُلَمَائِنَا بِالْحِجَازِ حَتَّى حَدَّثَنِي أَبُو إِدْرِيسَ، وَكَانَ مِنْ فُقَهَاءِ أَهْلِ الشَّامِ »
>
> "**I had never heard this from our scholars in the Ḥijāz** until Abū Idrīs told me about it — and he was among the jurists of al-Shām."

## The Syrian chains

All the chains of this hadith pass through **Syrian** transmitters:

| Syrian transmitter | Master | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **[[Persons/Abū Idrīs al-Khawlānī\|Abū Idrīs al-Khawlānī]]** (qāḍī of 'Abd al-Malik) | Abū Tha'laba al-Khushanī | [Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 1932](https://shamela.ws/book/711/5956) |
| **[[Persons/Khālid ibn Ma'dān\|Khālid ibn Ma'dān]]** (Homs) ← Jubayr ibn Nufayr | Abū Tha'laba al-Khushanī | [Musnad Aḥmad / Nasā'ī](https://shamela.ws/book/12748/8993) |
| **Muslim ibn Mishkam** (kātib of Abū al-Dardā', Damascus) | Abū Tha'laba al-Khushanī | [al-Musnad al-Jāmi'](https://shamela.ws/book/12748/8993) |
| **[[Persons/Maymūn ibn Mahrān\|Maymūn ibn Mahrān]]** (Jazīra) | Ibn 'Abbās | [Musnad Aḥmad n° 2619](https://shamela.ws/book/25794/1887) |

## The pattern

```
Abū Tha'laba al-Khushanī (Companion, Shām)
    ├── Abū Idrīs al-Khawlānī (qāḍī of 'Abd al-Malik) → al-ZUHRĪ (Ḥijāz)
    ├── Khālid ibn Ma'dān (police chief of Yazīd, Homs)
    └── Muslim ibn Mishkam (kātib of Abū al-Dardā', Damascus)

Ibn 'Abbās ← Maymūn ibn Mahrān (senior Umayyad official, Jazīra)
```

All the transmitters are **Umayyad officials or judges** from al-Shām and the Jazīra. The greatest scholar of the Ḥijāz (al-Zuhrī) had never heard of it.

## Connection with the primacy of the Sunna

This case illustrates the doctrine of the primacy of the Sunna over the Quran, defended by the same Syrian scholars:

- **[[Persons/Makhūl al-Shāmī|Makhūl]]** (faqīh of al-Shām, master of al-Awzāʿī): « القرآن أحوج إلى السنة من السنة إلى القرآن » — "The Quran needs the Sunna more than the Sunna needs the Quran" (al-Awzāʿī ← Makhūl — [al-Kifāya by al-Khaṭīb](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/29))

- **Yaḥyā ibn Abī Kathīr**: « السنة قاضية على الكتاب وليس الكتاب قاضيا على السنة » — "The Sunna overrides the Book and the Book does not override the Sunna" (same source, [al-Kifāya](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/29))

The prohibition of carnivores has **no Quranic basis**. It rests entirely on the Sunna — and precisely on a Sunna transmitted by Syrians that the Ḥijāzīs did not know.

## The Ibn 'Abbās contradiction

The chain of Maymūn ibn Mahrān attributes to **Ibn 'Abbās** the transmission of this hadith. Yet the methodological position of Ibn 'Abbās on dietary prohibitions is **diametrically opposed**: he refuses any prohibition not found in the Quran.

### The methodology of Ibn 'Abbās: the Quran alone

**Al-Ḥakam ibn 'Amr al-Ghifārī** (Companion) claimed in Basra that the Prophet had prohibited domestic donkeys. Ibn 'Abbās **refused** this position and recited the Quranic verse (al-'Atīq, [p. 50](https://shamela.ws/book/103/13050); Bukhārī 5529, Bayhaqī 19939):

> « قَدْ كَانَ يَقُولُ ذَاكَ الحَكَمُ بْنُ عَمْرٍو الغِفَارِيُّ عِنْدَنَا بِالْبَصْرَةِ، وَلَكِنْ **أَبَى ذَاكَ البَحْرُ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ**، وَقَرَأَ: ﴿قُلْ لَا أَجِدُ فِيمَا أُوحِيَ إِلَيَّ مُحَرَّمًا﴾ »
>
> "Al-Ḥakam ibn 'Amr al-Ghifārī used to say that at our place in Basra, but **al-Baḥr (the Ocean) Ibn 'Abbās refused** and recited: 'Say: I find not in what is revealed to me anything forbidden...'" (Quran 6:145)

The general principle of Ibn 'Abbās, reported in the same passage (Bayhaqī 19939):

> « وَقَدْ كَانَ أَهْلُ الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ يَتْرُكُونَ أَشْيَاءَ تَقَذُّرًا فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى كِتَابَهُ وَبَيَّنَ حَلَالَهُ وَحَرَامَهُ، فَمَا أَحَلَّ فَهُوَ حَلَالٌ، وَمَا حَرَّمَ فَهُوَ حَرَامٌ، وَمَا سَكَتَ عَنْهُ فَهُوَ عَفْوٌ »
>
> "**The people of the Jāhiliyya used to avoid things out of disgust. Then God revealed His Book and distinguished the lawful from the unlawful. What He declared lawful is lawful, what He prohibited is prohibited, and what He remained silent about is pardoned (ʿafw).**"

He then recited the verse (Quran 6:145) which prohibits only: carrion, spilled blood, pork.

In another passage (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 5129, [p. 49](https://shamela.ws/book/103/13049)), Ibn 'Abbās even doubts the prohibition of domestic donkeys, interpreting it as a circumstantial measure (preserving transport animals at Khaybar), then recites the same verse.

### The contradiction

| What Ibn 'Abbās says himself | What is attributed to him via Maymūn |
|---|---|
| "قل لا أجد فيما أوحي إلي محرما" — I find no prohibition [beyond the Quran] | "نهى عن كل ذي ناب من السباع" — the Prophet prohibited carnivores |
| Refuses the prohibition of al-Ḥakam al-Ghifārī (Companion) in the name of the Quran | Would have transmitted a prohibition **without Quranic basis** |
| "ما سكت عنه فهو عفو" — silence = permission | Would have taught a prohibition the Quran does not mention |

Shuʿba himself, transmitter of the hadith via Maymūn, expresses his doubts: "أكره أن أحدث برفعه" — "I dislike transmitting it as elevated [to the Prophet]" ([Musnad Aḥmad](https://shamela.ws/book/25794/1887)). And other transmitters (Ghaylān, al-Ḥajjāj) report the same hadith from Maymūn **without attributing it to the Prophet**.
