# The zamālatain of Yarmūk

During the battle of **Yarmūk** (15 AH / 636 CE), [[Persons/Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ|'Abd Allāh ibn 'Amr]] recovered **two zamālatain** (camel-loads) of **scriptural manuscripts** (كتب أهل الكتاب / كتب المتقدمين).

## The fact

Ibn Kathīr (al-Bidāya 2/107): "أصاب يوم اليرموك زاملتين من كتب المتقدمين، فكان يحدث منهما" — the material fact is contested by no one, including the defenders of the Sunna.

## The controversy

- **Bishr al-Marīsī** (d. 218 AH): first to accuse Ibn 'Amr of having presented the content of the zamālatain as prophetic hadiths. "وكان يقال له: لا تحدثنا عن الزاملتين" — "They would tell him: Don't narrate to us from the two zamālatain!"
- **Maḥmūd Abū Rayya** (20th c.): takes up the accusation in Aḍwāʾ ʿalā al-Sunna al-Muḥammadiyya
- The defenders respond that Ibn 'Amr distinguished his sources (prophetic vs. scriptural)

## Connection with the network

The zamālatain + [[Persons/Sarj al-Yarmūkī\|Sarj al-Yarmūkī]] (oral kitābī master) constitute the **dual source** of [[Concepts/Isrāʾīliyyāt|isrāʾīliyyāt]] for Ibn 'Amr, who then redistributes them via [[Persons/Nawf al-Bikālī\|Nawf]] and [[Persons/Abū Hurayra\|Abū Hurayra]] into the corpus of hadiths and tafsīr.
