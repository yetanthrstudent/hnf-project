# Hadith of the hijra to al-Shām

## Text (matn)

> "There will be a hijra after the hijra. The people of the earth will head toward the place of migration of their father Ibrahim (= al-Shām). The worst of its inhabitants will remain on earth; their land will vomit them out. [...] Peoples will be born in the East, reciting the Quran without it going beyond their throats. Each time a group is cut off, another arises, until the Dajjāl emerges among them."

## Critical analysis

The double convergence toward [[Persons/Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ\|Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ]] is the signature of a **fabricated tradition** later injected into multiple circuits to give the appearance of multiplicity of routes (taʿaddud al-ṭuruq).

The Syrian chain passes through the [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar\|Ka'b al-Ahbar]] network: Shahr (transmitter of Nawf) → [[Persons/Nawf al-Bikālī\|Nawf]] (Ka'b's stepson) → Ibn 'Amr (whom Ka'b met in Mecca).

## Political context

Produced during the revolt of Ibn al-Zubayr (64-73 AH) in order to:
1. **Sacralize al-Shām** as Ibrahim's land of hijra
2. **Disqualify the Ḥijāz** (land of "the worst inhabitants")
3. **Assimilate the opponents to the Kharijites** and to the Dajjāl
