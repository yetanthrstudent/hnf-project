# The massacre of Banū Qurayẓa — a study of the chains of transmission

After the battle of the Khandaq (5 AH / 627), the Prophet besieges the Banū Qurayẓa, accused of treason. Saʿd ibn Muʿādh pronounces the judgment: the men are executed, the women and children taken captive. The "canonical" account — that of Ibn Isḥāq and al-Wāqidī — gives a figure of 600 to 900 beheaded, trenches dug in the Medina marketplace, dramatic individual episodes (Ḥuyayy ibn Akhṭab, Rayḥāna, the woman with the millstone). This version has become a historical certainty. But who transmitted it, and through what channels?

## The independent chains — what the Ṣaḥīḥayn say

Before examining the detailed accounts, let us establish what the **independent chains** — those passing through channels other than Ibn Isḥāq and al-Wāqidī — actually transmit.

| Hadith | Isnād | Companion | Content | Mentions the number? |
|--------|-------|-----------|---------|----------------------|
| Bukhārī 4117, 4122 | Hishām → ʿUrwa → ʿĀʾisha | ʿĀʾisha | Jibrīl orders the march toward Qurayẓa | **No** |
| Bukhārī 4118 | Jarīr ibn Ḥāzim → Ḥumayd ibn Hilāl → Anas | Anas | Dust from the cavalry | **No** |
| Bukhārī 4119 | Juwayriya → Nāfiʿ → Ibn ʿUmar | Ibn ʿUmar | "Let no one pray ʿAṣr except at Qurayẓa" | **No** |
| Bukhārī 4028 | Ibn Jurayj → Mūsā ibn ʿUqba → Nāfiʿ → Ibn ʿUmar | Ibn ʿUmar | Naḍīr expelled, Qurayẓa killed | **No** (mentions execution, no number) |
| **Bukhārī 4121** | **Shuʿba → Saʿd ibn Ibrāhīm → Abū Umāma → Abū Saʿīd** | **Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī** | **Saʿd's judgment: "kill their combatants, capture their women/children"** | **No** |
| Abū Dāwud 4404 | Shuʿba → ʿAbd al-Malik ibn ʿUmayr → ʿAṭiyya al-Quraẓī | ʿAṭiyya (alleged survivor) | Puberty test (عانة) | **No** |

Six independent chains. **None** mentions the figure of 600-900. They evoke a siege, a judgment by Saʿd ibn Muʿādh, and reprisals — but never the number, never the trenches, never the individual narrative episodes. The ʿilal analysis (below) will show that these chains themselves are far more fragile than the initial table suggests.

### The hadith of Abū Saʿīd — the most detailed of the independents

The most substantive hadith in the Ṣaḥīḥayn is that of Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī (Bukhārī [n° 4121](https://shamela.ws/book/1681/6167)):

> نزل أهل قريظة على حكم سعد بن معاذ ، فأرسل رسول الله ﷺ إلى سعد ، فأتاه على حمار ، فلما دنا قريبا من المسجد قال رسول الله ﷺ للأنصار : « قوموا إلى سيدكم » ... فقال : تحكم فيهم أن تُقتل المقاتلة ، وأن تُسبى الذرية . قال : « لقد حكمت فيهم بحكم الملك »

> The Banū Qurayẓa surrendered to the judgment of Saʿd ibn Muʿādh. The Prophet sent for Saʿd, who came on a donkey. As he approached the mosque, the Prophet said to the Anṣār: "Stand for your master." [...] Saʿd pronounced: "That their combatants be killed and their descendants taken captive." The Prophet said: "You have judged according to the judgment of the King [= God]."

This is the factual core attested by an independent chain. No number, no trenches, no individual scenes.

### The hadith of ʿAṭiyya — the "survivor"

ʿAṭiyya al-Quraẓī, **alleged survivor**, reports the criterion used to distinguish men from children (Abū Dāwud [n° 4404](https://shamela.ws/book/1726/6171)):

> عُرِضنا على النبي ﷺ يوم قريظة ، فكان من أنبت قُتل ، ومن لم يُنبت خُلّي سبيله ، فكنت ممن لم يُنبت فخُلّي سبيلي

> "We were presented before the Prophet on the day of Qurayẓa. Whoever had pubic hair was killed, whoever did not was released. I was among those who did not — I was released."

In appearance, a direct testimony. But the ʿilal analysis (chain 6 below) will reveal that this "survivor" is a biographical phantom, and that the original version (Mujāhid) shows an anonymous man making declarations in a Kufan mosque.

## Analysis of the ʿilal — chain by chain

The six independent chains deserve individual examination. None is a direct, non-problematic eyewitness account of the execution itself.

### Chain 1: Bukhārī 4117/4122 — Hishām → ʿUrwa → ʿĀʾisha

**Matn**:

> لما رجع النبي ﷺ من الخندق ووضع السلاح واغتسل ، أتاه جبريل عليه السلام فقال : قد وضعت السلاح ، والله ما وضعناه ، فاخرج إليهم . قال : فإلى أين ؟ قال : ها هنا ، **وأشار إلى بني قريظة**

> "When the Prophet returned from the Khandaq, put down his weapons and bathed, Jibrīl came to him and said: 'You have put down your weapons? By God, we have not put ours down. Go out to them!' He said: 'Where to?' He said: 'There' — **and he pointed toward the Banū Qurayẓa**."

**ʿIlla 1: Hishām ibn ʿUrwa and Iraq.** Hishām is thiqa when transmitting in Medina. But his Iraqi transmissions are degraded — Mālik ibn Anas explicitly disapproved: **نقم على هشام بن عروة حديثه لأهل العراق** ("he blamed Hishām for what he narrated to the Iraqis"). Yaʿqūb ibn Shayba documents three stages of degradation during his Iraqi visits: first "حدثني أبي" (my father told me), then "أخبرني أبي عن" (he informed me about), then "أبي عن" (simple ʿanʿana). This hadith is transmitted with the formula **عن هشام عن أبيه عن عائشة** — complete ʿanʿana, without *ṣarīḥ al-samāʿ*.

**ʿIlla 2: the uncontrolled rafʿ of [[Persons/ʿUrwa ibn al-Zubayr|ʿUrwa]].** The problem goes beyond Hishām. ʿUrwa himself corresponded with the Umayyad caliphs (ʿAbd al-Malik, al-Walīd) in **mursal letters** — historical accounts without formal isnād, without systematic attribution to ʿĀʾisha, in a historian's register rather than a muḥaddith's. Al-Ṭabarī preserves four of these letters (beginning of revelation, hijra, Badr, Ḥudaybiyya — [p. 1575](https://shamela.ws/book/9783/1575), [p. 1597](https://shamela.ws/book/9783/1597), [p. 1638](https://shamela.ws/book/9783/1638), [p. 1653](https://shamela.ws/book/9783/1653)).

The mechanism of **rafʿ** (elevation) is as follows:
1. ʿUrwa **writes** to the caliph a historical account — mursal, without chain
2. Hishām and al-Zuhrī **transmit** the same account with a formalized isnād: Hishām → ʿUrwa → ʿĀʾisha → the Prophet
3. Bukhārī/Muslim **integrate** the formalized version as a marfūʿ hadith

We do not know whether the account of Jibrīl ordering the march toward Qurayẓa figured in ʿUrwa's correspondence in historical form before being "elevated" with an isnād ʿUrwa → ʿĀʾisha by Hishām. But the pattern is documented for other accounts — notably the death of Khadīja, which exists in both mursal (al-Ṭabarānī) and marfūʿ (Bukhārī/Muslim) versions with the same content.

The political stakes are massive: an **angelic order** to march on a people transforms a military event into **divine intervention**. It is an instrument of legitimation whose utility for the Umayyad government — which waged permanent military campaigns — is obvious.

**Content**: this hadith documents only the **order to march** toward Qurayẓa. No information about the siege, the judgment, or the executions.

### Chain 2: Bukhārī 4118 — Jarīr ibn Ḥāzim → Ḥumayd ibn Hilāl → Anas

**Matn**: Anas reports seeing the dust raised in the alley of the Banū Ghanm by Jibrīl's regiment during the march toward Qurayẓa.

**ʿIlla: Jarīr ibn Ḥāzim and ikhtilāṭ.** Jarīr (d. 170 AH) is thiqa in principle, but suffered from **late ikhtilāṭ** (mental confusion). Ibn Mahdī: **اختلط جرير بن حازم**; Abū Ḥātim: **تغير قبل موته بسنة** ("he changed a year before his death"). His sons isolated him to prevent anyone from transmitting from him during his period of confusion. Aḥmad: **في بعض حديثه شيء** ("there is something in some of his hadiths").

**Ḥumayd ibn Hilāl** is thiqa by consensus, but **Ibn Sīrīn** did not approve of him: **كان ابن سيرين لا يرضى حميد بن هلال** — the reason cited is his involvement in **عمل السلطان** (service to the government).

**Content**: purely atmospheric — dust in a street. Probative value for the massacre: **nil**.

### Chain 3: Bukhārī 4119 — Juwayriya → Nāfiʿ → Ibn ʿUmar

**Matn**:

> قال النبي ﷺ يوم الأحزاب : لا يصلين أحد العصر إلا في بني قريظة . فأدرك بعضهم العصر في الطريق ، فقال بعضهم : لا نصلي حتى نأتيها . وقال بعضهم : بل نصلي ، لم يرد منا ذلك . فذكر ذلك للنبي ﷺ **فلم يعنف واحدا منهم**

> "The Prophet said on the day of al-Aḥzāb: 'Let no one pray ʿAṣr except at Banū Qurayẓa.' The prayer time caught up with some on the way. Some said: 'We do not pray until we arrive.' Others: 'We pray — that is not what he meant.' The Prophet was informed, **and he did not blame either group**."

**No ʿilla in rijāl** — the chain Nāfiʿ → Ibn ʿUmar is one of the strongest in the corpus.

**Content**: a fiqh debate about prayer during a march. This hadith does **not concern the massacre** — it documents the march, not the events that followed. Commentators cite it in chapters on ijtihād, not on executions.

### Chain 4: Bukhārī 4028 — Ibn Jurayj → Mūsā ibn ʿUqba → Nāfiʿ → Ibn ʿUmar

**Matn**: a historical summary contrasting the fate of the Banū al-Naḍīr (expelled) and the Banū Qurayẓa: **فقتل رجالهم وقسم نساءهم وأولادهم وأموالهم بين المسلمين** ("he killed their men and distributed their women, children and property among the Muslims").

**ʿIlla: maghāzī nature.** Mūsā ibn ʿUqba (d. 141 AH) is an author of maghāzī — Mālik said of his maghāzī that they were "the soundest." But this hadith is a **narrative summary** of historical events, not a prophetic hadith in the strict sense. Ibn ʿUmar does not say **شهدت** (I witnessed) — he was ~15 years old during the Khandaq, and his presence at the Qurayẓa siege is not established.

**The verb** is **قتل** (qatala, form I) — "he killed" — not **قوتلوا** (qūtilū, form III passive, "they were fought"). But the hadith is placed in the **bāb of Banū al-Naḍīr** (n° 4028), not in that of Qurayẓa — it is al-Bukhārī who classifies it here for the narrative contrast.

### Chain 5: Bukhārī 4121 — Shuʿba → Saʿd ibn Ibrāhīm → Abū Umāma → Abū Saʿīd

This is the **most important** chain — the only hadith that documents Saʿd's judgment.

**ʿIlla 1: Abū Umāma ibn Sahl ibn Ḥunayf.** Al-Bukhārī himself says:

> أدرك النبي ﷺ **ولم يسمع منه**

> "He knew the Prophet's era **but did not hear from him**."

Abū Umāma (Asʿad) was born **around 9 AH** — two years before the Prophet's death. The Qurayẓa event took place in **5 AH**. He was therefore **less than two years old** at the time — if he was even born. Al-ʿIjlī classifies him as **تابعي ثقة** (tābiʿī thiqa), not as a Companion. Ibn Ḥajar (Taqrīb): **معدود في الصحابة ، له رؤية ولم يسمع من النبي** ("counted among the Companions, he saw [the Prophet] but did not hear from him").

**ʿIlla 2: Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī — a child.** Abū Saʿīd was **~13 years old** at Uḥud (3 AH), and the Prophet **sent him back** for being too young:

> عُرضت يوم أحد على النبي وأنا ابن ثلاث عشرة ... فقال : « ردّه » فردّني

> "I was presented to the Prophet on the day of Uḥud, I was 13 years old. [...] He said: 'Send him back.' And he sent me back."

The Khandaq (5 AH) is his **first battle** — he was ~15 years old. He was an adolescent, not a commander or a privileged observer. And **ʿĀʾisha herself** — transmitted through the ʿUrwa clan (al-Ṭabarānī, al-Muʿjam al-Kabīr; al-Ṭaḥāwī) — disqualifies him by name:

> وما علم أبي سعيد الخدري وأنس بن مالك بأحاديث رسول الله ﷺ ؟ وإنما كانا **غلامين صغيرين**

> "What do Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī and Anas ibn Mālik know of the Prophet's hadiths? They were nothing but **two small boys**."

This criticism, transmitted by Hishām ibn ʿUrwa from ʿĀʾisha, is remarkable: the ʿUrwa clan — the same that provides chain 1 — here undermines the witnesses of chains 2 and 5. If ʿĀʾisha considered Abū Saʿīd a غلام صغير incapable of testifying validly, what value has his account of Saʿd's judgment at Qurayẓa?

**Question of the real source**: if Abū Saʿīd was a child during the events, from **whom** does he get his account? He does not say شهدت (I witnessed). He reports a judgment pronounced in a circle of adults — was a 15-year-old child present? And if so, from whom did he hear it before transmitting it? The chain goes back to Abū Saʿīd as "source" — but Abū Saʿīd may be merely a **relay** of an account he heard from others, later.

The crucial term in the judgment is **مقاتلة** (muqātila — combatants), not **رجال** (rijāl — men). The sentence concerns "combatants," not "all pubescent males" — a distinction that Ibn Isḥāq's account erases.

### Chain 6: Abū Dāwud 4404 — ʿAbd al-Malik ibn ʿUmayr → ʿAṭiyya al-Quraẓī

This is the only chain with an **alleged direct witness** (ʿAṭiyya, who claims to be a survivor). It is also the most **problematic** — at every level.

**ʿIlla 1: ʿAbd al-Malik ibn ʿUmayr al-Lakhmī al-Kūfī (d. 136 AH) — accumulation of four defects.**

**Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal**:

> عبد الملك بن عمير **مضطرب الحديث جدا** مع قلة روايته ، ما أُرى له خمسمائة حديث ، وقد **غلط في كثير منها**

> "ʿAbd al-Malik ibn ʿUmayr has **extremely confused hadiths** despite their small number — I see no more than 500 hadiths for him, and he **made mistakes in many of them**."

The four documented defects:
1. **Iḍṭirāb** (confusion) — Aḥmad: مضطرب الحديث جدا
2. **Taghayyur** (memory deterioration) — Abū Ḥātim: تغير حفظه قبل موته; al-Dhahabī: وثقوه وقد تغير بأخرة
3. **Takhliṭ** (mixing of chains/matns) — Ibn Maʿīn: مخلط
4. **Tadlīs** — Ibn Ḥajar classifies him in the **3rd ṭabaqa** of mudallisīn (those whose hadiths are only accepted with *ṣarīḥ al-samāʿ*); Walī al-Dīn al-ʿIrāqī: **مشهور بالتدلیس** ("famous for tadlīs")

He lived **103 years** — an extreme longevity that explains the cognitive deterioration. The modus operandi of his tadlīs is documented: **كان يرسل عن بعض الصحابة ، وبعض هؤلاء قد رآهم ولم يسمع منهم ، فيروي عنهم بالعنعنة مع إسقاط الواسطة** ("he would commit irsāl with some Companions — he had seen them without hearing from them, and he would transmit from them by ʿanʿana while omitting the intermediary"). This is **exactly** the scenario of the ʿAṭiyya hadith: the formula is عن عبد الملك بن عمير **عن** عطية القرظي — ʿanʿana without *ṣarīḥ al-samāʿ*, from a mudallis of the 3rd ṭabaqa. There could be a hidden intermediary.

**ʿIlla 2: ʿAṭiyya al-Quraẓī — a biographical phantom.**

Ibn Ḥajar notes: **ما له إلا هذا الحديث الواحد** ("he has only this single hadith"). But the problem goes well beyond uniqueness. Who is ʿAṭiyya?

Tahdhīb al-Kamāl (al-Mizzī, n° 3962) reveals an extraordinarily thin profile:
- **No father's name** — he appears simply as "ʿAṭiyya al-Quraẓī"
- **لا يعرف نسبه** — "his genealogy is unknown" (Ibn al-Athīr, Asad al-Ghāba)
- **Unknown dates** — no birth or death date is recorded
- **Based in Kūfa** — not in Medina, where survivors of Qurayẓa would have been known

The only "proof" that he is a Qurayẓa survivor is **his own declaration**: "كنت من سبي قريظة." Nobody says "I knew him in Medina as a child of Qurayẓa" or "So-and-so confirms he was indeed from Qurayẓa." This is the same pattern as [[Persons/Muḥammad ibn Kaʿb al-Quraẓī]] — an unknown who appears in Kūfa with the nisba "al-Quraẓī" and whose entire identity rests on his own claim.

**ʿIlla 3: the version of Mujāhid — an anonymous man at the Kufan mosque.**

The comparison with the version of **Mujāhid ibn Jabr** (Abū Nuʿaym, Maʿrifat al-Ṣaḥāba, n° 4956) is devastating. In this version, transmitted through a high-quality Meccan chain (Sufyān ibn ʿUyayna → Ibn Abī Najīḥ → Mujāhid), Mujāhid does **not** say "ʿAṭiyya al-Quraẓī told me." He reports having heard **a man** (رجلاً) who **was speaking in the Kufan mosque**:

> كنت يوم حكم سعد بن معاذ في بني قريظة غلاما

> "I was a young boy on the day of Saʿd ibn Muʿādh's judgment on the Banū Qurayẓa..."

The man is **not identified by name**. This is not a formal transmission (*taḥdīth*) — it is an **accidental testimony** from someone making public declarations in a mosque. Mujāhid does not know him personally, does not validate his claims, and does not name him.

It is ʿAbd al-Malik ibn ʿUmayr — the mudallis — who **identifies** this anonymous man as "ʿAṭiyya al-Quraẓī" and transforms a mosque account into a formal hadith. The version of Mujāhid shows what the original transmission was: a man, in Kūfa, who **claimed** to have been a child during the Qurayẓa affair. Nothing more.

### Synthesis of the ʿilal

| Chain | Probative content | Main ʿilal | Severity |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|---------|
| 1 (ʿĀʾisha) | Order to march only | Hishām: Iraqi irsāl; ʿUrwa: uncontrolled rafʿ (mursal correspondence → marfūʿ hadith); political stakes (angelic order = divine legitimation) | **High** |
| 2 (Anas) | Dust in a street | Jarīr: late ikhtilāṭ; Anas: "غلام صغير" according to ʿĀʾisha | Low (non-probative content) |
| 3 (Ibn ʿUmar) | Debate about prayer | No ʿilla in rijāl | Nil (off-topic content) |
| 4 (Ibn ʿUmar) | Historical summary with قتل | Maghāzī nature, not prophetic hadith | Moderate |
| **5 (Abū Saʿīd)** | **Saʿd's judgment** | **Abū Umāma: born after the event; Abū Saʿīd: ~15-year-old child, "غلام صغير" according to ʿĀʾisha; real source unknown** | **Very high** |
| **6 (ʿAṭiyya)** | **Puberty test** | **ʿAbd al-Malik: 3rd ṭabaqa mudallis + accumulation of 4 defects; ʿAṭiyya: biographical phantom (unknown father, unknown dates, unverifiable nisba); Mujāhid: anonymous man at the Kufan mosque** | **Extreme** |

Chains 1-3 document **the march**, not the executions. Chain 4 is a maghāzī summary. Chain 5 passes through a child disqualified by ʿĀʾisha herself. Chain 6 — the only one with an alleged direct witness — rests on a biographical phantom identified by a notorious mudallis, whose original version (Mujāhid) shows an anonymous man making declarations in a Kufan mosque.

## The detailed account — Ibn Isḥāq

The **bottleneck**. Ibn Isḥāq (d. 150 AH / 767) is the source of the detailed account that everyone knows. He uses a **composite isnād** (*mujtamiʿ*) — an assembly of sources fused into a single narrative:

> حدثني عاصم بن عمر بن قتادة ، وعبد الله بن أبي بكر ، ومحمد بن كعب القرظي ، والزهري ، وعبد الله بن كعب بن مالك ، وغيرهم من أهل العلم ، بعض ذلك عن بعض ، كل قد حدثني بعض هذا الحديث

> "ʿĀṣim ibn ʿUmar ibn Qatāda, ʿAbd Allāh ibn Abī Bakr, Muḥammad ibn Kaʿb al-Quraẓī, al-Zuhrī, ʿAbd Allāh ibn Kaʿb ibn Mālik and others among the scholars told me — some from others — each a part of this account."

His named informants:
1. **Al-Zuhrī** — *mursal* (no Companion cited). Recall that al-Zuhrī's mursals are "like wind" according to Yaḥyā ibn Saʿīd al-Qaṭṭān — see [[Traditions/Assassination of Kaʿb ibn al-Ashraf]]
2. **ʿĀṣim ibn ʿUmar ibn Qatāda** (d. ~120 AH, Medina) — Medinan tābiʿī
3. **ʿAbd Allāh ibn Abī Bakr ibn Ḥazm** (d. 135 AH, Medina) — Medinan tābiʿī
4. **Muḥammad ibn Kaʿb al-Quraẓī** (d. 108-120 AH) — **descendant of the Banū Qurayẓa**
5. **ʿAbd Allāh ibn Kaʿb ibn Mālik**
6. A *mawlā* of the al-Zubayr family
7. "Others among our scholars"

### The problem

The composite isnād is a *talfīq* (assembly): the final account is a **mosaic** whose individual pieces cannot be attributed. Who provided the number 600-900? Who narrated the episode of Ḥuyayy ibn Akhṭab brought in chains? Who transmitted the scene of Rayḥāna? Who described the trenches at the marketplace? **Impossible to determine.** This is the structural blind spot of the composite isnād: traceability is destroyed by the fusion.

This is not a marginal problem — it is the very mechanism by which an account can expand without control. Each informant contributes "a part," and the final assembly is greater than the sum of its parts — without any single piece being individually verifiable.

### Downstream

Ibn Isḥāq's account is transmitted through two main routes:
- **Ibn Hishām** (d. 218 AH) — abridges Ibn Isḥāq (the Sīra that everyone reads)
- **Al-Ṭabarī** (d. 310 AH) — copies integrally via the chain Ibn Ḥumayd → Salama → Ibn Isḥāq ([Tārīkh al-Ṭabarī](https://shamela.ws/book/9783))

The entire subsequent historiographic tradition depends on these two relays — which merely **reproduce** Ibn Isḥāq.

## The detailed account — al-Wāqidī

Al-Wāqidī (d. 207 AH / 823) — **matrūk** according to Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal, al-Bukhārī, al-Nasāʾī, Abū Ḥātim — gives the most developed account and the highest number: **960 men** ([Maghāzī al-Wāqidī](https://shamela.ws/book/23680)).

His informants:
- Muḥammad ibn Kaʿb al-Quraẓī (the **same** as in Ibn Isḥāq)
- ʿAṭiyya al-Quraẓī (survivor — also source of the independent chain in Abū Dāwud)
- Al-Mithwar al-Quraẓī via Thaʿlaba al-Quraẓī
- Other Quraẓī descendants

**Downstream**: **Ibn Saʿd** (d. 230 AH), al-Wāqidī's secretary, copies him integrally in his Ṭabaqāt.

Al-Wāqidī is the only one to give **precise figures** for each operation (number of combatants at Qurayẓa, distribution of booty, etc.) — a suspicious precision for events that occurred 180 years before him, transmitted without verifiable isnād.

### Mālik's criticism

Mālik ibn Anas (d. 179 AH) pronounced a scathing judgment on Ibn Isḥāq, reported through several routes:

> دجال من الدجاجلة... يأتي أبناء اليهود يسائلهم

> "He is a *dajjāl* among impostors... he goes to find the sons of the Jews to question them."

The accusation of seeking information from the "sons of the Jews" (*abnāʾ al-Yahūd*) is exactly what we observe: the detailed informants of Ibn Isḥāq **and** al-Wāqidī on Qurayẓa are **converted descendants of the Banū Qurayẓa themselves**.

## The transmission bottleneck

```
EVENT (5 AH / 627)
    │
    │ ~120 years — no continuous chain
    │
    ├─── IBN ISḤĀQ (d. 150 AH) ◄── composite isnād:
    │        │                      al-Zuhrī (mursal)
    │        │                      + ʿĀṣim ibn ʿUmar ibn Qatāda
    │        │                      + ʿAbd Allāh ibn Abī Bakr
    │        │                      + Muḥammad ibn Kaʿb AL-QURAẒĪ ◄──┐
    │        │                      + "others"                       │
    │        │                                                       │
    │        ├── Ibn Hishām (d. 218 AH) ── abridged                  │
    │        └── al-Ṭabarī (d. 310 AH) ── integral copy              │
    │            (via Ibn Ḥumayd → Salama)                           │
    │                                                                │
    │                                                   COMMON       │
    │                                                  INFORMANT     │
    │                                                                │
    └─── AL-WĀQIDĪ (d. 207 AH, matrūk) ◄── Quraẓī descendants:      │
             │                              Muḥammad ibn Kaʿb ◄──────┘
             │                              + ʿAṭiyya al-Quraẓī
             │                              + al-Mithwar al-Quraẓī
             │
             └── Ibn Saʿd (d. 230 AH) ── integral copy


    ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

    INDEPENDENT CHAINS (Bukhārī / Muslim / Abū Dāwud)

    ├── ʿĀʾisha (Hishām → ʿUrwa) ──────── march toward Qurayẓa, NO NUMBER
    ├── Anas (Ḥumayd ibn Hilāl) ────────── dust, NO NUMBER
    ├── Ibn ʿUmar (Nāfiʿ) ──────────────── "pray at Qurayẓa," NO NUMBER
    ├── Abū Saʿīd (Saʿd ibn Ibrāhīm) ──── Saʿd's judgment, NO NUMBER
    └── ʿAṭiyya (ʿAbd al-Malik, mudallis) ── puberty test, NO NUMBER
```

The contrast is striking. On one side, a detailed account (number, trenches, individual episodes) that passes **entirely** through two disqualified or methodologically problematic transmitters — Ibn Isḥāq (*talfīq* without attribution) and al-Wāqidī (*matrūk*). On the other, five independent chains — themselves fragile once the ʿilal are taken into account — that evoke a military expedition and a judgment but **know nothing** of the mass massacre.

## The number — where does it come from?

The figure of 600-900 appears in **no** independent chain. It comes exclusively from the composite account of Ibn Isḥāq and al-Wāqidī. The central question: which informant provided it? The composite isnād makes the answer impossible — that is precisely its structural flaw.

### The variability of the number

The dispersion of figures across sources is a sign of a narrative in expansion:

| Source | Number given |
|--------|-------------|
| Ibn Isḥāq (via Ibn Hishām) | 600-700 |
| Ibn Isḥāq (via al-Ṭabarī) | 600-700, "some say 800-900" |
| Al-Wāqidī | **960** |
| Independent chains (Bukhārī, Muslim, Abū Dāwud) | **no number** |

The number **increases** over time and transmitters: 600, then 700, then 800-900, then 960 in al-Wāqidī. This is the classic pattern of a narrative in **inflationary expansion** — each relay adds magnitude.

### The maghāzī — a genre without foundations

Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal rules on the genre to which this account belongs (Ibn ʿAdī, al-Kāmil, [1/212](https://shamela.ws/book/12579/136); al-Khaṭīb, al-Jāmiʿ, [n° 1493](https://shamela.ws/book/13012/1723)):

> ثلاثة كتب ليس لها أصول : المغازي والملاحم والتفسير

> "Three types of books **have no foundations**: the maghāzī, the malāḥim and the tafsīr."

The Qurayẓa account is a *maghāzī* text par excellence — and the maghāzī are the genre that the classical critics themselves judge the least reliable. See [[Traditions/Assassination of Kaʿb ibn al-Ashraf]] for another case of the same genre.

## The Quraẓī descendants — a problematic source

**[[Persons/Muḥammad ibn Kaʿb al-Quraẓī]]** (d. 108-120 AH) is the **common informant** of Ibn Isḥāq and al-Wāqidī — the convergence point of the two channels of the detailed account. Who is he?

A man who first appears in **Kūfa**, then settles in Medina, and who bears the nisba "al-Quraẓī" — his only link to the tribe. His father Kaʿb ibn Sulaym has **no preserved riwāya**, no independent attestation. Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal reduces his Companion transmissions to a single one: **Muʿāwiya**. The majority of his Companion isnāds are unavowed mursals — he claims to transmit from al-ʿAbbās (d. 32 AH), Abū Dharr (d. 32 AH), Ibn Masʿūd (d. 32 AH), though he was born around 40 AH.

Unanimously declared **thiqa** — but this unanimity concerns technical reliability, not the veracity of his nisba nor the reality of his meetings with the Companions. See his [[Persons/Muḥammad ibn Kaʿb al-Quraẓī|full entry]].

The fundamental question is brutal: why would the **presumed descendants of an exterminated tribe** be the primary source of the account of their own extermination? And more fundamentally: **how does one verify a nisba** when the father has no trace and the genealogy stops at two generations? The circularity is total: the nisba "al-Quraẓī" presupposes the massacre, and the massacre account rests on the bearers of this nisba.

This is exactly Mālik's reproach: Ibn Isḥāq "goes to find the sons of the Jews." And these sons of the Jews — converts but carriers of their tribe's memory — provide an account that **no one else** transmits with these details.

Al-Wāqidī goes further: he does not content himself with Muḥammad ibn Kaʿb — he adds ʿAṭiyya al-Quraẓī, al-Mithwar al-Quraẓī via Thaʿlaba al-Quraẓī. **All** his detailed informants bear the nisba "al-Quraẓī." The most developed account of the massacre comes exclusively from the massacred tribe — or from those who claim to descend from it.

## What the independent chains establish — and what they do not

After the ʿilal analysis, let us take stock with lucidity. The six independent chains are not equal — and most are far more fragile than the initial table suggested.

### What holds up — with reservations

- A **military expedition** toward the Banū Qurayẓa after the Khandaq (chains 1-3: the march is attested, despite the ʿilal of chain 1)
- A **judgment** pronounced by Saʿd ibn Muʿādh against the **مقاتلة** (combatants) — but reported by a 15-year-old child (Abū Saʿīd) disqualified by ʿĀʾisha herself, via an intermediary (Abū Umāma) who was not born at the time of the events (chain 5)
- The verb **قتل** ("he killed") in a narrative maghāzī summary (chain 4)

### What does not hold up

- The **puberty test** (chain 6) — the only chain meant to document mass executions rests on a biographical phantom (unknown father, unknown dates, unverifiable nisba) identified by a mudallis accumulating four defects. The version of Mujāhid shows that the "source" was an anonymous person making declarations in the Kufan mosque. This is not a hadith — it is unverified hearsay, formalized after the fact.

### What is attested by no independent chain

- The number **600-900** (or 960) — exclusively via Ibn Isḥāq/Wāqidī
- The **trenches** dug at the Medina marketplace
- The episode of **Ḥuyayy ibn Akhṭab** brought in chains
- The episode of **Rayḥāna** bint ʿAmr
- The **woman with the millstone** who throws a stone from the fort
- The exact duration of the siege (25 nights according to al-Wāqidī)
- The details of prior negotiations
- The detailed distribution of booty
- The **systematic character** of the execution (the entire tribe beheaded)

### The verdict

The question is not "600 or fewer?" The question is: **what actually happened?**

The independent chains — once their ʿilal are taken into account — establish that an expedition took place, that a judgment was pronounced, and that combatants were executed. This is a **military event** — a siege followed by reprisals against combatants, a common occurrence in 7th-century Arabia.

The **mass massacre** — 600 to 900 men beheaded in trenches, an entire tribe exterminated — is an account that rests on **no reliable independent chain**. It comes entirely from the Ibn Isḥāq/Wāqidī bottleneck, fed by "Quraẓī descendants" whose identity is unverifiable, in a literary genre (the maghāzī) that Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal declares "without foundations."

The military event is plausible. The genocide is a narrative.
