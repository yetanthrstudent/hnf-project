# Jald shārib al-khamr — the flogging of the wine drinker

Canonical hadith on the punishment of the wine drinker, transmitted through the chain Shuʿba ← [[Persons/Qatāda ibn Di'āma|Qatāda]] ← [[Persons/Anas ibn Mālik|Anas ibn Mālik]]. The versions diverge on the instrument used, the number of lashes and the actors involved — even though the transmitter and the declared source are identical.

## The problem: divergence despite a "clear" hadith

The consumption of alcohol is the subject of a **major divergence** among scholars regarding the applicable earthly sanction: the number of lashes, the instrument, the circumstances. This divergence is difficult to explain if such an explicit prophetic hadith was truly circulating from the first century. The persistence of the disagreement suggests that these traditions **crystallized in the 2nd century**, after the divergence was already established.

The classical scholars evaluated these traditions by focusing on the isnād (chain of transmitters) without considering the **geography of transmission**: where does the hadith come from, in which region did it circulate, and how can one explain that the fuqahāʾ of the 1st century do not agree on such a fundamental question?

## The versions — texts and divergences

### Version 1 — Nasāʾī ([p. 7318](https://shamela.ws/book/8361/7318))

Chain: Muḥammad ibn al-Muthannā ← Muḥammad ibn Jaʿfar ← **Shuʿba**: سمعت قتادة ← Anas

> أُتِيَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ بِرَجُلٍ قَدْ شَرِبَ الْخَمْرَ، **فَجَلَدَهُ بِجَرِيدَتَيْنِ نَحْوًا مِنْ أَرْبَعِينَ** وَفَعَلَهُ أَبُو بَكْرٍ، فَلَمَّا كَانَ عُمَرُ اسْتَشَارَ النَّاسَ، فَقَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ عَوْفٍ: أَخَفُّ الْحُدُودِ ثَمَانُونَ فَأَمَرَ بِهِ عُمَرُ

> "A man who had drunk wine was brought to the Prophet. **He struck him with two palm branches about forty times.** Abū Bakr did the same. When it was ʿUmar's turn, he consulted the people; ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn ʿAwf said: 'The lightest of the ḥudūd: eighty.' ʿUmar ordered it."

Ṣīgha: **سمعت قتادة** — Shuʿba affirms having **heard** Qatāda directly.

### Version 2 — Bukhārī ([p. 10202](https://shamela.ws/book/735/10202))

Double chain: Ḥafṣ ibn ʿUmar ← **Hishām** ← Qatāda ← Anas + Ādam ← **Shuʿba**: حدّثنا قتادة ← Anas

> أَنَّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ **ضَرَبَ فِي الْخَمْرِ بِالْجَرِيدِ وَالنِّعَالِ**، وَجَلَدَ أَبُو بَكْرٍ أَرْبَعِينَ.

> "The Prophet **struck for wine with palm branches and sandals**, and Abū Bakr administered forty lashes."

Differences: addition of **sandals** (niʿāl), disappearance of ʿUmar and the consultation. The number "40" is attributed to Abū Bakr, not to the Prophet.

### Version 3 — Bayhaqī ([p. 19961](https://shamela.ws/book/7861/19961))

Base chain: Ādam ← Shuʿba ← Qatāda ← Anas — same text as the Nasāʾī version.

But al-Bayhaqī notes the variants from **other students of Qatāda**:

**Ibn Abī ʿArūba** ← Qatāda:
> عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ، أَنَّهُ جَلَدَ بِالْجَرِيدِ وَالنِّعَالِ أَرْبَعِينَ

> "The Prophet struck with palm branches and sandals, forty." — Palm branches + sandals, 40 fixed.

**Hammām** ← Qatāda:
> **فَأَمَرَ قَرِيبًا مِنْ عِشْرِينَ رَجُلًا، فَجَلَدَهُ كُلُّ رَجُلٍ جَلْدَتَيْنِ بِالْجَرِيدِ وَالنِّعَالِ أَرْبَعِينَ**

> "**He ordered about twenty men, and each gave him two strikes** with palm branches and sandals — forty."

The same scene is told in three different ways by three direct students of Qatāda. The Prophet strikes himself (Nasāʾī) or orders 20 men to strike (Hammām).

### Version 4 — Bayhaqī 2 ([p. 19959](https://shamela.ws/book/7861/19959))

Chain: Hishām ← Qatāda ← Anas

> أَنَّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ جَلَدَ فِي الْحَدِّ بِالْجَرِيدِ [...] وَضَرَبَ أَبُو بَكْرٍ أَرْبَعِينَ، فَلَمَّا أَنْ وَلِيَ عُمَرُ قَالَ: **إِنَّ النَّاسَ قَدْ دَنَوْا مِنَ الرِّيفِ**، فَمَا تَرَوْنَ فِي حَدِّ الْخَمْرِ؟ فَقَالَ لَهُ عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ عَوْفٍ: نَرَى أَنْ تَجْعَلَهُ كَأَخَفِّ الْحُدُودِ، **فَجَلَدَهُ ثَمَانِينَ**

> "The Prophet struck for the ḥadd with palm branches [...] Abū Bakr administered forty. When ʿUmar came to power, he said: **'People have drawn close to the fertile lands'** — what do you think about the ḥadd for wine? ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn ʿAwf said: Make it the lightest of the ḥudūd. **He administered eighty.**"

Addition of the **rīf** (fertile lands/cultivation zone) motive to justify the aggravation.

## Summary table of divergences

| Student of Qatāda | Instrument | Lashes | Actor | Source |
|------------------|------------|-------|--------|--------|
| **Shuʿba** (via Muḥammad ibn Jaʿfar) | 2 palm branches | ~40 | The Prophet alone | Nasāʾī |
| **Shuʿba** (via Ādam) + **Hishām** | Palms + sandals | 40 (Abū Bakr) | The Prophet | Bukhārī |
| **Ibn Abī ʿArūba** | Palms + sandals | 40 | The Prophet | Bayhaqī |
| **Hammām** | Palms + sandals | 2 x ~20 men = 40 | **~20 men** on order | Bayhaqī |
| **Hishām** | Palms | 40 (Abū Bakr) / 80 (ʿUmar) | Prophet, then caliphs | Bayhaqī 2 |

Four direct students of Qatāda — Shuʿba, Hishām, Ibn Abī ʿArūba, Hammām — produce versions where the **instruments**, the **number of lashes** and the **actors** vary. The text is not controlled at the level of Qatāda.

## The instability of the ṣīgha among Shuʿba's students

Even within the single Shuʿba circuit, the students diverge on the transmission formula (ṣīgha) used by Shuʿba himself:

| Student of Shuʿba | Reported ṣīgha |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Muḥammad ibn Jaʿfar | **سمعت قتادة** (samiʿtu — direct hearing) |
| Ādam | **حدّثنا قتادة** (ḥaddathanā — "he narrated to us") |
| Others | **عن قتادة** (ʿan — "from") |

The difference is crucial: "samiʿtu" excludes tadlīs, while "ʿan" is the ambiguous formula that allows it. Yet it is not Qatāda who changes formula — it is **Shuʿba's students** who report differently what they heard. The instability propagates at every link.

## Ibn ʿAdī's discovery: al-Ḥasan in the chain

### Shabbāba ibn Sawwār — a reliable transmitter

Al-Kāmil by Ibn ʿAdī ([p. 2194](https://shamela.ws/book/12579/2194)) reports the assessment of **Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn**:

> قُلتُ ليحيى بن مَعِين فشبابة في شُعْبَة، قال **ثقة**. وسألت يَحْيى عن شاذان؟ فقال: لاَ بأس به. قلت هو أَحَب إليك أُمّ شبابة؟ قال **شبابة أحب إلي**.

> "I said to Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn: And Shabbāba [transmitting] from Shuʿba? He said: **Reliable.** I asked Yaḥyā about Shadhdhān? He said: No problem with him. I said: Is he dearer to you or Shabbāba? He said: **Shabbāba is dearer to me.**"

Shabbāba is very reliable. Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal abandoned him **only for his irjāʾ** (theological doctrine), not for a transmission problem. Ibn ʿAdī concludes:

> وشبابة عندي إنما ذمه الناس للإرجاء الذي كان فيه **وأما في الحديث فإنه لا بأس به**

> "Shabbāba, in my view, was only blamed for the irjāʾ he professed. **As for hadith, there is no problem with him.**"

### The crucial ziyāda: Qatāda ← **al-Ḥasan** ← Anas

Yet on the next page ([p. 2195](https://shamela.ws/book/12579/2195)), Ibn ʿAdī reports Shabbāba's version:

> شبابة، حَدَّثَنا شُعْبَة، عَن قَتادَة **عَنِ الْحَسَنِ**، عَن أَنَس أَنَّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ أُتِيَ بِرَجُلٍ قَدْ شَرِبَ الْخَمْرَ فَضَرَبَهُ بِجَرِيدَتَيْنِ نَحْوًا مِنْ أَرْبَعِينَ

> "Shabbāba ← Shuʿba ← Qatāda ← **al-Ḥasan** ← Anas: A man who had drunk wine was brought to the Prophet, and he struck him with two palm branches about forty times."

Ibn ʿAdī comments:

> **وَلَمْ يَزِدْ فِي الإِسْنَادِ الْحَسَنَ، عَن شَبَابَةَ** [...] رَوَاهُ أَصْحَابُ شُعْبَة عَنْ شُعْبَة، عَن قَتادَة عَنْ أنس.

> "**No one added al-Ḥasan in the isnād except Shabbāba** [...] The other companions of Shuʿba transmit it from Shuʿba ← Qatāda ← Anas [without al-Ḥasan]."

### The implication

Shabbāba — a very reliable transmitter, preferred to Shadhdhān by Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn — adds **al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī** as an intermediary between Qatāda and Anas. All other students of Shuʿba omit this link.

Two possible readings:

1. **Shabbāba is right** — al-Ḥasan is indeed the real intermediary. In that case, Qatāda commits **tadlīs** in all other versions by suppressing al-Ḥasan to give the impression of direct contact with Anas. This is exactly the tadlīs described by al-Ḥākim: « وكذلك قتادة بن دعامة [...] إذا قال : قال أنس أو قال الحسن وهو **مشهور بالتدليس عنهما** » ([p. 19](https://shamela.ws/book/5997/19)). And the chain al-Ḥasan → Anas is considered unreliable.

2. **Shabbāba is mistaken** — he added al-Ḥasan through a memory error. Ibn ʿAdī suggests this possibility: "ولعل حدث به حفظا" (perhaps he transmitted it from memory). But even in this reading, the fact that such a reliable transmitter **naturally associates** al-Ḥasan with this chain suggests that the Qatāda-al-Ḥasan-Anas association was known for this hadith.

In both cases, the chain "Qatāda ← Anas" — presented as direct contact in the Ṣaḥīḥayn — is weakened.

## Diagnosis: qaṣṣ, not hadith

The profile of these variations is that of an **oral narrative reconstructed differently at each session** — a qaṣṣ — and not a tradition memorized with precision and transmitted verbatim. Qatāda, who was a recognized [[Concepts/Quṣṣāṣ|qāṣṣ]] ("كان قتادة يقص", Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal), told in his gatherings narratives mixing prophetic traditions, Companion accounts and moral exhortations. His students then cut up this narrative flow into individual hadiths with formal isnāds.

The result: a single episode (the punishment of the wine drinker) exists in multiple incompatible versions, all attached to Qatāda ← Anas, because **the source is not a transmitted hadith but a variable oral narrative**.

## What the divergence of the fuqahāʾ reveals

If a clear prophetic hadith on the ḥadd al-khamr was circulating from the first century, the fuqahāʾ of that era would not have diverged so much on the question. Yet they diverge — on the number of lashes, on the instrument, on whether it is even a fixed ḥadd or a taʿzīr (discretionary sanction). This ancient divergence is **prior** to the hadiths that claim to resolve it. The traditions were produced to justify already existing positions, not the other way around.

The same phenomenon is observed for other questions (wearing gold, for example): explicit hadiths appear in the 2nd century on subjects where the scholars of the 1st century had no known prophetic tradition.
