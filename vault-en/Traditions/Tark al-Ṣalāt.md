# Hadith of tark al-ṣalāt — "between man and shirk/kufr: abandoning prayer"

## The text

> « بين الرجل وبين الشرك والكفر ترك الصلاة »
>
> "Between man and polytheism/disbelief lies the abandonment of prayer."

Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (n° 82). Transmitted by [[Persons/Jābir ibn 'Abd Allāh\|Jābir ibn 'Abd Allāh]].

## The chains in Muslim

Muslim transmits this hadith through **two chains** ([pp. 268-269](https://shamela.ws/book/711/268)):

| # | Chain | Formula | Transmitter from Jābir |
|---|--------|---------|----------------------|
| 1 | Al-A'mash ← **Abū Sufyān** (Ṭalḥa ibn Nāfi') ← Jābir | 'an | **Ṣaḥīfa** transmitter |
| 2 | **Ibn Jurayj** ← "akhbaranī" **Abū al-Zubayr** ← Jābir | akhbaranī | **Ṣaḥīfa + tadlīs** transmitter |

### Chain 1: Al-A'mash ← Abū Sufyān

**Abū Sufyān Ṭalḥa ibn Nāfi'** — Shu'ba: « حديث أبي سفيان عن جابر إنما هي صحيفة » (his hadiths from Jābir are nothing but a ṣaḥīfa). Al-Bazzār: he heard nothing from Jābir, about 100 hadiths, all from a known ṣaḥīfa. He heard from Jābir only **4 hadiths** (Abū Khālid al-Dalānī). And he "attributes too much" (yusnidu kathīran) — he elevates to the Prophet what is of uncertain status.

[[Persons/Al-Aʿmash|Al-A'mash]] himself is a **mudallis** of the 2nd/3rd category. And he transmits here with ʿanʿana.

### Chain 2: Ibn Jurayj ← Abū al-Zubayr

**Ibn Jurayj** uses "akhbaranī" — which is supposed to guarantee direct hearing according to Aḥmad's rule. But **Ibn Ḥibbān** states that all the munkar traditions in the nuskha of Ibn Jurayj via Abū al-Zubayr pass through **Yāsīn al-Zayyāt** (kadhdhāb):

> « وكل ما وقع في نسخة ابن جريج عن أبي الزبير من المناكير كان ذلك مما سمعه ابن جريج عن ياسين الزيات عن أبي الزبير، فدلس عنه »
>
> "**Everything among the munkar** in the nuskha of Ibn Jurayj from Abū al-Zubayr comes from what Ibn Jurayj **heard from Yāsīn al-Zayyāt** from Abū al-Zubayr, and **he concealed it** (dallasa 'anhu)."

**[[Persons/Abū al-Zubayr al-Makkī|Abū al-Zubayr]]** himself mixed samāʿ and ṣaḥīfa — admission to al-Layth: « منه ما سمعت ومنه ما حدثت عنه » — "Part, I heard from him; part, it was reported to me from him."

The actual circuit of chain 2:

```
Jābir ibn 'Abd Allāh
        │
   Abū al-Zubayr (samāʿ? or ṣaḥīfa?)
        │
   YĀSĪN AL-ZAYYĀT (kadhdhāb — CONCEALED)
        │
   IBN JURAYJ (tadlīs: "Abū al-Zubayr reported to me")
        │
   Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim
```

## The third route: Ḥammād ibn Zayd — the iḍṭirāb from al-Dāraquṭnī

Al-Dāraquṭnī ('Ilal, [p. 397](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/397)) documents a major **iḍṭirāb** in the chain **[[Persons/Ḥammād ibn Zayd\|Ḥammād ibn Zayd]] ← 'Amr ibn Dīnār ← Jābir**:

| Version | Transmitter from Ḥammād | Declared chain | Status |
|---------|----------------------|-----------------|--------|
| 1 | Abū al-Rabī' al-Zahrānī | Ḥammād ← 'Amr ← Jābir ← the Prophet | **Marfū'** |
| 2 | Al-Qawārīrī | Ḥammād: "'Amr told me, **or one of my companions from 'Amr**" ← Jābir | **Mawqūf** |
| 3 | Aḥmad ibn Ibrāhīm al-Mawṣilī | Ḥammād: "'Amr told me, **or it reached me from him**" ← Jābir | **Marfū'** (doubt about the channel) |
| 4 | Ibn Ḥasāb | Ḥammād: "I heard 'Amr, **or it was reported to me from him**" ← Jābir | **Mawqūf** |

> يرويه حماد بن زيد، واختُلف عنه؛ فرواه أبو الربيع الزهراني، عن حماد بن زيد، عن عمرو بن دينار، عن جابر، عن النبي صلى الله عليه وسلم. وخالفه القواريري، رواه عن حماد، قال: حدثني عمرو، أو بعض أصحابي، عن عمرو، عن جابر، موقوفا
>
> "Ḥammād ibn Zayd transmits it, and **there is disagreement about him**. Abū al-Rabī' al-Zahrānī transmitted it from Ḥammād ibn Zayd, from 'Amr ibn Dīnār, from Jābir, from the Prophet. Al-Qawārīrī contradicted him: he transmitted it from Ḥammād who said: ''Amr told me — **or one of my companions, from 'Amr** — from Jābir', **mawqūf** (stopped at the Companion)."

### The quadruple instability

1. **Ḥammād is unsure about his own source**: did he hear directly from 'Amr, or from an intermediary?
2. **The status oscillates** between marfūʿ and mawqūf depending on who transmits from Ḥammād
3. **'Amr ibn Dīnār himself** did not hear from Jābir (admission to Shu'ba: « أنت سمعته من جابر ؟ قال : لا »)
4. **The Jābir in question** could be Jābir ibn Zayd, not Jābir ibn 'Abd Allāh (correction by Sufyān ibn 'Uyayna)

The scrupulous profile of [[Persons/Ḥammād ibn Zayd\|Ḥammād ibn Zayd]] — « يقصر في الأسانيد ويوقف المرفوع ويشك كثيرا بتوقفه » ("he would shorten isnāds, stop at the Companion what was attributed to the Prophet, and often hesitated by stopping" — Yaʿqūb ibn Shayba) — makes his hesitation consistent: he doubts because he genuinely does not know where he got this hadith.

## The verdict of al-Shāfiʿī — Al-Umm

Al-Shāfi'ī (Al-Umm, [p. 277](https://shamela.ws/book/1655/277)) uses this hadith to formulate the verdict:

**The tārk al-ṣalāt** (the one who abandons prayer) is **killed** if he does not repent — not as a kāfir, but as a Muslim condemned for a capital failing. Al-Shāfiʿī distinguishes between abandonment as kufr (which he rejects) and abandonment as a major obligation deserving the death penalty.

## Convergence: the ṣaḥīfa as single source

The three routes of the hadith lead to the same document:

```
                    ṢAḤĪFA OF AL-YASHKURĪ
                           │
        ┌──────────────────┼──────────────────┐
        │                  │                  │
   ABŪ SUFYĀN        ABŪ AL-ZUBAYR      'AMR IBN DĪNĀR
   (ṣaḥīfa only)    (partial samāʿ)    (no samāʿ)
        │                  │                  │
   AL-A'MASH          YĀSĪN → IBN JURAYJ  ḤAMMĀD IBN ZAYD
   (mudallis)          (kadhdhāb + tadlīs) (iḍṭirāb)
        │                  │                  │
   ┌────┴────┐        MUSLIM n° 82      DĀRAQUṬNĪ 'ILAL
   MUSLIM n° 82                        (4 contradictory versions)
```

The "plurality of routes" (taʿaddud al-ṭuruq) is a **mirage**: all roads lead to the **same ṣaḥīfa**. Three apparently independent transmitters draw from the same written document — and all three channels exhibit documented pathologies (unsorted ṣaḥīfa, tadlīs, iḍṭirāb).
