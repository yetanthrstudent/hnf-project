# Tafarrud (تفرد) — isolation in transmission

When a **single transmitter** reports a hadith from a master who had **numerous students**, it is a warning signal: if the master had actually taught this text, others would have transmitted it too. The problem worsens when the isolated transmitter belongs to a **different region** than the master — he claims to have received a text that nobody on location knows.

|---|---|---|---|---|
| **[[Persons/Al-Awzā'ī\|Al-Awzā'ī]]** (Syrian) | [[Persons/Nāfiʿ mawlā Ibn ʿUmar\|Nāfiʿ]] (Medinan) | [[Traditions/Kharijites - Coran - gosier\|Kharijite hadith]] — "people will come forth, reciting the Quran without it passing their throats" | Entirely Damascene chain (Hishām ← Yaḥyā ← al-Awzā'ī). No Medinan knows it — neither Mālik nor any other student of Nāfiʿ. | [Ibn Mājah no. 174](https://shamela.ws/book/1198/212) |
| **[[Persons/Sulaymān ibn Mūsā\|Sulaymān ibn Mūsā]]** (Syrian) | al-Zuhrī (Medinan) | Hadith of nikāḥ without walī — "every marriage without a guardian is invalid" | A Syrian alone reporting from al-Zuhrī, who had dozens of students in the Ḥijāz. | **[[Persons/Khālid ibn Ma'dān\|Khālid ibn Ma'dān]]** (Syrian) | 70 Companions (self-declared) | Self-legitimation — claims to have met 70 Companions | Transmitted exclusively by his family circle: his daughter 'Abda and his servant Umm al-Ḍaḥḥāk. ## The systemic pattern

In all three cases, a **Syrian** is the only one to report from a master **outside Shām**:

```
Nāfiʿ (Medina, dozens of students)          al-Zuhrī (Medina, dozens of students)
        │                                            │
        └── sole: AL-AWZĀʿĪ (Damascus) ──┐    └── sole: SULAYMĀN (Damascus) ──┐
                                         │                                  │
                                         └──── TAFARRUD ◄──────────────────┘
                                                   ▲
Khālid ibn Ma'dān (Homs)                          │
        └── "70 Companions" ── sole witness: himself (family circle) ──┘
```

Tafarrud functions as an **import mechanism**: a scholar from Shām claims a teaching that no local scholar of the master knows, thereby creating an authority that nobody on site can confirm or deny.

## Basran case: the hadith of the two festivals

| Isolated transmitter | Claimed master | Hadith | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **[[Persons/Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl\|Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl]]** (Basran) | [[Persons/Anas ibn Mālik\|Anas ibn Mālik]] | Hadith of the two festivals — "God has replaced them for you with al-Aḍḥā and al-Fiṭr" | [Awham al-Muḥaddithīn p. 4223](https://shamela.ws/book/1329/4223) |

Ḥumayd is the **sole transmitter** of this hadith in the entire patrimony — complete tafarrud. The case is aggravated by the fact that Ḥumayd is a proven mudallis: Shu'ba states that he actually heard only **five hadiths** from Anas (al-'Uqaylī, [p. 770](https://shamela.ws/book/13041/770)), and Ḥammād ibn Salama confirms that the majority of his traditions "from Anas" actually come from [[Persons/Thābit al-Bunānī|Thābit al-Bunānī]].

## Basran case: the divine thumb (Tirmidhī 3074)

| Isolated transmitter | Claimed master | Hadith | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **[[Persons/Ḥammād ibn Salama\|Ḥammād ibn Salama]]** (Basran) | multiple chain | Hadith of the divine thumb — the Jew describes the heavens on one finger, the earths on another finger, etc. | [Tirmidhī p. 5285](https://shamela.ws/book/7895/5285) |

Ḥammād is the **sole reliable transmitter** of this anthropomorphist hadith. The tafarrud is aggravated by the fact that Ḥammād's books are suspected of having been **infiltrated by his stepson Ibn Abī al-ʿAwjāʾ**, a zindīq executed under al-Mahdī (Mīzān, [p. 593](https://shamela.ws/book/1692/593)). This hadith of ṣifāt (corporeal divine attributes) corresponds exactly to the type of material a saboteur would have inserted.

## Related case: the prohibition of carnivores

An even more explicit case: the [[Traditions/Interdiction des carnassiers|prohibition of carnivore meat]] is transmitted exclusively by **Umayyad functionaries** from Shām (Abū Idrīs al-Khawlānī, Khālid ibn Ma'dān, Muslim ibn Mishkam, Maymūn ibn Mahrān). Al-Zuhrī himself acknowledges having **never heard** this prohibition in the Ḥijāz ([Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 1932](https://shamela.ws/book/711/5956)). This is not tafarrud in the strict sense (several Syrians transmit it), but the pattern is the same: content **locally unknown** propagated from Shām toward the Ḥijāz by agents of Umayyad power.

## Ighrāb — the radical cousin of tafarrud

Tafarrud is an **isolated point** in a chain — a Syrian alone reporting from a Medinan master. **[[Concepts/Ighrāb|Ighrāb]]** is the cousin phenomenon, but **on the scale of an entire corpus**: a transmitter returns from a distant land (al-Andalus, Khurāsān) with material that nobody in the East can verify.

The paradigmatic case is **[[Persons/Muʿāwiya ibn Ṣāliḥ]]** — a Ḥimṣī who became qāḍī of al-Andalus, returning around 154H with ~200 unique hadiths (*ifrādāt*). If tafarrud makes verification **difficult**, ighrāb makes it **impossible**: there were no hadith collectors in al-Andalus to provide *mutābaʿāt*.
