# Falsification of nusakh — when books are corrupted

Hadith criticism rests on the assumption that the **books** (nusakh, kutub) of transmitters are intact. Yet two major cases document the **direct corruption of written collections** by third parties — not copying errors, but **deliberate interpolations**.

## Case 1: Al-Layth ibn Saʿd — victim of [[Persons/Khālid al-Madāʾinī|Khālid al-Madāʾinī]]

### The forger

**Khālid ibn al-Qāsim al-Madāʾinī** — declared kadhdhāb by Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn, matrūk by al-Nasāʾī.

### The method

Khālid took al-Layth's **mursal isnāds** (chains where the Companion or intermediate tābiʿī is not named) and transformed them into **musnad** by inserting the names of reliable Medinan transmitters:

| Original (mursal) | Falsified (musnad) |
|---|---|
| al-Layth ← ? ← the Prophet | al-Layth ← Nāfiʿ ← Ibn ʿUmar ← the Prophet |
| al-Layth ← ? ← ʿĀʾisha | al-Layth ← ʿUrwa ← ʿĀʾisha |

The falsification is invisible to the later reader: the inserted names (Sālim, ʿUrwa, Nāfiʿ) are legitimate transmitters that al-Layth actually frequented. The forger exploits the **plausibility** of the network.

### The irony

Al-Layth is the **sole transmitter** to have verified the samāʿ of [[Persons/Abū al-Zubayr al-Makkī|Abū al-Zubayr al-Makkī]] from [[Persons/Jābir ibn ʿAbd Allāh|Jābir ibn ʿAbd Allāh]]: "mark for me what you heard." This sorting is the unique guarantee of the Abū al-Zubayr → Jābir corpus. Yet the books of al-Layth — which contain this sorting — were themselves **corrupted** by Khālid.

## Case 2: ʿAbd al-Razzāq al-Ṣanʿānī — double corruption

> See the dedicated page: [[Concepts/Muṣannaf of ʿAbd al-Razzāq]] — which adds a **third layer** of corruption: the external forger Muḥammad ibn ʿUkāsha al-Kirmānī.

### 2a. The nephew of Maʿmar

**Maʿmar ibn Rāshid** (d. 153 AH, Ṣanʿāʾ) is the principal master of ʿAbd al-Razzāq. His nephew (ibn akh Maʿmar), described as **rāfiḍī** (extreme Shiite), **inserted faḍāʾil ʿAlī** (merits of ʿAlī) into the books of ʿAbd al-Razzāq — traditions that did not exist in the original transmission of Maʿmar.

The mechanism:
1. ʿAbd al-Razzāq possesses the books of Maʿmar
2. The nephew of Maʿmar, having access to the same corpus, **inserts** pro-ʿAlī traditions
3. ʿAbd al-Razzāq transmits everything as coming from Maʿmar
4. Critics detect "faḍāʾil ʿAlī" unknown in his corpus

### 2b. Talqīn after blindness

ʿAbd al-Razzāq became **blind** at the end of his life. After his blindness, people would come and **prompt him with hadiths** (talqīn) that he had never transmitted, and he would accept them without verification, being unable to consult his books.

Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal makes the distinction: what he heard from ʿAbd al-Razzāq **before the blindness** (in Ṣanʿāʾ, in 197 AH) is reliable; what was transmitted **after** is suspect.

| Period | Status | Reason |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Before blindness | **Reliable** — Aḥmad and others heard him directly | Books intact, active memory |
| After blindness | **Suspect** — talqīn | Can no longer verify his own books |

### The combination

Both problems compound: the books of ʿAbd al-Razzāq potentially contain (a) interpolations from the nephew of Maʿmar and (b) post-blindness additions through talqīn. It is impossible, for a later reader, to distinguish the three layers (original, interpolated, prompted).

## Systemic consequence

The falsification of nusakh strikes at the **most fundamental level** of transmission: the book itself. Classical hadith criticism (jarḥ wa taʿdīl) evaluates **transmitters** — it assumes the books are intact. When an external forger modifies the content of a book attributed to a thiqa, the entire verification system is short-circuited:

- The transmitter is declared thiqa → the book is accepted
- But the book has been **altered** after the transmitter
- The result is a falsified tradition circulating under the authority of a thiqa

This is the **blind spot** of the system: criticism evaluates men, not manuscripts.
