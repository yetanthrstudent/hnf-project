# Al-Ziyāda fī al-kutub — when books are corrupted from within

The [[Concepts/Falsification of nusakh|falsification of nusakh]] documents **individual cases** of corruption: [[Persons/Khālid al-Madāʾinī|Khālid al-Madāʾinī]] inserts names into al-Layth's books, the nephew of Maʿmar interpolates the Muṣannaf of ʿAbd al-Razzāq. These are named, dated, identified affairs.

But these cases are not isolated accidents. The sources reveal a **systemic awareness**: 2nd-century scholars knew that the corruption of books was a **societal phenomenon** — common enough to justify radical precautionary protocols. A transmitter is not rejected because he lies, but because **someone else might modify his books without his knowledge**.

---

## I. Systemic awareness — five converging voices

### 1. Mālik ibn Anas (d. 179 AH, Medina) — "I fear additions during the night"

Two versions in al-Kifāya:

**Version 1** — al-Kifāya ([p. 820](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/820)), via Ashhab:

> قُلْتُ لِمَالِكٍ , الرَّجُلُ يُخْرِجُ كِتَابَهُ وَهُوَ ثِقَةٌ فَيَقُولُ هَذَا سَمَاعِي إِلَّا أَنَّهُ لَا يَحْفَظُ قَالَ لَا يُسْمَعُ مِنْهُ قَالَ يُونُسُ **لِأَنَّهُ إِنْ أُدْخِلَ عَلَيْهِ لَا يَعْرِفُ**
>
> "I said to Mālik: a man produces his book, he is thiqa, and says: 'this is my hearing,' but he does not memorize. He said: one does not listen to him. Yūnus said: **because if something is inserted, he will not recognize it.**"

**Version 2** — al-Kifāya ([p. 821](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/821)), via Ashhab / ʿAbd Allāh ibn ʿAbd al-Ḥakam:

> وَسُئِلَ مَالِكٌ أَيُؤْخَذُ مِمَّنْ لَا يَحْفَظُ وَهُوَ ثِقَةٌ صَحِيحٌ أَتُؤْخَذُ عَنْهُ الْأَحَادِيثُ فَقَالَ لَا يُؤْخَذُ عَنْهُ **أَخَافُ أَنْ يُزَادَ فِي كُتُبِهِ بِاللَّيْلِ**
>
> "Mālik was asked: does one take hadiths from a thiqa ṣaḥīḥ who does not memorize? He said: one does not take from him. **I fear that additions are made to his books during the night.**"

The phrase **بالليل** (during the night) implies the addition is done **secretly** — by a student, a copyist, a neighbor, anyone with physical access to the collection. Mālik describes an environment in which this practice is **plausible** enough to justify rejecting an otherwise irreproachable transmitter.

The diffusion of this statement in uṣūl treatises is massive — al-Qāḍī ʿIyāḍ (al-Ilmāʿ [p. 134](https://shamela.ws/book/6030/134)), Ibn Rajab (Sharḥ ʿIlal al-Tirmidhī [p. 453](https://shamela.ws/book/9552/453)), al-Zarkashī (Tashnīf al-masāmiʿ [p. 1460](https://shamela.ws/book/122142/1460)), Zakariyyā al-Anṣārī (Ghāyat al-wuṣūl [p. 139](https://shamela.ws/book/9112/139)) — all reproduce it.

### 2. Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal (d. 241 AH, Baghdad) — the blind man and the illiterate

Al-Kifāya ([p. 824](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/824)), via ʿAbd Allāh ibn Aḥmad:

> سَأَلْتُ أَبِي قُلْتُ مَا تَقُولُ فِي سَمَاعِ الضَّرِيرِ الْبَصَرِ قَالَ إِذَا كَانَ يَحْفَظُ مِنَ الْمُحَدِّثِ فَلَا بَأْسَ وَإِذَا لَمْ يَكُنْ يَحْفَظُ فَلَا , قَالَ أَبِي قَدْ كَانَ أَبُو مُعَاوِيَةَ الضَّرِيرُ إِذَا حَدَّثَنَا بِالشَّيْءِ الَّذِي نَرَى أَنَّهُ لَمْ يَحْفَظْهُ، يَقُولُ فِي كِتَابِنَا أَوْ فِي كِتَابِي عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ الشَّيْبَانِيِّ وَلَا يَقُولُ: ثَنَا وَلَا سَمِعْتُ. قُلْتُ: وَالْأُمِّيُّ؟ قَالَ هُوَ كَذَلِكَ بِهَذِهِ الْمَثَابَةِ إِلَّا مَا حَفِظَ مِنَ الْمُحَدِّثِ
>
> "I asked my father: what do you say about the hearing of a blind man? He said: if he memorizes from the muḥaddith, no problem; otherwise, no. My father said: when Abū Muʿāwiya the Blind reported something we could see he had not memorized, he would say: **'in our book'** or **'in my book'** — and he would not say 'he told me' or 'I heard.' I asked: and the illiterate? He said: it is the same."

Abū Muʿāwiya al-Ḍarīr (d. 195 AH), Muḥammad ibn Khāzim, blind, specialist of [[Persons/Al-Aʿmash|al-Aʿmash]], had developed a **protocol of distinction**:

| Formula | Meaning | Guarantee |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| **ثنا** / **سمعتُ** | Personal memorization | Strong — memory of hearing |
| **في كتابنا** / **في كتابي** | Found in his writings | Weak — the book may have been altered |

His honesty is at the same time an **admission**: a blind transmitter cannot verify whether someone has added to the content of his manuscripts.

### 3. Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn (d. 233 AH, Baghdad) — categorical rejection

Al-Kifāya ([p. 825](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/825)):

> سَأَلْتُ يَحْيَى بْنَ مَعِينٍ [...] رَجُلٌ ضَرِيرُ الْبَصَرِ [...] أَحَادِيثُ لَا يَحْفَظُهَا قَالَ لَا تَكْتُبْ إِلَّا مَا يَحْفَظُ [...] فَقُلْتُ إِنْ أَخَذْتُهُ مِنْ رَجُلٍ ثِقَةٍ ثُمَّ أَسْأَلُهُ , فَقَالَ: **لَيْسَ بِشَيْءٍ**
>
> "I asked Yaḥyā: a blind man who has hadiths he does not memorize? — Write only what he memorizes. — Even if I take it from a reliable man then ask him? — **It is worthless.**"

And on talqīn ([p. 826](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/826)):

> قِيلَ لِيَحْيَى بْنِ مَعِينٍ الرَّجُلُ يُلَقَّنُ حَدِيثَهُ؟ قَالَ: **إِذَا كَانَ يَعْرِفُ إِنْ أُدْخِلَ عَلَيْهِ** فَلَيْسَ بِحَدِيثِهِ بَأْسٌ وَإِنْ لَمْ يَكُنْ يَعْرِفُ إِذَا أُدْخِلَ عَلَيْهِ , فَكَأَنَّ يَحْيَى كَرِهَهُ
>
> "Yaḥyā was asked: what about a man who is prompted (yulaqqan) with his hadith? He said: **if he recognizes when something is inserted** (udkhila ʿalayhi), no problem; otherwise, Yaḥyā disapproved."

The term **أُدخل عليه** ("one inserts into him") recurs as a technical motif: it is the very name of the phenomenon.

### 4. Hushaym (d. 183 AH, Baghdad) — contempt

Al-Kifāya ([p. 823](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/823)):

> مَنْ لَمْ يَحْفَظِ الْحَدِيثَ فَلَيْسَ هُوَ مِنْ أَصْحَابِ الْحَدِيثِ **يَجِيءُ أَحَدُهُمْ بِكِتَابٍ كَأَنَّهُ سِجِلُّ مَكَاتِبٍ**
>
> "Whoever does not memorize hadith is not a man of hadith. One of them shows up with a book **like a slave's manumission contract**."

### 5. Al-Khaṭīb al-Baghdādī — the doctrinal synthesis

Al-Khaṭīb unifies the cases (Mālik, the blind man, the illiterate) under a single **ʿilla** (legal cause) — al-Kifāya ([p. 826](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/826)):

> وَنَرَى الْعِلَّةَ الَّتِي لِأَجْلِهَا مَنَعُوا صِحَّةَ السَّمَاعِ مِنَ الضَّرِيرِ وَالْبَصِيرِ الْأُمِّيِّ هِيَ **جَوَازُ الْإِدْخَالِ عَلَيْهِمَا مَا لَيْسَ مِنْ سَمَاعِهِمَا** وَهِيَ الْعِلَّةُ الَّتِي ذَكَرَهَا مَالِكٌ فِيمَنْ لَهُ كُتِبَ وَسَمَاعُهُ فِيهَا صَحِيحٌ غَيْرَ أَنَّهُ لَا يَحْفَظُ
>
> "The cause (ʿilla) for which they prohibited the hearing from the blind man and the sighted illiterate is the **possibility of inserting what does not belong to their hearing** — and it is the same cause invoked by Mālik for the one who has books and valid samāʿ but does not memorize."

## II. Documented cases — beyond the "nusakh"

The [[Concepts/Falsification of nusakh|falsification of nusakh]] covers al-Layth and ʿAbd al-Razzāq (see also [[Concepts/Muṣannaf of ʿAbd al-Razzāq]] for the three layers of corruption of the Muṣannaf). But the sources name **at least four other victims**:

### 1. [[Persons/Ḥammād ibn Salama]] — the zindīq stepson

Mīzān al-Iʿtidāl ([p. 593](https://shamela.ws/book/1692/593)) — Ibn al-Thaljī:

> ابن أبي العوجاء ربيب حماد بن سلمة، **فكان يدس في كتبه**
>
> "Ibn Abī al-ʿAwjāʾ was the stepson of Ḥammād ibn Salama, and he **inserted into his books** (yuduss)."

Ibn Abī al-ʿAwjāʾ: zindīq executed under al-Mahdī. Domestic proximity suffices — no complicity or negligence needed. The hadiths of ṣifāt (divine anthropomorphism) that proliferate in Ḥammād's corpus correspond to the type of material a satirical heretic would insert to discredit Islam from within. See the full entry: [[Persons/Ḥammād ibn Salama]].

The muṣṭalaḥ sources regularly cite the case: al-Tabrīzī (al-Kāfī [p. 179](https://shamela.ws/book/789/179)), al-Suyūṭī (al-Baḥr [p. 664](https://shamela.ws/book/7729/664)), al-Zarkashī (al-Nukat [p. 256](https://shamela.ws/book/5978/256)), al-Biqāʿī (al-Nukat al-wafiyya [p. 148](https://shamela.ws/book/23798/148)).

### 2. Sufyān ibn Wakīʿ — the treacherous warrāq

Al-Irshād of al-Khalīlī ([p. 501](https://shamela.ws/book/12993/501)):

> ضعَّفوه وكان له وَرَّاق **أدخل في حديثه** ما ليس له فقال له الكوفيون: **ويحك أفسدتَ شيخنا وابن شيخنا**
>
> "They weakened him. He had a **warrāq** (copyist) who **inserted into his hadith** what did not belong to it. The Kufians told him: 'Woe to you, you have corrupted our shaykh and the son of our shaykh!'"

The professional copyist — the very person supposed to **preserve** the text — is the agent of its corruption.

### 3. Ibn Lahīʿa — the transmitter who no longer controls anything

Two levels of documented corruption:

**a) Insertion into books** — al-Manār al-munīf of Ibn al-Qayyim ([p. 99](https://shamela.ws/book/9388/99)):

> والله تعالى أعلم بما **دُسَّ في كُتب** ابن لهيعة وإلا فهو أعلم بالحديث من أن يروج عليه مثل هذا الهذيان
>
> "God knows what was **slipped into the books** of Ibn Lahīʿa — for he was too learned in hadith for such nonsense to pass through him."

Ibn al-Qayyim acknowledges the phenomenon while defending the person: the problem is not the transmitter, but his **books**.

**b) Blind acceptance of foreign books** — Muqaddima of Ibn al-Ṣalāḥ ([p. 206](https://shamela.ws/book/22870/206)):

> ذُكِرَ عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ حَسَّانَ: أَنَّهُ رَأَى قَوْمًا مَعَهُمْ جُزْءٌ سَمِعُوهُ مِنِ ابْنِ لَهِيعَةَ فَنَظَرَ فِيهِ فَإِذَا لَيْسَ فِيهِ حَدِيثٌ وَاحِدٌ مِنْ حَدِيثِ ابْنِ لَهِيعَةَ، فَجَاءَ إِلَى ابْنِ لَهِيعَةَ، فَأَخْبَرَهُ بِذَلِكَ، فَقَالَ: **مَا أَصْنَعُ؟ يَجِيئُونِي بِكِتَابٍ، فَيَقُولُونَ: هَذَا مِنْ حَدِيثِكَ، فَأُحَدِّثُهُمْ بِهِ**
>
> "Yaḥyā ibn Ḥassān saw people with a juzʾ they had 'heard' from Ibn Lahīʿa. He examined it: **not a single hadith belonged to Ibn Lahīʿa**. He went and told him. Ibn Lahīʿa replied: **'What can I do? They come to me with a book and say: this is your hadith. So I transmit it to them.'**"

This testimony is devastating: a transmitter agrees to transmit an **entire** book that contains nothing of his, simply because it is attributed to him. This is no longer external falsification — it is the **collapse of control** by the transmitter himself.

Ibn al-Ṣalāḥ generalizes ([p. 207](https://shamela.ws/book/22870/207)): "The student comes with a juzʾ or a book and says: 'this is your transmission.' The shaykh lets him read without verifying."

### 4. Al-Mughīra ibn Saʿīd — infiltration of the Shiite corpus

Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq himself denounces the operation (via al-Ṭūsī, [p. 669](https://shamela.ws/book/12763/669)):

> إن المغيرة بن سعيد لعنه الله **دسَّ في كتب** أصحاب أبي أحاديث لم يحدث بها **فاتقوا الله ولا تقبلوا ما خالف قول ربنا وسنة نبينا**
>
> "Al-Mughīra ibn Saʿīd — may God curse him — **slipped into the books** of my father's [al-Bāqir] companions hadiths he never said. Fear God and do not accept what contradicts the word of your Lord and the Sunna of your Prophet."

The phenomenon crosses confessional boundaries: the Shiite corpus and the Sunni corpus suffer the same mechanism.

### 5. Ghunaym ibn Sālim — the forged nuskha, stolen and reworked

Tadhkirat al-Ḥuffāẓ of Ibn al-Qaysarānī ([p. 226](https://shamela.ws/book/1578/226)), abridgment of the Kitāb al-Majrūḥīn of Ibn Ḥibbān:

> ورواه غنيم بن سالم، عن أنس [...] وغنيم هذا **له نسخة موضوعة عن أنس**، ولعل الرازي **سرقه منه، وغير الألفاظ، وزاد ونقص**، والله أعلم
>
> "Ghunaym ibn Sālim transmitted it from Anas [...] This Ghunaym possessed a **nuskha mawḍūʿa** (forged copy) attributed to Anas. It is likely that al-Rāzī [Yaḥyā ibn al-ʿAlāʾ] **stole** [this hadith] from him, then **altered the wording, added and removed**."

Yaḥyā ibn al-ʿAlāʾ al-Rāzī — described as "isolating himself (yanfarid) from the thiqāt with things suspected of being his own deliberate work" and heavily charged by Wakīʿ — does not need to fabricate *ex nihilo*. He draws from an already existing **forged nuskha**, reworks the text, and redistributes it under a new isnād (via Ṣafwān ibn Sulaym → ʿAṭāʾ ibn Yasār → Ibn ʿAbbās). The **access vector** here is the material support itself: Ghunaym's forged copy becomes a **reservoir** from which others draw to create new versions.

### Summary table

| Victim | Forger | Method | Access | Source |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Al-Layth ibn Saʿd | [[Persons/Khālid al-Madāʾinī\|Khālid al-Madāʾinī]] | mursal → musnad | Copyist/transmitter | Mīzān |

| [[Persons/Ḥammād ibn Salama\|Ḥammād ibn Salama]] | Ibn Abī al-ʿAwjāʾ (zindīq) | hadiths of ṣifāt | Family (stepson) | Mīzān [p. 593](https://shamela.ws/book/1692/593) |
| Sufyān ibn Wakīʿ | His warrāq | insertion into hadith | Professional (copyist) | al-Irshād [p. 501](https://shamela.ws/book/12993/501) |

The **access vector** is striking: two family cases (stepson, nephew), one professional (copyist), one ideological (sectarian). The book is not protected by a vault — it circulates in the household, passes through the hands of a copyist, is accessible to a student. Physical proximity suffices.

## III. Protection protocols — admission through precaution

Al-Kifāya, chapters 836-860, documents the protocols scholars developed to counter this risk. Their very existence proves the scale of the phenomenon.

### The book must never leave one's hands

Al-Khaṭīb ([p. 853](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/853)):

> وَيَجِبُ عَلَى صَاحِبِ الْكِتَابِ أَنْ **يَحْتَفِظَ بِكِتَابِهِ** [...] فَإِنْ خَرَجَ عَنْ يَدِهِ وَعَادَ إِلَيْهِ فَقَدْ **تَوَقَّفَ بَعْضُ الْعُلَمَاءِ عَنْ جَوَازِ الْحَدِيثِ مِنْهُ**
>
> "The owner of the book **must keep his book**. If it leaves his hands and returns, **some scholars prohibited transmitting from it.**"

### Refusal to lend the manuscript

ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Mahdī — al-Kifāya ([p. 854](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/854)):

> يَا صَبِيُّ **أَنَا أَدْفَعُ إِلَيْكَ كِتَابِي**
>
> "Boy, you think I am going to **give you my book**?" — he only allowed copying while sitting next to the person.

### Unlocalized doubt invalidates the entire book

Haytham ibn Jamīl — al-Kifāya ([p. 853](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/853)):

> سَمِعْتُ مِنَ شُعْبَةَ سَبْعَمِائَةِ حَدِيثٍ، **شَكَكْتُ فِي وَاحِدٍ مِنْهَا تَرَكْتُهَا كُلَّهَا**
>
> "I heard from [[Persons/Shuʿba ibn al-Ḥajjāj|Shuʿba]] 700 hadiths. I doubted **one** — I abandoned **all 700**."

ʿAlī ibn al-Ḥasan al-Shaqīqī — al-Kifāya ([p. 851](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/851)):

> نَهِقَ حِمَارٌ يَوْمًا فَخَفِيَ عَلَيَّ حَدِيثٌ أَوْ بَعْضُ حَدِيثٍ ثُمَّ نَسِيتُ أَيَّ حَدِيثٍ كَانَ مِنَ الْكِتَابِ **فَتَرَكْتُ الْكِتَابَ كُلَّهُ**
>
> "A donkey brayed one day and a hadith — or part of one — escaped me. Then I forgot which one it was in the book. **I abandoned the entire book.**"

Al-Khaṭīb formalizes the rule ([p. 850](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/850)): if the doubt concerns an identified hadith, that one alone is rejected; if the doubt is **unlocalized**, **the entire book is invalid**.

### A book absent too long is abandoned

Al-Anṣārī — al-Kifāya ([p. 856](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/856)):

> سَمِعَ مَعِي إِنْسَانٌ فَأَخَذَهُ لِيَنْسَخَ **وَطَالَتْ غَيْبَتُهُ عَنِّي فَتَرَكْتُهُ وَلَمْ أَرَوِهِ**
>
> "Someone took my book to copy it. **His absence dragged on** — I abandoned [the book] and did not transmit it."

### Al-Khaṭīb's rule on returned books

Al-Kifāya ([p. 857](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/857)):

> وَالَّذِي عِنْدِي فِي هَذَا أَنَّهُ مَتَى غَابَ كِتَابُهُ عَنْهُ ثُمَّ عَادَ إِلَيْهِ **وَلَمْ يَرَ فِيهِ أَثَرَ تَغْيِيرٍ حَادِثٍ مِنْ زِيَادَةٍ أَوْ نُقْصَانٍ أَوْ تَبْدِيلٍ** وَسَكَنَتْ نَفْسُهُ إِلَى سَلَامَتِهِ جَازَ لَهُ أَنْ يَرْوِيَ مِنْهُ
>
> "If the book returns and one sees **no trace of alteration — neither addition (زيادة), nor deletion (نقصان), nor substitution (تبديل)** — and the transmitter is confident in its integrity, he may transmit from it."

Al-Khaṭīb **explicitly** names the three types of falsification as real risks.

### No knife in hearing sessions

Muqaddima of Ibn al-Ṣalāḥ ([p. 193](https://shamela.ws/book/22870/193)):

> كَانَ الشُّيُوخُ يَكْرَهُونَ حُضُورَ السِّكِّينِ مَجْلِسَ السَّمَاعِ حَتَّى لَا يُبْشَرَ شَيْءٌ [...] **الْحَكُّ تُهْمَةٌ**
>
> "The shaykhs forbade bringing a **knife** to hearing sessions, so that nothing could be scraped [from the parchment]. **Scraping is an accusation.**"

Scraping (ḥakk) parchment allows erasing a word and writing another — this is **substitution** (tabdīl) at the character level. The prohibition of knives in sessions shows the risk is not theoretical.

### "When knowledge entered into books..."

[[Persons/Al-Awzāʿī|Al-Awzāʿī]] — Muqaddima of Ibn al-Ṣalāḥ ([p. 179](https://shamela.ws/book/22870/179)):

> كَانَ هَذَا الْعِلْمُ كَرِيمًا يَتَلَقَّاهُ الرِّجَالُ بَيْنَهُمْ، **فَلَمَّا دَخَلَ فِي الْكُتُبِ دَخَلَ فِيهِ غَيْرُ أَهْلِهِ**
>
> "This knowledge was noble, transmitted between men. **When it entered into books, the unqualified entered into it.**"

Al-Awzāʿī makes the fundamental diagnosis: the transition from oral to written is not progress — it is an **opening of vulnerability**. The oral required physical presence and memory; the written opens the corpus to anyone with access to the manuscript.

## IV. When the transmitter no longer recognizes his own book

The phenomenon is not limited to external fraud. The sources document cases where the transmitter himself **no longer recognizes** the content of his own books:

Shuʿba — al-Kifāya ([p. 847](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/847)):

> وَجَدْتُ مُذْ ثَلَاثَةِ أَيَّامٍ فِي كِتَابٍ عِنْدِي عَنْ مَنْصُورٍ عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ ... **مَا أَدْرِي كَيْفَ كَتَبْتُهُ وَلَا أَذْكُرُ أَنِّي سَمِعْتُهُ**
>
> "Three days ago I found in one of my books a hadith from Manṣūr, from Mujāhid... **I do not know how I wrote it and I do not remember hearing it.**"

ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Mahdī — al-Kifāya ([p. 842-843](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/842)):

> إِنَّ الرُّقْعَةَ لَتَقَعُ فِي يَدِي مِنْ حَدِيثِي كَأَنِّي لَمْ أَسْمَعْهَا، **وَلَوْلَا أَنَّهَا بِخَطِّي** مِنْ حَدِيثِي **مَا حَدَّثْتُ بِهَا**
>
> "A slip falls into my hands, from my own hadiths, as if I had never heard it. **If it were not in my own handwriting**, I would not have transmitted it."

And Ibn Mahdī at [p. 845](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/845):

> وَجَدْتُ فِي كُتُبِي بِخَطِّ يَدِي عَنْ شُعْبَةَ مَا لَمْ أَعْرِفْهُ **فَطَرَحْتُهُ**
>
> "I found in my books, in my own handwriting, hadiths from Shuʿba that I did not recognize. **I threw them away.**"

The only protection is the **recognition of one's own handwriting** — and even that sometimes fails. When the transmitter no longer recognizes his own content, he is in the same situation as the victim of external insertion: he does not know what is authentic.

## V. The material support — three complementary cases

The preceding cases document the **insertion** into others' books. But the vulnerability of the material support is not limited to idkhāl. Three cases drawn from the classical sources illustrate different mechanisms: **faithful copying** that preserves errors, the **material document** of uncertain provenance, and **editorial insertion** that extends the phenomenon into the age of print.

### 1. Abū Mūsā and Ghundar's book — faithful copying and its consequences

ʿIlal al-Dāraquṭnī ([p. 1283](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/1283#p5)):

> قال أبو موسى: **هكذا نسخته من كتاب محمد بن جعفر غندر** أن رسول الله مرت به جنازة فقام
>
> "Abū Mūsā said: **this is how I copied it from the book of Muḥammad ibn Jaʿfar Ghundar** — that the Prophet saw a funeral procession pass and stood up."

The hadith is transmitted via Shuʿba → Jābir → al-Shaʿbī → "Abū Saʿīd ibn Zayd." The problem: the kunya **Abū Saʿīd** before "ibn Zayd" is an aberrant formulation. Aḥmad transmits the same hadith simply as "Saʿīd ibn Zayd," without the kunya. Al-Dāraquṭnī rules: « الأشبه بالصواب قول أحمد » (the most correct is Aḥmad's version).

The case illustrates the paradox of **faithful copying**: Abū Mūsā scrupulously copies Ghundar's manuscript, including the anomaly. His honesty (« هكذا نسخته ») guarantees only **fidelity to the support** — not fidelity to the original hadith. The manuscript is a filter: it freezes its author's errors, and the copy perpetuates them.

### 2. ʿAddāʾ ibn Khālid ibn Hawza — the material kitāb

Al-Sunan al-Kubrā of al-Nasāʾī, ḥadīth 11688 ([p. 16135](https://shamela.ws/book/8361/16135#p1)):

> قال لي العداء بن خالد: **ألا أقرئك كتابا كتبه لي رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم؟** قال: قلت: بلى. **فأخرج لي كتابا** «هذا ما اشترى العداء بن خالد بن هوذة من محمد صلى الله عليه وسلم اشترى منه عبدا - أو أمة - لا داء ولا غائلة ولا خبثة، بيع المسلم المسلم»
>
> "Al-ʿAddāʾ told me: Shall I **read you a document the Prophet wrote for me**? I said: yes. **He produced a document**: 'This is what al-ʿAddāʾ ibn Khālid ibn Hawza purchased from Muḥammad: a slave (or slave girl), without disease, hidden defect, or vice — a sale of Muslim to Muslim.'"

The case is singular: this is not an orally transmitted hadith, but a **material object** that al-ʿAddāʾ physically exhibits (« فأخرج لي كتابا »). The content is a sales contract — a legal act, not a prophetic utterance. The chain passes through ʿAbbād (ṣāḥib al-karābīs) and ʿAbd al-Majīd: who saw this document in al-ʿAddāʾ's hands, and how can its material authenticity be verified after the holder's death? The **support** here is the only proof — and it escapes all control of the oral chain.

### 3. Editorial insertion in the Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī

Sunan al-Tirmidhī, ed. Bashshār ʿAwwād Maʿrūf, note on ḥadīth no. 2300 ([p. 4040](https://shamela.ws/book/7895/4040)):

The ḥadīth of Khuraym ibn Fātik on false testimony (شهادة الزور) was **removed** from the main text by Bashshār, on the grounds that it **does not belong to the Jāmiʿ**:

- Al-Mizzī does not mention it in Tuḥfat al-Ashrāf
- Al-Tabrīzī (Mishkāt al-Maṣābīḥ) attributes it to Abū Dāwūd and Ibn Māja — not al-Tirmidhī
- Al-Suyūṭī (al-Durr al-Manthūr) does not mention al-Tirmidhī either
- The hadith is **absent from the manuscripts** consulted

> هذا الحديث ليس من جامع الترمذي قطعا، إذ لم يذكره المزي في تحفة الأشراف، وإنما أضافه محققه [...] فأخطأ في ذلك
>
> "This hadith **absolutely** does not belong to the Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī. Al-Mizzī does not mention it in Tuḥfat al-Ashrāf. It was his [previous] editor who **added** it — and he was wrong."

A modern editor **inserted** a hadith into the printed text of a canonical collection. The mechanism is identical to classical idkhāl — a third party introduces foreign material into a corpus that does not belong to him — but the **support** has changed: from manuscript to print. Bashshār's correction shows that criticism of the material support remains necessary, including for modern editions.

## VI. The counter-argument — "our books are more reliable than we are"

Some scholars defended the opposite position. ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dāwūd — al-Kifāya ([p. 839](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/839)):

> **لِأَنَّ كُتُبَنَا أَحْفَظُ مِنَّا**
>
> "**Our books are more reliable than our memory.**"

And Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn against Abū Ḥanīfa ([p. 836](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/836)):

> كَانَ أَبُو حَنِيفَةَ يَقُولُ لَا تُحَدِّثُ إِلَّا بِمَا تَعْرِفُ وَتَحْفَظُ [...] وَأَمَّا نَحْنُ فَنَقُولُ إِنَّهُ يُحَدِّثُ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ يَجِدُهُ فِي كِتَابِهِ بِخَطِّهِ عَرَفَهُ أَوْ لَمْ يَعْرِفْهُ
>
> "Abū Ḥanīfa used to say: transmit only what you know and memorize. As for us, we say: he transmits everything he finds in his book, in his own handwriting, whether he recognizes it or not."

Both positions coexist. But the three qualities of Marwān ibn Muḥammad ([p. 830](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/830)) summarize the compromise:

> لَا غِنًى لِصَاحِبِ حَدِيثٍ عَنْ ثَلَاثٍ , **صِدْقٍ وَحِفْظٍ وَصِحَّةِ كَتْبٍ** فَإِنْ كَانَتْ فِيهِ ثِنْتَانِ وَأَخْطَأَتْهُ وَاحِدَةٌ لَمْ يَضُرَّهُ
>
> "Three things are indispensable for the muḥaddith: **sincerity, memory, and integrity of the books**. If he has two out of three, it is not serious."

The **integrity of the books** is elevated to the rank of a formal condition — at the same level as memory and sincerity. This is not a logistical detail: it is a **pillar of the system**.

## VII. The societal phenomenon — synthesis

```
FIVE VOICES, THREE GENERATIONS, THREE CITIES

 Mālik (d. 179 AH, Medina)        « يُزاد في كتبه بالليل »
       │
 Hushaym (d. 183 AH, Baghdad)      « كأنه سجل مكاتب »
       │
 Aḥmad (d. 241 AH, Baghdad)        « إذا أُدخل عليه لا يعرف »
       │
 Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn (d. 233 AH)     « إذا كان يعرف إن أُدخل عليه »
       │
 Al-Khaṭīb (d. 463 AH, Baghdad)   « جواز الإدخال عليهما »
       │
       └── SYNTHESIS: a single ʿilla (cause) = al-idkhāl
```

Seven documented victims, five voices that denounce, protection protocols that are so many admissions. This is not a transmission accident — it is a **structural phenomenon** recognized by the system itself.

The blind spot remains the same as the one identified in the [[Concepts/Falsification of nusakh|falsification of nusakh]]: classical hadith criticism evaluates **men** (al-jarḥ wa al-taʿdīl), not **manuscripts**. When an external forger — stepson, copyist, nephew, infiltrator — modifies the content of a book attributed to a thiqa, the verification system is short-circuited. The transmitter is declared reliable; but the book no longer is.
