# "Kafaytukum tadlīs thalātha" — كفيتكم تدليس ثلاثة

## The principle

[[Shu'ba ibn al-Ḥajjāj]] (d. 160 H), nicknamed "amīr al-mu'minīn fī l-ḥadīth", declares:

> **كفيتكم تدليس ثلاثة: الأعمش، وأبي إسحاق، وقتادة**

> "I have spared you the tadlīs of three: **[[Persons/Al-Aʿmash|al-Aʿmash]]**, **Abū Isḥāq [al-Sabī'ī]**, and **[[Persons/Qatāda ibn Di'āma|Qatāda]]**."

Chain (declared ṣaḥīḥ): al-Bayhaqī ← Ibrāhīm ibn Abī Ṭālib ← Rajā' al-Ḥāfiẓ ← **al-Naḍr ibn Shumayl** ← Shu'ba.

Al-Naḍr ibn Shumayl is the most prolific of Shu'ba's transmitters.

**Doctrinal consequence**: when Shu'ba transmits from one of these three, even with the 'an'ana (the particle "عن" / "from"), the narration is accepted as **muttaṣil** (connected). Shu'ba is supposed to have transmitted from them only what he had verified they had actually heard.

Shu'ba also expressed his hatred of tadlīs:

> « لأن أزني أحب إلي من أن أدلس » — "I would rather commit adultery than practise tadlīs."

> « لأن أقع من السماء إلى الأرض، أحب إلي من أن أدلس » — "I would rather fall from the sky than practise tadlīs."

(Siyar a'lām al-nubalā', entry on Shu'ba)

## Shu'ba's methodology: "haddathanā" vs "haddatha"

### The detection principle

Shu'ba observed Qatāda's **introductory formulas** to distinguish what he had actually heard from what he had not heard:

**al-'Ilal of Aḥmad** (no. 5068, [p. 1390](https://shamela.ws/book/2331/1390)):

> « قال شعبة: **كنت أتفطن إلى فم قتادة** إذا حدث. فإذا حدث بما قد سمع قال: **حدثنا** سعيد بن المسيب، و**حدثنا** أنس، و**حدثنا** الحسن، و**حدثنا** مطرف. وإذا حدث ما لم يسمع قال: **حدث** سليمان بن يسار، و**حدث** أبو قلابة. »

> "Shu'ba said: **I used to watch Qatāda's mouth closely** when he transmitted. When he reported what he had heard, he would say: 'Sa'īd ibn al-Musayyab **told us**' (ḥaddatha**nā**), 'Anas **told us**', 'al-Ḥasan **told us**', 'Muṭarrif **told us**'. But when he reported what he had not heard, he would say: 'Sulaymān ibn Yasār **narrated**' (ḥaddatha), 'Abū Qilāba **narrated**'."

Variant via Ibn Sa'd (al-'Ilal, [p. 370](https://shamela.ws/book/794/370)):

> « قال شعبة: **كنت أعرف حديث قتادة ما سمع مما لم يسمع**. إذا قال: حدثنا أنس، وحدثنا الحسن، وحدثنا سعيد، وحدثنا مطرف. وإذا جاء ما لم يسمع كان يقول: قال سعيد بن جبير، وقال أبو قلابة. »

### The marker

| Qatāda's formula | Meaning according to Shu'ba |
|--------------------|---------------------------|
| **حدّثنا** (ḥaddathanā) — "he told us" | **Heard** — direct transmission |
| **حدّث** (ḥaddatha) — "he narrated" | **Not heard** — indirect transmission (tadlīs) |
| **قال** (qāla) — "he said" | **Not heard** — same category |

## Application: Qatāda and Abū al-'Āliya

### What Shu'ba asserts

Tirmidhī ([p. 318](https://shamela.ws/book/7895/318)), via 'Alī ibn al-Madīnī ← Yaḥyā ibn Sa'īd al-Qaṭṭān ← Shu'ba:

> « **لم يسمع قتادة من أبي العالية إلا ثلاثة أشياء** :
> 1. حديث عمر أن النبي ﷺ نهى عن الصلاة بعد العصر حتى تغرب الشمس، وبعد الصبح حتى تطلع الشمس
> 2. حديث ابن عباس عن النبي ﷺ قال: لا ينبغي لأحد أن يقول أنا خير من يونس بن متى
> 3. حديث علي: القضاة ثلاثة »

> "Qatāda only heard from Abū al-'Āliya **three things**:
> 1. The hadith of Omar: the Prophet forbade praying after the 'aṣr until sunset, and after the ṣubḥ until sunrise.
> 2. The hadith of Ibn 'Abbās: 'It is not fitting for anyone to say: I am better than Yūnus ibn Mattā.'
> 3. The hadith of 'Alī: 'The judges are of three types.'"

### The decisive counter-example: the Isrā' hadith in the Ṣaḥīḥayn

This hadith of the Isrā' (night journey — description of Moses, of 'Īsā, of Mālik the guardian of Hell, of the Dajjāl) is transmitted through the chain **Qatāda ← Abū al-'Āliya ← Ibn 'Abbās**. It is **not listed** among the three things Shu'ba says Qatāda heard from Abū al-'Āliya.

Yet this hadith is **muttafaq 'alayh** (unanimously recognised as authentic):

| Collection | No. | Chain |
|---------|------|--------|
| Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī | 3239, 3396 | via Shu'ba ← Qatāda ← Abū al-'Āliya ← Ibn 'Abbās |
| Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim | 165 | via Shu'ba ← Qatāda ("**سمعت أبا العالية**") ← Ibn 'Abbās |
| Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim | 165 (2nd chain) | via **Shaybān ibn 'Abd al-Raḥmān** ← Qatāda ← Abū al-'Āliya |
| Musnad Aḥmad | 2197 | via Shaybān ← Qatāda (declared ṣaḥīḥ 'alā sharṭ al-shaykhayn) |

In Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, **through Shu'ba's own transmission**, Qatāda says **"سمعت أبا العالية"** ("I heard Abū al-'Āliya") — the strongest samāʿ formula. Shu'ba thus transmits himself a **4th hadith** where Qatāda claims samāʿ from Abū al-'Āliya, contradicting his own tally of "3 things only".

In al-Īmān of Ibn Mandah (no. 722, [p. 834](https://shamela.ws/book/13005/834)), the same hadith is transmitted via Shaybān with the form **"ḥaddatha"** (3rd person = not heard). The same hadith thus carries two opposite markers depending on the transmitter: **"sami'tu"** in Shu'ba's version, **"ḥaddatha"** in Shaybān's — a sign of instability in Qatāda's wording.

## Application: Abū Isḥāq al-Sabī'ī and al-Barā'

Shu'ba applies the same method with Abū Isḥāq al-Sabī'ī — the second of the three mudallis he "protects".

### The hadith of sitting in the street

Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim ([p. 1501-1502](https://shamela.ws/book/181/1501)): Shu'ba transmits from Abū Isḥāq ← al-Barā' ibn 'Āzib. Then Shu'ba asks Abū Isḥāq:

> "Did you hear this hadith from al-Barā' ibn 'Āzib?" — **No.**

### The hadith on Muslims who died before the prohibition of alcohol

Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim ([p. 1503](https://shamela.ws/book/181/1503)): same pattern. Shu'ba ← Abū Isḥāq ← al-Barā'. Shu'ba checks:

> "Did you hear this from al-Barā'?" — **No.**

In both cases, Abū Isḥāq says "al-Barā' said" (يقول البراء) when he did not hear it from him — this is the tadlīs that Shu'ba detects and documents. **Despite this, Imam Muslim retains both hadiths in his collection.**

---

## The flaws in the system

1. **Detection without prevention**: the two examples with Abū Isḥāq show that Shu'ba **himself transmits** tadlīs, detects it after the fact, and the hadiths circulate in the Ṣaḥīḥ regardless. The "protector" documents the problem without solving it.

2. **The fragility of the marker**: the method rests on a **single phoneme** (the suffix "-nā") observed by ear in real time. A mudallis aware of Shu'ba's presence could adapt his wording.

3. **Deliberate deception**: [[Persons/Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl]] admits to having concealed his sources from Shu'ba (فألبسته عليه — al-'Uqaylī, [p. 772](https://shamela.ws/book/13041/772)). The method rests on the good faith of the one being monitored — which is precisely what a mudallis does not offer by definition.

4. **The internal contradiction**: Shu'ba asserts "3 things only" for Qatāda/Abū al-'Āliya — but **himself transmits** in Muslim (no. 165) a 4th hadith (the Isrā') where Qatāda declares "سمعت أبا العالية". His own corpus contradicts his own tally.

5. **The instability of the marker**: the same Isrā' hadith bears "سمعت" (direct samāʿ) in Shu'ba's version (Muslim 165) and "حدّث" (not heard) in Shaybān's version (Ibn Mandah 722). The linguistic marker varies according to the transmitter — which undermines the method that takes it as a criterion.
