# Silsilat al-Dhahab — the golden chain

## The formula

**Al-Bukhārī** pronounces the canonical formula (Tahdhīb al-Kamāl, [p. 15875](https://shamela.ws/book/3722/15875)):

> « أصح الأسانيد: مالك عن نافع عن ابن عمر »
>
> "The most authentic of chains: **Mālik, from [[Persons/Nāfiʿ mawlā Ibn ʿUmar|Nāfiʿ]], from Ibn ʿUmar**."

Three links, three imams. Ibn ʿUmar is a Companion, son of the caliph ʿUmar. Nāfiʿ is his freed slave, who attended him for decades. Mālik is the master of Medina. The chain seems unassailable.

## The origin of the term

The term **silsilat al-dhahab** itself does **not** come from al-Bukhārī. It is **al-Ḥāzimī** (Abū Bakr, d. 584 H) who compiled a volume on what Aḥmad transmits from al-Shāfiʿī and entitled it "Silsilat al-dhahab" — for the extended chain: **Aḥmad → al-Shāfiʿī → Mālik → Nāfiʿ → Ibn ʿUmar** (Sharḥ al-Mūqiẓa, [p. 122](https://shamela.ws/book/36195/122); Nukat of Ibn Ḥajar, [p. 240](https://shamela.ws/book/8316/240)).

Al-Bukhārī also uses a competing formula for another chain: **Abū al-Zinād → al-Aʿraj → Abū Hurayra** which he calls "dībāj khusrawānī" (royal brocade) — which relativizes the exclusivity of the silsilat al-dhahab.

## The three flaws

### 1. Nāfiʿ — the untouchable but cracked link

[[Persons/Nāfiʿ mawlā Ibn ʿUmar|Nāfiʿ]] is unanimously thiqa. Al-Khalīlī says: "no error is known of him." But al-Dāraquṭnī documents a case where his immediate source changes **five times** for the same hadith — the hadith of the jāriya (ʿIlal no. 2975, [pp. 4900-4902](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/4900)):

| Version | Source of Nāfiʿ |
|---------|-----------------|
| Via Abū Ḥanīfa | **Ibn ʿUmar** |
| Bukhārī no. 2304 | **A son of Kaʿb** (unnamed) |
| Muwaṭṭaʾ | **A man of the Anṣār** (anonymous) |
| ʿAbd al-Razzāq no. 8829 | **A man of the Banū Salima** |
| ʿAbd al-Razzāq no. 8819 | **Sulaymān ibn Yasār** |
| ʿAbd al-Razzāq no. 8818 | **(nobody)** — mursal |

The students who report these contradictory versions are themselves thiqāt (Mālik, ʿUbaydullāh, Ibn ʿUyayna, Ayyūb). Five reliable transmitters cannot have each invented a different source. The problem is **Nāfiʿ himself**.

See the detailed entry: [[Persons/Nāfiʿ mawlā Ibn ʿUmar]]

### 2. Mālik — not the best transmitter of Nāfiʿ

The choice of Mālik as the summit of the chain is **partisan**, not consensual. The Iraqi critics prefer ʿUbaydullāh:

- **Aḥmad**: ʿUbaydullāh is the most solid and most ḥāfiẓ in Nāfiʿ
- **Aḥmad ibn Ṣāliḥ**: "ʿUbaydullāh is **dearer to me than Mālik** in the hadith of Nāfiʿ"
- **Yaḥyā al-Qaṭṭān**: **became angry** when Ibn Mahdī said that Mālik was more reliable than ʿUbaydullāh in Nāfiʿ
- The Mālikites themselves contest the addition of al-Shāfiʿī at the top of the chain

If the "best transmitter of Nāfiʿ" is not Mālik for Aḥmad and Yaḥyā al-Qaṭṭān, the silsilat al-dhahab is only golden in one camp.

### 3. The Kaʿb circuit — the material circulating in the chain

In the three hadiths where [[Persons/Nāfiʿ mawlā Ibn ʿUmar|Nāfiʿ]] and Sālim (Ibn ʿUmar's son) diverge, Nāfiʿ **lowers** the attribution systematically while Sālim **elevates** it to the Prophet. The most telling case:

- **Sālim**: hadith "takhruju nār min qibal al-Yaman" — from the **Prophet** (marfūʿ)
- **Nāfiʿ**: the same hadith — from **[[Ka'b al-Ahbar|Kaʿb al-Aḥbār]]** (maqṭūʿ)
- **Verdict of the critics**: Nāfiʿ is correct

The muḥaddithūn therefore **implicitly** acknowledge that Kaʿb's material circulates under the Prophet's name in the most prestigious chains. They acknowledge it for this specific hadith — but never raise the question for the others.

This is the same phenomenon documented in the chain [[Persons/Al-Aʿmash|al-Aʿmash]] ← [[Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān|Abū Ṣāliḥ]] ← [[Abū Hurayra]] (Nuskhat Wakīʿ, hadith no. 21: shakk Abū Hurayra/Kaʿb), and denounced by Ibn Rajab (Fatḥ al-Bārī 3/410): "if one listed the hadiths elevated to the Prophet that are actually from Kaʿb, **the list would be long**."

## The circularity

The system rests on a **validation loop**:
1. Al-Bukhārī declares that Mālik → Nāfiʿ → Ibn ʿUmar is the most authentic chain
1. The guarantee of this chain rests on Nāfiʿ's reliability
2. Nāfiʿ's reliability rests on the trust Mālik places in him
3. Mālik's trust in Nāfiʿ legitimizes the chain

No **empirical** examination of the content validates the chain from the outside. The iḍṭirāb documented by al-Dāraquṭnī (six versions, five different sources for the same hadith) is proof that this circularity produces false positives.

## Relations

- **Links**: [[Persons/Nāfiʿ mawlā Ibn ʿUmar]] (the transmitter), Mālik (the validator), Ibn ʿUmar (the Companion)
- **Competing chain**: Abū al-Zinād → al-Aʿraj → [[Abū Hurayra]] — "dībāj khusrawānī" (al-Bukhārī)
- **Same problem**: [[Persons/Al-Aʿmash]] ← [[Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān]] ← [[Abū Hurayra]] — the most frequent chain of the Ṣaḥīḥayn, vulnerable through tadlīs and the ṣaḥīfa
- **Kaʿb network**: [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar]] — Kaʿb's material elevated to the Prophet in both chains
