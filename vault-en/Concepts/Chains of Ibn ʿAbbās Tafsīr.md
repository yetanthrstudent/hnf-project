# The chains of Ibn ʿAbbās's tafsīr

**ʿAbd Allāh ibn ʿAbbās** (d. 68 AH) is the exegetical source par excellence of Sunni Islam. The tafsīr corpus attributed to him underpins a major portion of Quranic exegesis. But the **four main chains** leading back to him are **all problematic**: interrupted (*munqaṭiʿ*), tainted by *tadlīs*, or transmitted through weak links.

None of these chains constitutes a solid connected isnād according to the criteria of the classical system itself.

## Summary table

| # | Chain | Verdict | Main problem |
|---|-------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | Muʿāwiya → ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭalḥa → Ibn ʿAbbās | **Munqaṭiʿ** | ʿAlī never met Ibn ʿAbbās (unanimous) |
| 2 | al-Ḍaḥḥāk ibn Muzāḥim → Ibn ʿAbbās | **Munqaṭiʿ** | al-Ḍaḥḥāk did not meet Ibn ʿAbbās (Shuʿba) |
| 3 | ʿUthmān ibn ʿAṭāʾ → ʿAṭāʾ al-Khurāsānī → Ibn ʿAbbās | **Doubly weak** | ʿUthmān is *matrūk*; ʿAṭāʾ did not meet Ibn ʿAbbās |
| 4 | Ibn Abī Najīḥ → Mujāhid → Ibn ʿAbbās | **The best, but problematic** | Ibn Abī Najīḥ = mudallis; did not hear everything from Mujāhid |

## Chain 1: Muʿāwiya → ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭalḥa → Ibn ʿAbbās

```
Ibn ʿAbbās (d. 68 AH)
    ╳ (break — unanimous)
ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭalḥa (d. ~143 AH)    ← shamela.ws/narrator/4501
    │
Muʿāwiya ibn Ṣāliḥ (d. ~158 AH)    ← shamela.ws/narrator/6239
    │
ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ṣāliḥ (kātib al-Layth)
    │
al-Bukhārī (taʿlīqāt only)
```

**[[Persons/ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭalḥa]]** ([shamela.ws/narrator/4501](https://shamela.ws/narrator/4501)) **never met** Ibn ʿAbbās — this is a **consensus**. The probable intermediaries are **Mujāhid** and **ʿIkrima**, but the chain omits them. It is a **ṣaḥīfa** (written document) transmitted without *samāʿ* (direct hearing). See his entry for the detailed evaluations and comparison with the real students of Ibn ʿAbbās.

**[[Persons/Muʿāwiya ibn Ṣāliḥ]]** is the next link — himself carrier of ~200 *ifrādāt* and a paradigmatic case of **[[Concepts/Ighrāb|ighrāb]]**.

Upstream, **ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ṣāliḥ** — the secretary (*kātib*) of al-Layth ibn Saʿd — is himself **controversial**: accused of negligence in the conservation of his manuscripts. His neighbor **Abū Ṣāliḥ** allegedly inserted apocryphal material into them.

Despite all this, **al-Bukhārī** uses this ṣaḥīfa — but only in the **taʿlīqāt** (suspended citations, without a complete chain), never in the body of the Ṣaḥīḥ with a connected isnād. This choice is in itself an admission: al-Bukhārī recognizes the value of the content but refuses to endorse it through a formal chain.

## Chain 2: al-Ḍaḥḥāk ibn Muzāḥim → Ibn ʿAbbās

```
Ibn ʿAbbās (d. 68 AH)
    ╳ (break)
al-Ḍaḥḥāk ibn Muzāḥim (d. ~105 AH)    ← shamela.ws/narrator/2957
    │
(often passes through Juwaybir ibn Saʿīd — very weak)
```

**Shuʿba ibn al-Ḥajjāj** — the greatest critic of his era — asserts that **al-Ḍaḥḥāk did not meet Ibn ʿAbbās** ([shamela.ws/narrator/2957](https://shamela.ws/narrator/2957)). Al-Ḍaḥḥāk took his tafsīr from **Saʿīd ibn Jubayr**, an actual student of Ibn ʿAbbās — but the chain omits this intermediary.

The problem worsens downstream: al-Ḍaḥḥāk's tafsīr often passes through **Juwaybir ibn Saʿīd**, a **very weak** transmitter (*ḍaʿīf jiddan*) whom the majority of critics abandon.

## Chain 3: ʿUthmān → ʿAṭāʾ al-Khurāsānī → Ibn ʿAbbās

```
Ibn ʿAbbās (d. 68 AH)
    ╳ (break — mursal)
ʿAṭāʾ al-Khurāsānī (d. ~135 AH)
    │
ʿUthmān ibn ʿAṭāʾ (his son — matrūk)
```

**Doubly weak**:
1. **ʿAṭāʾ al-Khurāsānī** did not meet Ibn ʿAbbās — his transmission is a **mursal** (interrupted)
2. **ʿUthmān ibn ʿAṭāʾ**, his own son and sole transmitter of this tafsīr, is **matrūk** (abandoned by critics)

This chain is the weakest of the four. The tafsīr attributed to ʿAṭāʾ al-Khurāsānī is in reality a **family compilation** without external control.

## Chain 4: Ibn Abī Najīḥ → Mujāhid → Ibn ʿAbbās

```
Ibn ʿAbbās (d. 68 AH)
    │ (the best connection of the four)
Mujāhid ibn Jabr (d. ~104 AH)
    │
    ├── direct hearing (partial)
    │
Ibn Abī Najīḥ (d. ~131 AH) — mudallis, 3rd ṭabaqa
    │
    └── also took from the kitāb of al-Qāsim ibn Abī Bazza (wijāda, not samāʿ)
```

This is the **best** of the four isnāds — Mujāhid is a genuine student of Ibn ʿAbbās, recognized for his tafsīr scholarship. But three problems remain:

1. **Ibn Abī Najīḥ** is a **mudallis** classified in the **3rd ṭabaqa** (Ibn Ḥajar, Ṭabaqāt al-Mudallisīn) — he conceals his real sources. When he says "from Mujāhid" (*ʿan Mujāhid*), he has not necessarily heard directly from him.

2. Ibn Abī Najīḥ did **not hear everything** from Mujāhid. Part of his tafsīr comes from the **kitāb** (written book) of **al-Qāsim ibn Abī Bazza** — this is not *samāʿ* but *wijāda* (discovery in a manuscript).

3. Ibn Abī Najīḥ is described as **muʿtazilī** — suspected of modifying the **theological** content of traditions, particularly on questions of *ṣifāt* (divine attributes) and *qadar* (free will).

## Comparative volume — Shamela data

The data from the Maktaba al-Shāmila reveals a quantitative paradox:

| Transmitter | Met Ibn ʿAbbās? | Tafsīr refs. | % of total | Sunna refs. |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭalḥa** | **No** (unanimous) | **4,458** | 66% | 588 |
| **ʿIkrima** | Yes (mawlā, in the household) | 4,176 | 12% | 12,440 |
| **Saʿīd ibn Jubayr** | Yes (direct student) | 4,167 | 18% | 8,732 |
| **al-Ḍaḥḥāk** | **No** (Shuʿba) | 2,408 | 55% | 392 |
| **Mujāhid** | Yes (3 recitations) | 1,268 | 16% | 2,632 |
| **Ṭāwūs** | Yes (direct student) | 226 | 4% | 2,272 |

The two transmitters who **never met** Ibn ʿAbbās (ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭalḥa and al-Ḍaḥḥāk) are also the most **specialized in tafsīr** (66% and 55%) — they are carriers of **documents**, not students. A genuine student transmits from everything (fiqh, hadith, narrative); a ṣaḥīfa carrier transmits only the document.

See [[Persons/ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭalḥa]] for the detailed analysis of the Mujāhid paradox and the lexical nature of the content.

## The founding paradox

The most widely used tafsīr corpus in Sunni Islam — that of Ibn ʿAbbās — rests on chains that **the system itself** recognizes as interrupted or weak.

The paradox is twofold:
1. **Each chain taken individually** is deficient: *munqaṭiʿ*, *matrūk*, *mudallis*
2. **All four converge** toward Ibn ʿAbbās — which suggests an authentic core, but transmitted through entirely defective channels

Al-Bukhārī illustrates this tension: he **uses** the material (in the taʿlīqāt) but **refuses** to certify it (no complete isnād in the body of the Ṣaḥīḥ). It is a pragmatic compromise: the content is too important to be ignored, but too weak to be guaranteed.

## The circuit of the four chains

```
                        IBN ʿABBĀS (d. 68 AH)
                       /     |      |       \
                      ╳      ╳      │        ╳
                     /       |      │         \
         ʿAlī ibn    al-Ḍaḥḥāk   Mujāhid   ʿAṭāʾ
         Abī Ṭalḥa                  │       al-Khurāsānī
             │            │         │            │
         Muʿāwiya    Juwaybir    Ibn Abī      ʿUthmān
         ibn Ṣāliḥ   (very       Najīḥ       ibn ʿAṭāʾ
             │        weak)      (mudallis)    (matrūk)
         ʿAbd Allāh
         ibn Ṣāliḥ
         (controversial)

    ╳ = break (munqaṭiʿ)     │ = attested transmission
```

All four paths to Ibn ʿAbbās are **obstructed**: three by breaks (*munqaṭiʿ*), the fourth by *tadlīs* and partial transmission via manuscript.
