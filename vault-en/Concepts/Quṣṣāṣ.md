# The Quṣṣāṣ (قصّاص)

Institution of public preaching in early Islam. The quṣṣāṣ told stories of the prophets, eschatological traditions, and [[Isrā'īliyyāt]] in the mosques.

## Historical evolution

| Period | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Before Islam | Free preaching, unregulated |
| Under [[Omar ibn al-Khattab\|'Umar]] | Strict control — only the caliph authorizes (Tarikh al-Madina p. 19-24) |
| Under [[Mu'āwiya ibn Abī Sufyān\|Mu'āwiya]] | Institutionalization — quṣṣāṣ as mandated functionaries |
| Under 'Umar ibn 'Abd al-'Azīz | Remuneration: 2 dinars/month (Madina p. 26) |
| Under Hishām | Reduction to 6 dinars/year (progressive disengagement) |

## The hadith of the three types

> **لا يقص إلا أمير أو مأمور أو مختال**
> "None may preach except a ruler, a mandated person, or an impostor."

This hadith is used by 'Awf ibn Mālik to contest the qaṣṣ of [[Ka'b al-Ahbar]] in [[Homs]]. [[Mu'āwiya ibn Abī Sufyān|Mu'āwiya]] circumvents the objection by officially mandating Ka'b.
