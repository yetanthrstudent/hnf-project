# Rajā' ibn Ḥaywa

**Adviser to the Umayyad caliphs**, judge, key figure in the sacralisation of **Bayt al-Maqdis** (Jerusalem). Abū al-Miqdām al-Kindī al-Shāmī al-Filasṭīnī (p. 7908). Third ṭabaqa of tābi'ūn of the Shām (Ibn Sa'd, p. 7907).

## Identity

- Tribe: **Kinda** — « أنا من الذين أنعم الله عليهم بالإسلام وعدادي في كندة » (p. 7907)
- Palestinian origin (فلسطيني)
- **Qāḍī** (judge) — p. 7908
- Reliable transmitter: « رجاء يحدث كما سمع » — "Rajā' transmits as he heard" (Ibn 'Awn, p. 7908)

## Transmission from Ka'b al-Aḥbār

Via his son **'Āṣim ibn Rajā'** (Faḍā'il Bayt al-Maqdis, shamela.ws/book/329/288):

> 'Āṣim <-- **Rajā'** <-- **[[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar|Ka'b]]**: When Ka'b left [[Lieux/Homs|Homs]] to pray in Jerusalem, "upon reaching the mile of Īliyā', he would cease speaking, reciting only the Quran and dhikr. He entered through Bāb al-Asbāṭ facing al-Quds, and prayed five prayers in the mosque." He was asked why: « **لأني أجد في بعض الكتب** أن الحسنات تضاعف في هذا المسجد » — "**Because I find in certain of the Books** that good deeds are multiplied in this mosque."

Ka'b sacralises Jerusalem by referring to the **scriptural Books** (بعض الكتب), and it is Rajā' who transmits this sacralisation.

## Builder of the Dome of the Rock

**'Abd al-Malik ibn Marwān** charges Rajā' ibn Ḥaywa with supervising the construction of the **Dome of the Rock** (Qubbat al-Ṣakhra) (Dimashq [p. 7910](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7910)) -- *ref. to be verified — the detailed account is attested in multiple sources, the exact page in Dimashq remains to be confirmed*.

The connection **Ka'b --> Rajā' --> 'Abd al-Malik** traces the circuit of sacralisation: Ka'b provides the **scriptural legitimation** ("I find in the Books"), Rajā' transmits and **executes** the architectural project, 'Abd al-Malik provides the **political power**.

## Relations within the network

- **Transmits from** [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar]] (via his son 'Āṣim)
- **Transmits from** [[Persons/Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ|'Abd Allāh ibn 'Amr]] and from Mu'āwiya (p. 7908)
- **Teacher of** [[Persons/Makhūl al-Shāmī]] — who calls him "my shaykh" (p. 27815)
- **Adviser to** 'Abd al-Malik ibn Marwān (construction of the Dome)
- **Key chain**: [[Persons/Al-Awzā'ī]] <-- [[Persons/Makhūl al-Shāmī|Makhūl]] <-- **Rajā'** <-- [[Persons/Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ|Ibn 'Amr]]

## Position in the circuit

```
Ka'b al-Aḥbār ("في بعض الكتب")     Ibn 'Amr (zamālatain)
         ↓                                    ↓
    RAJĀ' IBN ḤAYWA (caliphal adviser, judge, builder)
         ↓                          ↓
   'Abd al-Malik (Dome)     Makhūl (fiqh of the Shām)
                                    ↓
                              Al-Awzā'ī (Syrian school)
```

Rajā' is the **pivot** between scriptural legitimation (Ka'b), Umayyad power ('Abd al-Malik), and Syrian jurisprudence (Makhūl --> al-Awzā'ī).
