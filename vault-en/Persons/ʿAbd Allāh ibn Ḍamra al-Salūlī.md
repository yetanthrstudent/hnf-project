# ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra al-Salūlī — the invisible link

**Transmitter of [[Ka'b al-Ahbar|Kaʿb al-Aḥbār]]**, intermediary between isrāʾīliyyāt and the canonical chains of [[Abū Hurayra]]. His biography in Tahdhīb al-Kamāl spans **five lines**. He is absent from both Ṣaḥīḥs. None of the great early critics claims to know him — Ibn al-Madīnī: « لم يتبين عندي » (it was not clear to me). Yet he is declared **thiqa** on the basis of a single tawthīq (al-ʿIjlī) and a mention in Ibn Ḥibbān's Thiqāt — the most lenient compiler. And it is through him that hadiths attributed to Abū Hurayra transit which, according to Abū Zurʿa, are actually words of **Kaʿb** (Q.2022: هذا خطأ).

## The shortest biography — Tahdhīb al-Kamāl

The tarjama no. 3345 in Tahdhīb al-Kamāl ([p. 7614](https://shamela.ws/book/3722/7614)) is remarkably brief for a supposedly reliable transmitter:

> **ت سي ق: عَبد اللَّهِ بن ضمرة السلولي.**
>
> **رَوَى عَن:** كعب الأحبار (سي)، وأَبي الدَّرْدَاء، وأبي هُرَيْرة (د ق).
>
> **رَوَى عَنه:** ثابت بن ثوبان، وذكوان أَبُو صالح السمان (سي) وعبد الرحمن بْن سابط، وعطاء بْن قرة السلولي (ت ق)، ومجاهد بن جبر، وأَبُو الزبير المكي.

> « [Sigla] **Tirmidhī, Nasāʾī (al-Yawm wa-l-Layla), Ibn Mājah: ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra al-Salūlī.** »
>
> « **He transmitted from:** Kaʿb al-Aḥbār (Nasāʾī, Yawm wa-l-Layla), Abū al-Dardāʾ, and Abū Hurayra (Abū Dāwud, Ibn Mājah). »
>
> « **Transmitted from him:** Thābit ibn Thawbān, Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān (Nasāʾī, Yawm wa-l-Layla), ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Sābiṭ, ʿAṭāʾ ibn Qurra al-Salūlī (Tirmidhī, Ibn Mājah), Mujāhid ibn Jabr, and Abū al-Zubayr al-Makkī. »

> قال البخاري: قال علي: هو أخو عاصم بن ضمرة، **ولم يتبين عندي**.
>
> وذكره ابنُ حِبَّان فِي كتاب "الثقات".

> « Al-Bukhārī said: ʿAlī [ibn al-Madīnī] said: he is the brother of ʿĀṣim ibn Ḍamra — **but it was not clear to me.** »
>
> « Ibn Ḥibbān mentioned him in his book *al-Thiqāt*. »

Three shuyūkh, six transmitters, one sentence from Ibn al-Madīnī, one mention from Ibn Ḥibbān — that is all. No anecdote, no date of birth or death, no biographical context, no city of residence (only al-ʿIjlī, centuries later, will say "Kūfite"). Al-Mizzī found nothing more.

This is a **biography constructed from isnāds** — and solely from them. Nothing is known about this man outside the fact that he appears in chains of transmission. His shuyūkh (Kaʿb, Abū Hurayra, Abū al-Dardāʾ) are deduced from the isnāds where his name appears; his transmitters (Abū Ṣāliḥ, ʿAṭāʾ ibn Qurra, Mujāhid) are those who cite him in the same isnāds. Outside this lattice of chains, ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra **does not exist** in the sources: no account features him, no contemporary speaks of him, no early critic evaluated his hadiths individually. He is a man whom **nobody knows** — but whom the system validated after the fact because reliable people cite him. Circular reasoning par excellence.

## The tawthīq — what does it rest on?

| Critic                           | Judgment                               | Strength                                    |
| ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| **Al-ʿIjlī**                       | كوفي تابعي ثقة                         | Sole explicit tawthīq                   |
| **Ibn Ḥibbān**                     | al-Thiqāt (5/34, 51)                   | Lenient — Ibn Ḥibbān includes unknowns |
| **Ibn Ḥajar** (Taqrīb)             | وثقه العجلي                            | Cites al-ʿIjlī, adds nothing          |
| **Ibn al-Madīnī** (via al-Bukhārī) | لم يتبين عندي                          | **Uncertainty**                          |
| **Al-Bukhārī**                     | Reports Ibn al-Madīnī without contradicting | Silence                                  |

The pattern is one of **circular** tawthīq: Ibn Ḥajar declares thiqa citing al-ʿIjlī; al-ʿIjlī gives an overall judgment without justification; Ibn Ḥibbān includes him in his Thiqāt according to his rule: every man for whom he found no explicit jarḥ is included. No early critic claims to know him personally, possess biographical anecdotes, or have evaluated his corpus. The greatest isnād specialist of his era — ʿAlī ibn al-Madīnī — simply says: **"it was not clear to me."**

The implicit rule is that of **silence taken for approval**: since nobody declared him weak, he is reliable. But the absence of jarḥ is not a tawthīq — it is an **absence of information**.

## The hadith "al-dunyā malʿūna" — the textbook case

The hadith "This world is cursed, cursed is what it contains — except the dhikr of Allāh, what is related to it, a scholar or a student" is the best-known hadith passing through ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra.

**Sunan al-Tirmidhī**, no. 2322 ([p. 4018](https://shamela.ws/book/1435/4018)):

> حدثنا محمد بن حاتم المؤدب قال: حدثنا علي بن ثابت قال: حدثنا **عبد الرحمن بن ثابت بن ثوبان**، قال: سمعت **عطاء بن قرة**، قال: سمعت **عبد الله بن ضمرة**، قال: سمعت **أبا هريرة**، يقول: سمعت رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم يقول: **« ألا إن الدنيا ملعونة ملعون ما فيها إلا ذكر الله وما والاه وعالم أو متعلم »**
>
> « This hadith is **ḥasan gharīb**. »

**Sunan Ibn Mājah**, no. 4112 ([p. 2865](https://shamela.ws/book/98138/2865)): same chain, via Abū Khālid ʿUtba ibn Ḥammād.

### The single circuit

```
The Prophet (?)
        │
   ABŪ HURAYRA
        │
   ʿABD ALLĀH IBN ḌAMRA AL-SALŪLĪ
        │
   ʿAṬĀʾ IBN QURRA (al-Salūlī — same clan)
        │
   ʿABD AL-RAḤMĀN IBN THĀBIT IBN THAWBĀN
        │
        ├── ʿAlī ibn Thābit → al-Tirmidhī (no. 2322)
        │
        └── ʿUtba ibn Ḥammād → Ibn Mājah (no. 4112)
```

**Three observations**:

1. **Single madār** — The hadith rests on a single circuit: Ibn Thawbān ← ʿAṭāʾ ibn Qurra ← Ibn Ḍamra. No independent corroboration. Al-Tirmidhī knows it and says so: **gharīb**.

2. **Endogamous clan** — ʿAṭāʾ ibn Qurra is himself a **Salūlī** (identical nisba). Ibn Ḍamra and his principal transmitter belong to the same clan. This is a closed tribal circuit — the material circulates within a small network of Banū Salūl.

3. **Ibn Thawbān** — ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Thābit ibn Thawbān is criticized by Aḥmad: « كان رجلا صالحا ولكنه قلب أحاديث » — "He was a pious man but he **inverted hadiths** (qalaba)." Ibn Maʿīn: ḍaʿīf. Al-Nasāʾī: laysa bi-qawī.

The hadith's content — the world is cursed, only dhikr and knowledge matter — is a typical ascetic theme of [[Ka'b al-Ahbar|Kaʿb al-Aḥbār]]'s preaching (see [[Ka'b al-Ahbar#2. The ascetic preacher (p. 7970-7976)]]). The same "proto-Sufi" register that Kaʿb preaches from the "Books."

## Abū Zurʿa's correction — the actual circuit

When Abū Zurʿa examines hadiths attributed to "Suhayl → Abū Ṣāliḥ → Abū Hurayra," he corrects: the source is not Abū Hurayra. It is **al-Salūlī → Kaʿb**.

**ʿIlal Ibn Abī Ḥātim**, Q.2022 ([p. 3002](https://shamela.ws/book/1350/3002)):

> قال أبو زرعة: **هذا خطأ؛ إنما هو: سهيل، عن أبيه، عن السلولي، عن كعب؛ قوله.**
>
> « **This is an error. The correct chain is: Suhayl, from his father, from al-Salūlī, from Kaʿb — his own words.** »

**Suʾālāt al-Bardhaʿī**, Q.453 ([p. 449](https://shamela.ws/book/812/449)):

> **وإنما هو عن سهيل، عن أبيه، عن السلولي، عن كعب.**

The identification is certain: **al-Salūlī** in the ʿilal = ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra al-Salūlī in the rijāl. Al-Tamhīd of Ibn ʿAbd al-Barr ([p. 1833](https://shamela.ws/book/1719/1833)) makes the link explicit.

### The hidden circuit

```
KAʿB AL-AḤBĀR (isrāʾīliyyāt)                 The Prophet
        │                                             │
ʿABD ALLĀH IBN ḌAMRA AL-SALŪLĪ                  ABŪ HURAYRA
        │                                             │
        └───────────► ABŪ ṢĀLIḤ AL-SAMMĀN ◄───────────┘
                      (ṣaḥīfa: both sources mixed)
                               │
                      SUHAYL IBN ABĪ ṢĀLIḤ
                      (inherited ṣaḥīfa + ikhtilāṭ)
                               │
              ┌────────────────┼────────────────┐
              │                │                │
            Mālik         Ḥammād            Wuhayb
          (before ikhtilāṭ)  ibn Salama      (nuskha)
              │                │                │
              └────────┬───────┘                │
                       │                        │
                  ṢAḤĪḤ MUSLIM              Musnad Aḥmad
                (shawāhid mainly)

Documented error (Abū Zurʿa):
   "Suhayl → Abū Ṣāliḥ → Abū Hurayra → the Prophet (marfūʿ)"
                    ↓ correction ↓
   "Suhayl → Abū Ṣāliḥ → al-Salūlī → Kaʿb (mawqūf — qawluhu)"
```

Abū Ṣāliḥ transmits from both sides: from Abū Hurayra (prophetic hadiths) and from ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra (isrāʾīliyyāt from Kaʿb). In the ṣaḥīfa, both sources mix. Suhayl inherits the whole and — especially after the ikhtilāṭ — can no longer distinguish one from the other. Kaʿb's material is elevated to marfūʿ.

## The double circuit of Ibn Ḍamra

ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra actually feeds **two routes** into the canonical collections:

| Route | Circuit | Ends up in |
|---|---|---|
| **Via Abū Ṣāliḥ** | Ibn Ḍamra → Abū Ṣāliḥ → Suhayl → Mālik/Wuhayb | Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (shawāhid) |
| **Via ʿAṭāʾ ibn Qurra** | Ibn Ḍamra → ʿAṭāʾ → Ibn Thawbān → ... | Tirmidhī, Ibn Mājah |

In the first route, Ibn Ḍamra is **erased**: the chain is shortened to "Abū Ṣāliḥ → Abū Hurayra," and it is Abū Zurʿa who restores the missing link. In the second, he is named — but the circuit is judged **gharīb** (singular) and relies on a criticized transmitter (Ibn Thawbān).

## Evaluations

| Critic | Judgment | Actual meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Al-ʿIjlī** | كوفي تابعي ثقة | Sole tawthīq — no justification |
| **Ibn Ḥibbān** | al-Thiqāt | Lenient rule: every rāwī without jarḥ enters |
| **Ibn al-Madīnī** (via Bukhārī) | لم يتبين عندي | "Not clear to me" — the greatest muḥaddith of his time |
| **Ibn Ḥajar** | وثقه العجلي — 3rd ṭabaqa | Cites al-ʿIjlī, nothing more |
| **Al-Bukhārī** | Excludes him from the Ṣaḥīḥ | — |
| **Muslim** | Excludes him from the Ṣaḥīḥ | — |

The paradox: a transmitter whom **both** Ṣaḥīḥ authors excluded, whom the greatest isnād critic declares "not clear," but who is declared thiqa because al-ʿIjlī wrote one word and Ibn Ḥibbān did not find him in his lists of weak transmitters. And this transmitter serves as the bridge between Kaʿb al-Aḥbār and Abū Hurayra's chains.
