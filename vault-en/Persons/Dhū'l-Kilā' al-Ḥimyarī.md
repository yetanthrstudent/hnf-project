# Dhū'l-Kilā' al-Ḥimyarī

Himyarite military chief, **cousin** of [[Ka'b al-Ahbar]] (same tribal clan). Commander of the right wing of [[Mu'āwiya ibn Abī Sufyān|Mu'āwiya]] at Ṣiffīn (Dimashq [p. 7095](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7095)). Settled in [[Homs]] like Ka'b.

## Relationships

- **Cousin of** [[Ka'b al-Ahbar]] (same Ḥimyar tribe, clan Dhū'l-Kilā' / Dhū Ra'ayn) -- *to be verified in primary source*
- **In the service of** [[Mu'āwiya ibn Abī Sufyān]]
- **Son**: Shuraḥbīl ibn Dhī'l-Kilā' (Umayyad commander, killed at Khāzir 686 CE — Ṭabarī [p. 5814](https://shamela.ws/book/9783/5814)) -- *ref. to be verified*
- **Challenged by** 'Awf ibn Mālik about Ka'b's qaṣṣ -- *secondary source*

## The qiṣāṣ scene --

*Source: secondary research (hadithtransmitters.hawramani.com, Musnad Ahmad). To be located and verified in primary Shamela source.*

'Awf ibn Mālik enters the mosque of [[Homs]], leaning on Dhū'l-Kilā'. [[Ka'b al-Ahbar]] is doing qaṣṣ. 'Awf says: "Will you not stop your cousin?" and cites the hadith: "The [[Concepts/Quṣṣāṣ|quṣṣāṣ]] are of three types: a ruler, a delegate, or an impostor." Ka'b stops — then [[Mu'āwiya ibn Abī Sufyān|Mu'āwiya]] orders him to resume.

## Siffin

- Commander of the right wing (Ḥimyar + people of Homs) — القائم بأمر معاوية في حرب صفين (Dimashq [p. 7095](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7095))
- Killed in battle (37 H / 657 CE) (Dimashq [p. 7728](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7728))
- Mu'āwiya reportedly « فرح بموته » (rejoiced at his death) because Dhū'l-Kilā' was preparing a defection after becoming convinced of 'Alī's innocence -- *secondary source, not verified in Shamela*

## Summary

Ka'b and Dhū'l-Kilā' = the two faces of the Himyarite clan of [[Homs]]:
- Dhū'l-Kilā' → **military force**
- Ka'b → **narrative and religious legitimation**
