# Sufyān ibn ʿUyayna — the "best transmitter of Zuhrī" reassessed

Meccan imam, considered by the classical critics as the most reliable transmitter of al-Zuhrī — alongside Mālik. Pillar of both Ṣaḥīḥayn. Yet the sources document at least **four types of failures** in his transmissions from Zuhrī: the [[Concepts/Ittibāʿ al-Majarra|majarra]] (isnād substitution from memory), a **hidden intermediary** (Wā'il), a **wijāda** (transmission from a book, not audition), and a **contested raf'** (hadith wrongly elevated to marfūʿ status). These problems are not marginal anecdotes — they strike at the very heart of his specialty.

## The reputation — "the best for al-Zuhrī"

**Al-Rāzī**: thiqa imam — "the most reliable in the hadith of Zuhrī are Ibn ʿUyayna and Mālik" (Musnad al-Ḥumaydī, [p. 261](https://shamela.ws/book/8493/261)).

**ʿAlī ibn al-Madīnī**: "Who is stronger than Ibn ʿUyayna in the hadith of Zuhrī?"

This status makes him a **non-negotiable pillar** of the system: when Sufyān says "ḥaddathanā al-Zuhrī," the chain is accepted without question. It is precisely this status that makes the documented failures below so significant.

## Failure 1: The majarra — al-Shāfiʿī catches him in the act

See detailed entry: [[Concepts/Ittibāʿ al-Majarra]] (case 1).

Al-Shāfiʿī diagnoses that Sufyān **followed the Milky Way** in the hadith of ʿUmar on the ṭawāf:

| Version | Chain | Verdict |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Sufyān | al-Zuhrī → **ʿUrwa** → ʿAbd al-Raḥmān → ʿUmar | **Wrong** — majarra |
| Mālik, Yūnus | al-Zuhrī → **Ḥumayd ibn ʿAbd al-Raḥmān** → ʿAbd al-Raḥmān → ʿUmar | **Correct** |

The association al-Zuhrī → ʿUrwa is so famous that Sufyān slid into it automatically. Al-Shāfiʿī:

> اتبع سفيان ابن عيينة المجرّة

Manāqib al-Shāfiʿī ([p. 596](https://shamela.ws/book/22797/596))

## Failure 2: The hidden intermediary — Wā'il

ʿIlal al-Dāraquṭnī, hadith 3465 ([p. 5603](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/5603)) — the hadith "māl Abī Bakr" ("no wealth has benefited me like that of Abū Bakr"):

Two versions circulate:

| Transmitter | Chain | Type |
|---|---|---|
| **Sufyān** | al-Zuhrī → ʿUrwa → ʿĀʾisha | **musnad** (connected) |
| **Maʿmar** | al-Zuhrī → Saʿīd ibn al-Musayyab | **mursal** |

Al-Ḥumaydī confronts Sufyān — he is categorical:

> فَقِيلَ لِسُفْيَانَ فَإِنَّ مَعْمَرًا يَقُولُهُ عَنْ سَعِيدٍ فَقَالَ: مَا سَمِعْنَا مِنَ الزُّهْرِيِّ إِلَّا عَنْ عُرْوَةَ عَنْ عَائِشَةَ

> "Sufyān was told: Maʿmar reports it from Saʿīd [mursal]. He said: we heard from Zuhrī only the ʿUrwa → ʿĀʾisha version."

Musnad al-Ḥumaydī, hadith 252 ([p. 261](https://shamela.ws/book/8493/261))

But when **Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn** applies pressure, Sufyān reveals a hidden intermediary:

> فقال يحيى بن معين: عن ابن عيينة، عن الزهري، عن عروة، عن عائشة، قال: **فقيل لابن عيينة سمعته من الزهري، فقال: حدثني وائل**

> "Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn [reports]: from Ibn ʿUyayna, from Zuhrī, from ʿUrwa, from ʿĀʾisha. Ibn ʿUyayna was asked: did you hear it from Zuhrī? He said: **Wā'il transmitted it to me.**"

ʿIlal al-Dāraquṭnī ([p. 5603](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/5603))

The reversal is striking: facing al-Ḥumaydī, Sufyān is assertive — "we heard from Zuhrī only ʿUrwa → ʿĀʾisha." Facing Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn, he concedes: **ḥaddathanī Wā'il**. He did not hear the hadith directly from Zuhrī — he received it through an intermediary he normally does not mention. Al-Dāraquṭnī concludes diplomatically: "both versions are preserved from Zuhrī" (وكلاهما محفوظان عن الزهري).

## Failure 3: The Makhzumiyya — the book of Ayyūb ibn Mūsā

ʿIlal al-Dāraquṭnī, hadith 3466 ([p. 5605](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/5605)) — the hadith "I would have cut the hand of Fāṭima!":

ʿAlī ibn al-Madīnī reports:

> حدثنا **سفيان**، قال: **ذهبت أسأل الزهري، عن حديث المخزومية**، قال: ... تعني تلك التي سرقت الحلي، قال: **فصاح بي، فسكت**، قلت لسفيان: فلم تحمله عن أحد؟ قال: **وجدته في كتاب**، قلت لسفيان: كتاب من؟ قال: **كان كتاب أيوب بن موسى**، عن الزهري، عن عروة، عن عائشة

> "Sufyān said: **I went to ask al-Zuhrī about the hadith of the Makhzumiyya**. [Zuhrī] said: you mean the one who stole the jewelry? **He shouted at me, and I fell silent.** I said to Sufyān: so you did not receive it from anyone? He said: **I found it in a book**. I said: whose book? He said: **it was the book of Ayyūb ibn Mūsā**, from al-Zuhrī, from ʿUrwa, from ʿĀʾisha."

### Why did Zuhrī shout?

The sources give **no explicit reason**. No commentator (al-Qasṭallānī, Ibn al-Mulaqqin, al-Barmāwī) explains the shout. Several hypotheses are conceivable:

| Hypothesis | Argument |
|-----------|----------|
| Refusal to transmit this hadith to Sufyān | The fact that Sufyān could **not** obtain the samāʿ and had to resort to a book |
| Political sensitivity | The hadith touches the honor of the Banū Makhzūm, a major Quraysh clan — a delicate subject for al-Zuhrī, who lived at the Umayyad court |
| Sufyān's youth | He was about 15-16 years old when he studied with al-Zuhrī (arrived in Mecca around 120 H, Zuhrī died in 124 H) |
| Al-Zuhrī's temperament | Known for his sharp reactions in hadith assemblies |

The detail **فسكت** ("he fell silent") in al-Dāraquṭnī is revealing: Sufyān did not dare insist — the shout cut short any oral transmission.

### What is certain

Regardless of the reason for the shout, two facts are established:

1. **Sufyān admits a wijāda**: he did not hear the hadith from someone — he "found it in a book" (وجدته في كتاب). This is a **wijāda**, the weakest form of transmission
2. **The book belongs to a third party**: Ayyūb ibn Mūsā ibn ʿAmr ibn Saʿīd ibn al-ʿĀṣ al-Umawī — a transmitter whose book is not controlled by Sufyān, exactly the scenario Mālik fears in [[Concepts/Al-Ziyāda fī al-kutub|Al-Ziyāda fī al-kutub]]

### The hadith in al-Bukhārī

The hadith appears in **Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī** (no. 3733) with the isnād: ʿAlī [ibn al-Madīnī] → Sufyān → al-Zuhrī → ʿUrwa → ʿĀʾisha. Al-Bukhārī **includes the wijāda anecdote** within the body of the hadith — an act of transparency. He accepts the hadith not solely on Sufyān's authority, but because **other routes** (Ayyūb ibn Mūsā himself, Ismāʿīl ibn Umayya, Yūnus) report the same hadith through **direct audition** from al-Zuhrī (ʿIlal al-Dāraquṭnī, [p. 5605](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/5605)).

The problem is therefore not that the Makhzumiyya hadith is necessarily false — it is corroborated by other chains. The problem is what this incident reveals about **Sufyān's method**: for at least one major hadith, he relies not on audition but on a found book — yet in standard isnāds, he appears as a direct transmitter from Zuhrī.

## Failure 4: The ṣiyām — contested raf'

Sunan al-Nasāʾī, hadith 2661 ([p. 3956](https://shamela.ws/book/8361/3956)):

> ٢٦٦١ - أَخْبَرَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ حَرْبٍ المَوْصليُّ، قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، عَنْ حَمْزَةَ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللهِ، عَنْ حَفْصَةَ قَالَتْ: «لَا صِيَامَ لِمَنْ لَمْ يُجْمِعِ الصِّيامَ قَبْلَ الْفَجْرِ»

> Aḥmad ibn Ḥarb al-Mawṣilī informed us: Sufyān transmitted to us, from al-Zuhrī, from Ḥamza ibn ʿAbd Allāh, from Ḥafṣa: "There is no fasting for one who did not intend to fast before dawn."

Al-Nasāʾī comments:

> قَالَ أَبُو عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ: وَالصَّوَابُ عِنْدَنَا مَوْقُوفٌ، وَلَمْ يَصِحَّ رَفْعُهُ [...] **أَرْسَلَهُ مَالِكٌ**

> "What is correct in our view is **mawqūf** [Ḥafṣa's own statement, not the Prophet's], and it is not authentic to elevate it to marfūʿ. Mālik transmitted it as mursal."

The same pattern as failure 2: Sufyān "connects" an isnād where Mālik transmits more cautiously.

## The position on the ru'ya — "Kill him!"

Sufyān adopts an intransigent position on the ru'yat Allāh (the vision of God on the Day of Judgment) — a typically **Shāmī/Sunnī** theological position of the 2nd-3rd centuries, opposed to the Muʿtazila and Jahmiyya.

Naqḍ al-Dārimī ʿalā al-Marīsī ([p. 28](https://shamela.ws/book/18091/28)), citing the Tārīkh Baghdād:

> كنت عند ابن عيينة إذ أقبل بشر المريسي، فتكلم بذاك الكلام الرديء، فقال ابن عيينة: **اقتلوه**، قال ابن خلاد: فأنا فيمن ضربته بيدي

> "I was with Ibn ʿUyayna when Bishr al-Marīsī came and uttered his foul words. Ibn ʿUyayna said: **'Kill him!'** Ibn Khallād said: and I am among those who struck him with my hand."

And on al-Marīsī's denial of the ru'ya:

> قيل لسفيان بن عيينة: إن بشرا المريسي يقول: إن الله لا يرى يوم القيامة، فقال: **قاتله الله دويبة** ألم يسمع الله يقول: {كَلَّا إِنَّهُمْ عَنْ رَبِّهِمْ يَوْمَئِذٍ لَمَحْجُوبُونَ}

> "Sufyān was told: Bishr al-Marīsī says God will not be seen on the Day of Judgment. He said: **'May God fight this little creature!'** Has he not heard God say: {No! On that day they shall be veiled from their Lord} [83:15] — He made the veil a punishment for them. If both allies and enemies are veiled, what advantage do allies have over enemies?"

This position is **consistent** with the corpus of ṣifāt (anthropomorphism) circulating in Shāmī/Ḥijāzī networks of the period — the same milieu as [[Persons/Ḥammād ibn Salama|Ḥammād ibn Salama]] and the traditions of the divine vision.

## The pattern: musnad vs. mursal

A recurring pattern emerges in Sufyān's transmissions:

| Hadith | Sufyān's version | Alternative version | Problem |
|--------|------------------|---------------------|---------|
| ʿUmar and the ṭawāf | Zuhrī → **ʿUrwa** → ʿAbd al-R. → ʿUmar | Zuhrī → **Ḥumayd** → ʿAbd al-R. → ʿUmar | [[Concepts/Ittibāʿ al-Majarra|Majarra]] — false intermediary |
| Māl Abī Bakr | Zuhrī → ʿUrwa → ʿĀʾisha (**musnad**) | Zuhrī → Saʿīd (**mursal**) | Hidden intermediary **Wā'il** |
| Makhzumiyya | Zuhrī → ʿUrwa → ʿĀʾisha | Zuhrī **shouts** at Sufyān | **Wijāda** — book of Ayyūb ibn Mūsā |
| Ḥafṣa's fasting | Zuhrī → Ḥamza → Ḥafṣa (**marfūʿ**) | Mālik: **mursal** / **mawqūf** | Contested **raf'** |

In each case, Sufyān's version is **more connected**, **cleaner**, **more legally exploitable** than the alternative. And in each case, a critic or witness highlights a problem: false link, hidden intermediary, uncontrolled book, unjustified elevation.

The tendency is not necessarily deliberate fraud — it may be explained by a combination of **failing memory**, **excessive trust in third-party books**, and the **institutional pressure** to provide connected isnāds in a hadith market that values the musnad over the mursal. But the result is the same: a corpus presented as direct audition from Zuhrī that, for at least a portion, is not.

## The connection with Al-Ziyāda fī al-kutub

The Makhzumiyya case makes Sufyān a direct example of the phenomenon described in [[Concepts/Al-Ziyāda fī al-kutub]]: a transmitter who relies on a **third party's book** (Ayyūb ibn Mūsā) that he does not control, and whose content was rejected by al-Zuhrī himself. The wijāda is the weakest form of transmission — yet it ends up in Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī under the appearance of a samāʿ.

## Relationships

- **Transmits from** al-Zuhrī (documented problems), ʿAmr ibn Dīnār, [[Persons/Al-Aʿmash|al-Aʿmash]]
- **Transmitted by** al-Shāfiʿī, al-Ḥumaydī (shaykh of Bukhārī and Aḥmad), ʿAlī ibn al-Madīnī, Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal, Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn
- **Corrected by** al-Shāfiʿī (majarra), Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn (Wā'il), al-Nasāʾī (raf' of the ṣiyām)
- **Denounces** [[Persons/Ibrāhīm ibn Abī Yaḥyā|Ibrāhīm ibn Abī Yaḥyā]] — forbids transmitting from him
- **Theological position**: ru'yat Allāh (divine vision) — Shāmī/Sunnī, calls for the killing of Bishr al-Marīsī
