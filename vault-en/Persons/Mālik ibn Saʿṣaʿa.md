# Mālik ibn Saʿṣaʿa — the Companion of a single hadith

## The problem

Mālik ibn Saʿṣaʿa is presented as a **Companion of the Prophet**. His entire existence in hadith literature rests on a **single account**: the hadith of the **Miʿrāj** (the nocturnal ascension through the seven heavens). And this account reaches the corpus through a **single chain**:

```
The Prophet
    │
Mālik ibn Saʿṣaʿa (presumed Companion)
    │
Anas ibn Mālik
    │
QATĀDA ibn Diʿāma (sole transmitter from Anas for this hadith)
    │
┌───┴───┐
│       │
Shuʿba  Hishām al-Dastuwāʾī (and other students of Qatāda)
```

**Al-Dāraquṭnī** confirms that Qatāda is the **sole vector** of this character in the corpus (ʿIlal al-Dāraquṭnī, shamela.ws/book/9082 -- *exact reference to be located*).

## What is missing

1. **No independent biography.** The ṭabaqāt and rijāl works document Mālik ibn Saʿṣaʿa only in connection with this unique hadith. No death date, no confirmed place of residence, no transmitting descendants, no second tradition.

2. **No other student of Anas knows this hadith.** Anas ibn Mālik has **dozens** of direct transmitters — Thābit al-Bunānī, Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl, al-Zuhrī, ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz ibn Ṣuhayb, and many others. None of them reports the Miʿrāj hadith via Mālik ibn Saʿṣaʿa. Only [[Qatāda ibn Di'āma|Qatāda]] knows it.

3. **No corroboration from other Companions.** An event as cosmic as the Prophet's ascension through the seven heavens, with encounters of past prophets, should be known from multiple sources. The fact that it passes through only one unknown Companion, relayed by a single mudallis, is methodologically problematic.

## The recurring pattern

Mālik ibn Saʿṣaʿa fits into a **pattern** documented for Qatāda: being the sole entry point for a major account into the canonical corpus. The same phenomenon is observable for:

- **Abū al-ʿĀliya**: Shuʿba asserts that Qatāda heard from him only 3 things — but the Isrāʾ hadith (4th hadith) is in Bukhārī and Muslim
- **Khilās ibn ʿAmr**: Yaḥyā ibn Saʿīd al-Qaṭṭān refuses the entire Qatāda ← Khilās chain as mursal
- **The ṣaḥīfa of Jābir**: Qatāda did not hear Sulaymān al-Yashkurī but transmits the entire ṣaḥīfa

In each case, Qatāda absorbs material without verifiable samāʿ and reproduces it in the oral circuit as if he had heard it directly.

## The status of "Companion"

The principle of **[[Concepts/ʿAdālat al-Ṣaḥāba|ʿadālat al-ṣaḥāba]]** (absolute probity of the Companions) presupposes that the status of Companion is **established**. Yet this status, for Mālik ibn Saʿṣaʿa, rests entirely on:

- A single hadith
- Transmitted by a single student of Anas (Qatāda)
- Qatāda being a notorious mudallis and a qāṣṣ who transforms written and oral material into formal hadith

The reasoning is **circular**: Mālik is a Companion because he appears in a hadith — and the hadith is authentic because its transmitter is a Companion.
