# Sarj al-Yarmūkī

**Convert** from the People of the Book who lived in the Prophet's era and converted after him. An identical profile to [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar]]: a former scriptural scholar integrated into the Muslim community during the conquests.

## His tradition

Transmitted via the chain **Ḥammād ibn Salama <-- Ya'lā ibn 'Aṭā' <-- Baḥīr Abū 'Ubayd <-- Sarj**:

> « أجد في الكتاب أن هذه الأمة اثنا عشر ربيا نبيهم أحدهم، فإذا وفت العدة طغوا وبغوا وكان بأسهم بينهم »

> "I find in the Book that this community will have twelve chiefs, their prophet being one of them, and when the number is reached they will become tyrannical and their violence will be among themselves."

Attested in three sources: al-Iṣāba (Ibn Ḥajar), Kitāb al-Fitan (Nu'aym ibn Ḥammād), Tārīkh Dimashq (Ibn 'Asākir).

## Link with Ibn 'Amr

All three sources unanimously confirm:
> « وكان عبد الله بن عمرو **يتعلم من سرج هذا** »
> "And [[Persons/Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ|'Abd Allāh ibn 'Amr]] **would learn from this Sarj**."

Sarj is thus a **scriptural master** of Ibn 'Amr — a direct channel for the transmission of biblical/Jewish material into the Islamic corpus, parallel to the [[Concepts/Isrā'īliyyāt|zamālatain]] recovered at Yarmūk.
