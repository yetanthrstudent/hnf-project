# Thābit al-Bunānī

**Basran transmitter**, one of the principal reporters of **[[Persons/Anas ibn Mālik|Anas ibn Mālik]]** alongside [[Persons/Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl|Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl]] and [[Persons/Qatāda ibn Di'āma|Qatāda ibn Di'āma]]. Classified **thiqa ma'mūn** (reliable and trusted) by Ibn Sa'd.

## Death under Khālid al-Qasrī

Ibn Sa'd dates his death by the governorship of [[Persons/Khālid ibn 'Abd Allāh al-Qasrī|Khālid al-Qasrī]] over Iraq (Tahdhīb al-Tahdhīb, [p. 257](https://shamela.ws/book/1293/257); Ṭabaqāt Ibn Sa'd):

> « كان ثقة مأمونا **توفي في ولاية خالد القسري** »

> "He was reliable and trusted, **died during the governorship of Khālid al-Qasrī**."

Hishām dates his death to **118 H** in Basra. His death is thus contemporaneous with that of [[Persons/Qatāda ibn Di'āma|Qatāda ibn Di'āma]] (117 H in Wāsiṭ).

## Intimate bond with Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl

[[Persons/Ḥammād ibn Zayd|Ḥammād ibn Zayd]] reports from [[Persons/Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl|Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl]] (Ṭabaqāt Ibn Sa'd):

> « قال لي ثابت البناني: **اغسلني ولا تسلخنّ جلدي** »

> "Thābit al-Bunānī told me: **wash me [after my death] and do not flay my skin.**"

A request of intimate trust — Thābit entrusts his mortuary washing to Ḥumayd, a sign of their personal closeness.

## The hidden role in Ḥumayd's tadlīs

Thābit is the **actual source** of a large proportion of the hadiths that [[Persons/Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl|Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl]] attributes to Anas. [[Persons/Ḥammād ibn Salama|Ḥammād ibn Salama]] (Ḥumayd's nephew):

> « عامة ما يروي حميد عن أنس لم يسمعه منه، إنما سمعه من ثابت »

> "The majority of what Ḥumayd reports from Anas, he did not hear from him — **he heard it from Thābit**."

Thābit → Ḥumayd → "Anas said": the real chain is masked by tadlīs. Thābit is thus the invisible intermediary of dozens of canonical hadiths.

## The Thābit → Anas association as "majarra"

The association Thābit → Anas is so automatic (~90% of his traditions) that it functions as an **attractor**: when a transmitter takes from Thābit a hadith that passes through a different link, he instinctively routes it to Anas. This is the documented case of **al-Mubārak ibn Faḍāla** in the hadith "when a man loves his brother" — Abū Ḥātim diagnoses an **[[Concepts/Ittibāʿ al-Majarra|ittibāʿ al-majarra]]**: the true chain passes through Ḥabīb ibn Ṣubayʿa al-Ḍubaʿī, not through Anas. It is [[Persons/Ḥammād ibn Salama|Ḥammād ibn Salama]], the most reliable of Thābit's students, who preserves the correct chain.
