# Jābir ibn 'Abd Allāh — the ṣaḥīfa and its channels

One of the most prolific Companions (~1,540 hadiths), last Companion to die in Medina (~78 H). **The central problem**: out of his ~90 transmitters, the four most prolific (Abū al-Zubayr, Abū Sufyān, 'Amr ibn Dīnār, [[Qatāda ibn Di'āma|Qatāda]]) are accused of having used the **ṣaḥīfa of Sulaymān al-Yashkurī** rather than hearing directly from Jābir. [[al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī]], whose irsāl is notorious, confirms the problem through his formula "ballaghanī."

## The ṣaḥīfa of Sulaymān ibn Qays al-Yashkurī

### The author

**Sulaymān ibn Qays al-Yashkurī**, Basran tābi'ī, sat with Jābir, heard from him, and wrote a **ṣaḥīfa**. He died during the fitna of Ibn al-Zubayr (64-73 H), **before Jābir himself**. Ibn Ḥibbān describes him as among the best people of Medina. He was one-eyed (a'war).

### The posthumous circulation

After Sulaymān's death, the ṣaḥīfa **remained with his wife** (baqiyat 'inda imra'atihi). It was then transported to Basra, where **[[Qatāda ibn Di'āma|Qatāda]] memorized it** (ḥafiẓahā Qatāda).

Yaḥyā ibn Ma'īn: "Sulaymān al-Yashkurī — Qatāda did not hear from him, nor did 'Amr ibn Dīnār, because he was killed during the fitna of Ibn al-Zubayr." When asked if 'Amr had reached him: "Perhaps 'Amr reached him" (la'alla 'Amran adrakahu).

## The five channels of the ṣaḥīfa

| Transmitter | Type of access | Direct samā' from Jābir? | Proof |
|---|---|---|---|
| **[[Abū al-Zubayr al-Makkī]]** | 2 books given to al-Layth | **Partial** — « منه ما سمعت ومنه ما حدثت عنه » | Al-Layth ibn Sa'd |
| **Abū Sufyān Ṭalḥa ibn Nāfi'** | Ṣaḥīfa | **4 hadiths only** (Abū Khālid al-Dalānī) | Shu'ba: « إنما هي صحيفة » |
| **'Amr ibn Dīnār** | Probable access to the ṣaḥīfa | **No** — confession to Shu'ba | Shu'ba: « أنت سمعته من جابر ؟ قال : لا » |
| **[[Qatāda ibn Di'āma]]** | Memorization of the ṣaḥīfa in Basra | **No** — did not hear al-Yashkurī | Ibn Ma'īn |
| **[[al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī]]** | Access via ṣaḥīfa | **No** — irsāl confirmed ("ballaghanī") | Argument from silence |

### Abū Sufyān — "he attributes too much"

Shu'ba: « حديث أبي سفيان عن جابر إنما هي صحيفة » — his hadiths from Jābir are nothing but a ṣaḥīfa. Abū Khālid al-Dalānī: he heard from Jābir only **4 hadiths**. Al-Bazzār: « لم يسمع منه شيئا وقد روى عنه نحو مائة حديث وإنما هي صحيفة عرفت » — he heard nothing from him, yet transmitted about 100 hadiths; it is nothing but a known ṣaḥīfa.

The criticism "**yusnidu kathīran**" (يسند كثيرا — he attributes too much) means that Abū Sufyān elevated to the Prophet material whose status (prophetic vs. personal) was not clearly marked in the ṣaḥīfa.

### 'Amr ibn Dīnār — the confession and the confusion

Shu'ba asks 'Amr directly (Musnad Aḥmad 3/368):

> « قلت لعمرو : أنت سمعته من جابر ؟ قال : لا »
>
> "I said to 'Amr: did you yourself hear it from Jābir? He said: **No.**"

Al-Ḥākim: « عامة حديث عمرو بن دينار عن الصحابة غير مسموعة » — the majority of his hadiths from the Companions were not heard directly.

**The confusion of the two Jābirs**: Sufyān ibn 'Uyayna corrects Abū Ḥanīfa (Su'ālāt al-Bardha'ī, [p. 1030](https://shamela.ws/book/812/1030)): « فقلت : حدثنا عمرو عن **جابر بن زيد**، ليس جابر بن عبد الله » — 'Amr was transmitting from Jābir ibn Zayd (Basran tābi'ī of the Ibāḍiyya, d. ~93 H), not from the Companion. This confusion problem is recurrent.

### Al-Ḥasan — the argument from silence

When al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī possessed the ṣaḥīfa and transmitted hadiths from Jābir, he used **« بلغني أن أصحاب رسول الله قالوا »** ("it reached me that the Companions of the Prophet said") — the formula of irsāl. Had he heard directly from Jābir, he would have used the samā' formula. His recourse to "ballaghanī" proves that he had **only the ṣaḥīfa** as a source.

## The circuit of the ṣaḥīfa — synthesis

```
JĀBIR IBN 'ABD ALLĀH (d. ~78 H, Medina)
        │
   [DIRECT AUDITION — samā' confirmed]
        │
SULAYMĀN IBN QAYS AL-YASHKURĪ
   (writes the ṣaḥīfa, dies ~64-73 H during the fitna of Ibn al-Zubayr)
        │
   [WRITTEN ṢAḤĪFA — remains with his wife, transported to Basra]
        │
        ├── QATĀDA (memorizes the ṣaḥīfa)
        │   │
        │   └─→ Hammām, Sa'īd, Shu'ba → ṢAḤĪḤAYN
        │
        ├── AL-ḤASAN AL-BAṢRĪ (irsāl: "ballaghanī")
        │
        ├── ABŪ SUFYĀN ṬALḤA IBN NĀFI' ("إنما هي صحيفة")
        │   │
        │   └─→ al-A'mash → ṢAḤĪḤAYN (hadith tark al-ṣalāt)
        │
        ├── ABŪ AL-ZUBAYR (partial samā' + ṣaḥīfa)
        │   │
        │   ├─→ al-Layth (the only one to have sorted)
        │   └─→ Ibn Jurayj (contaminated by Yāsīn al-Zayyāt) → MUSLIM
        │
        └── 'AMR IBN DĪNĀR ("أنت سمعته من جابر ؟ قال : لا")
            │
            └─→ Ḥammād ibn Zayd → IḌṬIRĀB (4 versions, Dāraquṭnī)
```

## Consequence

When the same hadith circulates through **several independent channels**, all passing through the ṣaḥīfa of al-Yashkurī, the "plurality of routes" (ta'addud al-ṭuruq) is a mirage: all roads lead to the **same written document**. The hadith of [[Traditions/Tark al-Ṣalāt|tark al-ṣalāt]] is the perfect example: the two chains in Muslim (al-A'mash ← Abū Sufyān ← Jābir and Ibn Jurayj ← Abū al-Zubayr ← Jābir) pass through ṣaḥīfa transmitters.

And the only verified channel — that of al-Layth ibn Sa'd, who had Abū al-Zubayr sort samā'/non-samā' — is itself compromised by [[Khālid al-Madā'inī]], a forger who interpolated al-Layth's books. The chain of trust is broken at **every level**. See [[Concepts/Falsification des nusakh]].
