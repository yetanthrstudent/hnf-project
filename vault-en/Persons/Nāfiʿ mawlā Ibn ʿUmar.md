# Nāfiʿ mawlā Ibn ʿUmar — the untouchable link

**Freed slave of Ibn ʿUmar**, unanimously thiqa, pillar of the [[Concepts/Silsilat al-Dhahab]] (Mālik --> Nāfiʿ --> Ibn ʿUmar) — "the most authentic chain" according to al-Bukhārī. Al-Khalīlī goes so far as to say: "**no error is known** of his in all that he transmitted". Yet al-Dāraquṭnī documents precisely such errors — and the chain carrying the jāriya hadith, a textbook case of iḍṭirāb, shows a transmitter whose **immediate source changes with every narration**.

## Profile

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Full name | Abū ʿAbd Allāh Nāfiʿ, mawlā ʿAbd Allāh ibn ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb |
| Origin | Captured slave — Maghreb, Nīshāpūr or Kabul depending on sources. Father: Hurmuz or Kāwus |
| Death | 117 H (majority opinion); 119 H (Aḥmad, Ibn ʿUyayna) |
| Role | Principal transmitter of Ibn ʿUmar, sent to Egypt by ʿUmar ibn ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz to teach |
| Status | Pillar of the silsilat al-dhahab — "aṣaḥḥ al-asānīd" (al-Bukhārī) |

## Evaluation — flawless unanimity (in appearance)

| Critic | Judgment |
|----------|----------|
| **Al-Khalīlī** | « من أئمة التابعين، متفق عليه، صحيح الرواية، ولا يُعرف له خطأ في جميع ما روى » — "imam, unanimous, **no known error**" |
| **Mālik** | "When I heard Nāfiʿ transmit from Ibn ʿUmar, I no longer cared about hearing from anyone else" |
| **Sufyān ibn ʿUyayna** | « فأيّ حديث أوثق من حديث نافع؟ » — "Which hadith is more reliable than Nāfiʿ's?" |
| **Ibn Ḥajar** | « ثقة ثبت فقيه مشهور » |

This consensus explains why **nobody ever criticises Nāfiʿ himself** — when there is a problem, his students are blamed. But the problems documented by al-Dāraquṭnī come **from Nāfiʿ**, not from his students.

## An artificially favoured transmitter

### The son vs the slave

**Sālim ibn ʿAbd Allāh** is the biological son of Ibn ʿUmar. Al-Nasāʾī says explicitly: « سالم أجلّ من نافع » — "**Sālim is more eminent than Nāfiʿ**". And yet: it is the **slave** who receives the silsilat al-dhahab, not the son.

More revealing still — ʿAbbās al-Dūrī reports from Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn:

> "Nāfiʿ did not transmit [publicly] before the death of Sālim."

Nāfiʿ lived in **Sālim's shadow**. As long as the biological son of Ibn ʿUmar was alive, the slave did not transmit publicly. Only after Sālim's death (~106 H) does Nāfiʿ become the reference — not because he was better, but because the **main channel** had disappeared and Nāfiʿ was the only one left standing.

### The mechanism of elevation

The construction of Nāfiʿ's status follows a cumulative logic:

1. **Mālik chooses Nāfiʿ** as his main source for Ibn ʿUmar — a choice of geographical proximity (both Medinans) and availability (Sālim is dead)
2. **Al-Bukhārī** consecrates the chain: "aṣaḥḥ al-asānīd: Mālik ʿan Nāfiʿ ʿan Ibn ʿUmar" — the formula crystallises
3. **Al-Ḥāzimī** (d. 584 H) extends the chain upstream (Aḥmad --> al-Shāfiʿī --> Mālik --> Nāfiʿ --> Ibn ʿUmar) and calls it "silsilat al-dhahab" — the label is born
4. **Al-Khalīlī** locks it down: "no error is known of his in all that he transmitted" — any future criticism is rendered illegitimate
5. Later scholars **inherit** the consensus without re-examining it — and when al-Dāraquṭnī documents a flagrant iḍṭirāb, nobody questions Nāfiʿ; they blame his students

The result: a transmitter whose contradictions are **documented in the very sources** of the tradition, but whom the tradition refuses to criticise because doing so would mean destroying the most prestigious chain in the corpus.

### The real problem: untouchability

The fact that critics side with Nāfiʿ on the 3 divergences with Sālim is not suspicious in itself — Nāfiʿ tends toward caution (he lowers the attribution to ʿUmar or Kaʿb instead of elevating it to the Prophet), and this caution is often a mark of reliability. The problem lies elsewhere. It is in the **untouchable status** the tradition has built for him: when the jāriya iḍṭirāb shows that his source changes five times, nobody says "Nāfiʿ contradicts himself". Everyone says "his students are mistaken". The "silsilat al-dhahab" label has rendered criticism of Nāfiʿ **structurally impossible** — not because he is irreproachable, but because questioning him would destroy too much material.

## The irsāls — what he did not hear

| Chain | Judgment | Critic |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Nāfiʿ --> ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb | **munqaṭiʿ** | Aḥmad |
| Nāfiʿ --> ʿUthmān | **mursal** | Abū Zurʿa |
| Nāfiʿ --> Umm Salama | « لا يصح له سماع » | Al-Dāraquṭnī |
| Nāfiʿ --> ʿĀʾisha | partially **mursal** | Abū Ḥātim |
| Nāfiʿ --> Ḥafṣa | partially **mursal** | Abū Ḥātim |

Despite these irsāls, al-Bukhārī and Muslim include transmissions from Nāfiʿ via ʿĀʾisha and Abū Hurayra in the Ṣaḥīḥayn.

## The jāriya hadith — a textbook case of iḍṭirāb

### The narrative

A servant girl (jāriya) of Kaʿb ibn Mālik was tending sheep at Salʿ (near Medina). Seeing a ewe about to die, she slaughtered it with a stone. The Prophet authorised the consumption of the meat.

### The six variants of Nāfiʿ's source

Al-Dāraquṭnī documents the iḍṭirāb in three pages (ʿIlal no. 2975, [pp. 4900-4902](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/4900)). The problem is not the matn (the text is stable) — it is **Nāfiʿ's immediate source** that changes with each narration:

| Version | Chain via Nāfiʿ | Nāfiʿ's source | Companion | Ref. |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------|------|
| **Dāraquṭnī** (via Abū Ḥanīfa) | Nāfiʿ --> **Ibn ʿUmar** --> Kaʿb | **Ibn ʿUmar** | Kaʿb | [ʿIlal 2975](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/4900) |
| **Bukhārī** no. 2304 | ʿUbaydillāh --> Nāfiʿ --> **ibn Kaʿb** --> Kaʿb | **A son of Kaʿb** (which one?) | Kaʿb | [Bukhārī](https://shamela.ws/book/1681/3657) |
| **Muwaṭṭaʾ** | Mālik --> Nāfiʿ --> **rajul min al-Anṣār** --> Muʿādh/Saʿd | **An Anṣārī man** (anonymous) | **Muʿādh ibn Saʿd or Saʿd ibn Muʿādh** (shakk!) | [Muwaṭṭaʾ](https://shamela.ws/book/1699/1409) |
| **ʿAbd al-Razzāq** no. 8829 | Ibn ʿUyayna --> Ayyūb ibn Mūsā --> Nāfiʿ --> **rajul min Banī Salima** --> Ibn ʿUmar | **A man of Banū Salima** (anonymous) | — | [Muṣannaf](https://shamela.ws/book/84/2377) |
| **ʿAbd al-Razzāq** no. 8819 | ʿUbaydillāh --> Nāfiʿ --> **Sulaymān ibn Yasār** | **Sulaymān ibn Yasār** | — (mursal) | [Muṣannaf](https://shamela.ws/book/84/2376) |
| **ʿAbd al-Razzāq** no. 8818 | Maʿmar --> Ayyūb / ʿUbaydillāh --> Nāfiʿ | **(nobody)** | — (mursal) | [Muṣannaf](https://shamela.ws/book/84/2376) |

Six versions, **five different sources** for Nāfiʿ (Ibn ʿUmar, a son of Kaʿb, an Anṣārī, a man of Banū Salima, Sulaymān ibn Yasār) — plus a mursal version with no source at all. And the Companion himself varies: Kaʿb ibn Mālik in most versions, but "Muʿādh ibn Saʿd or Saʿd ibn Muʿādh" in the Muwaṭṭaʾ (with a shakk on the name).

### Al-Dāraquṭnī's verdict

Al-Dāraquṭnī rules: the **maḥfūẓa** (preserved) version is that of Juwayriya ibn Asmāʾ and Muḥammad ibn Isḥāq — "Nāfiʿ heard **an Anṣārī man** narrate to Ibn ʿUmar". Hence: an **anonymous** intermediary between Nāfiʿ and Ibn ʿUmar. The direct connection Nāfiʿ --> Ibn ʿUmar is an error.

### What this means

The standard explanation — "it is Nāfiʿ's students who err, not Nāfiʿ himself" — does not hold. The students in question are **themselves thiqāt**: Mālik, ʿUbaydullāh ibn ʿUmar, Ibn ʿUyayna, Ayyūb. One cannot accuse five reliable transmitters of each having invented a different source for Nāfiʿ. The problem comes **from Nāfiʿ himself**, who narrated this hadith on different occasions while changing his source.

This case cannot be explained by the theory of "multiple occasions": the story of a specific servant girl of Kaʿb ibn Mālik who slaughters a specific ewe at Salʿ cannot have occurred **six times**.

## The three divergences between Nāfiʿ and Sālim

Al-Nasāʾī identifies three hadiths where Nāfiʿ and Sālim (Ibn ʿUmar's son) diverge on the same tradition (Sharḥ ʿIlal al-Tirmidhī, [p. 607](https://shamela.ws/book/9552/607)). Remarkable pattern: in all three cases, Nāfiʿ **lowers** the attribution while Sālim **elevates** it to the Prophet:

| Hadith | Sālim | Nāfiʿ | Verdict |
|--------|-------|-------|---------|
| "Fī-mā saqat al-samāʾ al-ʿushr" (tithe) | **Marfūʿ** (from the Prophet) | **Mawqūf** (from ʿUmar) | Nāfiʿ preferred |
| "Man bāʿa ʿabdan wa-lahu māl" (sale of slave) | **Marfūʿ** | **Mawqūf** (from ʿUmar) | Nāfiʿ preferred |
| "Takhruju nār min qibal al-Yaman" (fire from Yemen) | **Marfūʿ** | **From [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar\|Kaʿb al-Aḥbār]]**, not the Prophet | Nāfiʿ preferred |

The third case is the most significant for our purposes: what Sālim attributes to the Prophet, Nāfiʿ attributes to **Kaʿb al-Aḥbār**. The critics side with Nāfiʿ — which means that even the muḥaddithūn implicitly acknowledge that material from Kaʿb has been **elevated** to the Prophet. This is the same phenomenon documented in the chain [[Persons/Al-Aʿmash|al-Aʿmash]] <-- Abū Ṣāliḥ <-- [[Persons/Abū Hurayra]], and the same one denounced by **Ibn Rajab**: "if one were to list the hadiths elevated to the Prophet that are actually from Kaʿb, the list would be long".

## The ghusl of the dead — from an observed act to a prophetic hadith

A striking example of the **evolution** of material through the chains. Al-Bayhaqī (Sunan al-Kubrā, Bāb al-ghusl min ghusl al-mayyit) juxtaposes four traditions of Nāfiʿ on the same subject — ritual washing after touching a dead body. Read together, they document three successive stages.

### Stage 1 — the observed act (fiʿl)

Bayhaqī no. 1468 ([p. 1674](https://shamela.ws/book/7861/1674)) — Shuʿayb ibn Abī Ḥamza --> Nāfiʿ:

> قَالَ نَافِعٌ: قَدْ رَأَيْتُ عَبْدَ اللهِ بْنَ عُمَرَ **حَنَّطَ سَعِيدَ بْنَ زَيْدٍ** وَحَمَلَهُ فِيمَنْ حَمَلَهُ، ثُمَّ **دَخَلَ الْمَسْجِدَ فَصَلَّى وَلَمْ يَتَوَضَّأْ**
>
> "Nāfiʿ said: I saw ʿAbd Allāh ibn ʿUmar **embalm Saʿīd ibn Zayd**, carry him among those who carried him, then **enter the mosque and pray without performing ablutions**."

A simple eyewitness testimony. Nāfiʿ **sees** Ibn ʿUmar do something. No prescription, no hadith — an act.

### Stage 2 — collective practice

Bayhaqī no. 1466 ([p. 1672](https://shamela.ws/book/7861/1672)) — Wuhayb --> ʿUbayd Allāh ibn ʿUmar --> Nāfiʿ --> Ibn ʿUmar:

> كُنَّا نُغَسِّلُ الْمَيِّتَ فَمِنَّا مَنْ يَغْتَسِلُ وَمِنَّا مَنْ لَا يَغْتَسِلُ
>
> "We used to wash the dead; among us, **some performed the ghusl and others did not**."

Bayhaqī no. 1467 ([p. 1673](https://shamela.ws/book/7861/1673)) — Shuʿayb --> Nāfiʿ (without Ibn ʿUmar!):

> كُنَّا نُغَسِّلُ الْمَيِّتَ فَيَتَوَضَّأُ بَعْضُنَا وَيَغْتَسِلُ بَعْضٌ
>
> "We used to wash the dead; some of us performed the wuḍūʾ and others the ghusl."

The individual act (Ibn ʿUmar embalms Saʿīd ibn Zayd) has become a **collective practice** ("we used to..."). The subject shifts from one person to a group.

### Stage 3 — the prescriptive statement (mawqūf)

Bayhaqī no. 1465 ([p. 1671](https://shamela.ws/book/7861/1671)) — al-ʿUmarī --> Nāfiʿ --> Ibn ʿUmar:

> كَانَ ابْنُ عُمَرَ يَقُولُ: **مَنْ غَسَّلَ مَيِّتًا فَأَصَابَهُ مِنْهُ شَيْءٌ فَلْيَغْتَسِلْ وَإِلَّا فَلْيَتَوَضَّأْ**
>
> "Ibn ʿUmar **used to say**: Whoever washes a dead body and is touched by something from it, **let him perform the ghusl**; otherwise, **let him perform the wuḍūʾ**."

The observed act has become a **qawl** — a normative statement. Ibn ʿUmar no longer "does"; he "says". And the content has **reversed**: in stage 1, Ibn ʿUmar prays **without wuḍūʾ** after embalming a dead body; in stage 3, he **prescribes** ghusl or wuḍūʾ.

### Al-Dāraquṭnī: والموقوف أصح

Al-Dāraquṭnī (ʿIlal no. 2896, [p. 4778](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/4778)) examines this hadith:

> وَسُئِلَ عَنْ حَدِيثِ نَافِعٍ، عن ابن عمر: كنا نغسل الميت، فينا من يغتسل وفينا من لم يغتسل.
>
> [...] ورواه ليث، عن نافع، موقوفا أيضا. وكذلك رواه سعيد بن جبير، عن ابن عمر، موقوفا.
>
> **والموقوف أصح.**
>
> "The mawqūf is more authentic."

Al-Dāraquṭnī is right: the hadith is indeed mawqūf (from Ibn ʿUmar), not marfūʿ (from the Prophet). But he does not see that **even the mawqūf has evolved**: between the observed act (stage 1) and the prescriptive statement (stage 3), the content has transformed.

### Stage 4 — the prophetic hadith (marfūʿ)

The same content reappears — this time attributed to the Prophet — through an entirely different circuit: **[[Persons/Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ|Suhayl]] --> Abū Ṣāliḥ --> Abū Hurayra**.

Tirmidhī no. 993 ([p. 1726](https://shamela.ws/book/1435/1726)):

> ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz ibn al-Mukhtār --> **Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ** --> **Abū Ṣāliḥ** --> **Abū Hurayra** --> the Prophet: **« من غسله الغسل ومن حمله الوضوء »**

Abū Dāwud no. 3162 ([p. 4352](https://shamela.ws/book/1726/4352)) adds a detail: Sufyān transmits the same chain **with** an additional intermediary — **Isḥāq mawlā Zāʾida** — between Abū Ṣāliḥ and Abū Hurayra. The version without Isḥāq (Tirmidhī) has shortened the chain.

The critiques are devastating:

| Critic | Verdict |
|---|---|
| **Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal + ʿAlī ibn al-Madīnī** | **لا يصح في الباب شيء** — "Nothing is established in this chapter" |
| **Al-Bukhārī** | The mawqūf is correct |
| **Al-Bayhaqī** | الروايات المرفوعة غير قوية — "The marfūʿ versions are not strong. **The correct one is the mawqūf of Abū Hurayra**" |
| **Al-Shāfiʿī** | "If it is authentic, I will make it my opinion" — an admission that he is not convinced |
| **Al-Dhuhlī** | "I know of **no established hadith** for this prescription" |

### The complete evolution

```
STAGE 1 — OBSERVED ACT (fiʿl)
Nāfiʿ SEES Ibn ʿUmar embalm Saʿīd ibn Zayd and pray without wuḍūʾ

        ↓ (transformation)

STAGE 2 — COLLECTIVE PRACTICE
"We used to wash the dead; some performed the ghusl, others did not"

        ↓ (prescription)

STAGE 3 — STATEMENT OF IBN ʿUMAR (mawqūf)
"Whoever washes a dead body, let him perform the ghusl; otherwise the wuḍūʾ"
                                                                    ← Al-Dāraquṭnī
        ↓ (channel change)                                           stops here

STAGE 4 — PROPHETIC HADITH (marfūʿ)
Suhayl → Abū Ṣāliḥ → Abū Hurayra → the Prophet
(same circuit as the cases documented by Abū Zurʿa — Q.2022)
```

Al-Dāraquṭnī catches the passage from stage 4 to stage 3 (والموقوف أصح). But he does not see that stage 3 itself is a **construction** from stage 1 — and that the content has **reversed** along the way: Ibn ʿUmar prays without ablutions, then "prescribes" ablutions.

And stage 4 — the elevation to the Prophet — passes through the channel **Suhayl --> Abū Ṣāliḥ --> Abū Hurayra**: exactly the circuit of which Abū Zurʿa says هذا خطأ (Q.2022) and of which Aḥmad + Ibn al-Madīnī say: **nothing is established**.

## Nāfiʿ vs the other transmitters of Ibn ʿUmar

Who is the best in Nāfiʿ? The debate reveals that Mālik's supremacy is **not unanimous**:

| Critic | Best transmitter of Nāfiʿ |
|----------|-------------------------------|
| **Aḥmad** | **ʿUbaydullāh** — the most solid and the most ḥāfiẓ |
| **Aḥmad ibn Ṣāliḥ** | "ʿUbaydullāh is **dearer to me than Mālik** in the hadith of Nāfiʿ" |
| **Yaḥyā al-Qaṭṭān** | ʿUbaydullāh and Ayyūb — **became angry** when Ibn Mahdī said Mālik was more reliable |
| **Al-Nasāʾī** | (1) Mālik, (2) Ayyūb, (3) ʿUbaydullāh |

The Iraqis (Aḥmad, Yaḥyā al-Qaṭṭān) prefer ʿUbaydullāh. The Medinans and al-Nasāʾī prefer Mālik. The "silsilat al-dhahab" therefore rests on a **partisan** choice, not on a consensus.

## Relations

- **Transmits from** Ibn ʿUmar (master/patron), ʿĀʾisha (mursal?), Abū Hurayra
- **Does NOT transmit from** ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb (munqaṭiʿ), ʿUthmān (mursal), Umm Salama (no samāʿ)
- **Transmitted by** Mālik, ʿUbaydullāh ibn ʿUmar, Ayyūb al-Sakhtiyānī, Ibn ʿUyayna, Yaḥyā ibn Saʿīd al-Anṣārī, ʿUmar ibn Nāfiʿ (his son)
- **Diverges from** Sālim ibn ʿAbd Allāh (Ibn ʿUmar's son) in 3-4 hadiths
- **Link to** [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar]] — the fire from Yemen hadith is from Kaʿb, not the Prophet
- **Link to** [[Persons/Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ|Suhayl]] — the ghusl of the dead: mawqūf in Nāfiʿ, marfūʿ in Suhayl --> Abū Ṣāliḥ --> Abū Hurayra (Aḥmad + Ibn al-Madīnī: لا يصح في الباب شيء)

> [!note] Personal comment
>
> Al-Khalīlī says: "no error is known of his". Al-Dāraquṭnī devotes three pages to documenting a case where his source changes five times. Both cannot be right. The difference is that al-Khalīlī's phrase is repeated everywhere, and al-Dāraquṭnī's three pages lie dormant in the ʿIlal.
>
> The jāriya case is all the more telling because the students of Nāfiʿ who transmit these contradictory versions are themselves thiqāt — Mālik, ʿUbaydullāh, Ibn ʿUyayna, Ayyūb. Accusing five reliable transmitters of each having invented a different source is less parsimonious than admitting that Nāfiʿ himself did not remember from whom he had this hadith.
>
> But the most revealing case is that of the three divergences with Sālim. Nāfiʿ says: it is from Kaʿb. Sālim says: it is from the Prophet. The critics side with Nāfiʿ. Which means they know — and implicitly admit — that material from Kaʿb circulates under the Prophet's name. They know it for this particular hadith. But they never ask the question for the others.
