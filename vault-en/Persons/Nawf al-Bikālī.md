# Nawf al-Bikālī

Stepson of [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar]] (ابن امرأة كعب — mentioned 5 times in his entry). [[Concepts/Quṣṣāṣ|Qāṣṣ]] in [[Homs]], imam in [[Damascus]], killed in battle in the service of the Umayyads.

## Relations

- **Stepson of** [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar]] (son of Ka'b's wife)
- **Transmits to** [[Persons/Shahr ibn Ḥawshab]] (next link in the network)
- **Heard from** [[Persons/Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ]], Thawbān, Abū Ayyūb al-Anṣārī
- **Died in the service of** [[Persons/Mu'āwiya ibn Abī Sufyān|the Umayyads]] (army of Muḥammad ibn Marwān)
- **Criticised by** [[Umm al-Dardā']] — reproaches him for not practising what he preaches (Dimashq [p. 28834](https://shamela.ws/book/71/28834))
- The traditions linking him to [['Alī ibn Abī Ṭālib]] are **proto-Shiite fabrications** — chains dismantled by Ibn al-Madīnī (Dimashq [p. 28828](https://shamela.ws/book/71/28828))

## Combined functions

1. **Qāṣṣ in Homs** — the same city as Ka'b. Umm al-Dardā' identifies him as one of the "two qāṣṣīn of Homs" (p. 28834)
2. **Imam of Damascus** — كان نوف البكالي إماما لأهل دمشق (p. 28837)
3. **Umayyad soldier** — killed at the battle of al-Ṭawāna (~83 H / 702 CE) in the army of Muḥammad ibn Marwān, brother of caliph 'Abd al-Malik

## The hadith of the hijra in Nawf's biography

Page 28829 of the biography contains the hadith of the [[Traditions/Hijra to the Shām|hijra to the Shām]] with the chain:

```
Qatāda → Shahr ibn Ḥawshab → Nawf al-Bikālī → Ibn 'Amr → the Prophet
```

This is the **same hadith** as Mustadrak no. 8558, but through the Ka'b network: Shahr (transmitter of Nawf) --> Nawf (Ka'b's stepson) --> Ibn 'Amr (whom Ka'b met in Mecca).

## "He read the Books" — Matthew 7:15 in the Tafsīr

Al-Ṭabarī ([p. 2226](https://shamela.ws/book/7798/2226)), via Sa'īd ibn Abī Hilāl --> al-Quraẓī --> Nawf:

> « عن نوف، **وكان يقرأ الكتب**، قال: إني لأجد صفة ناس من هذه الأمة **في كتاب الله المنزل** : قوم يحتالون الدنيا بالدين... **يلبسون لباس مسوك الضأن وقلوبهم قلوب الذئاب** »

> "Nawf — **and he read the Books** — said: I find the description of people of this community **in the revealed Book of God**: people who deceive the world through religion... **they wear sheepskins but their hearts are the hearts of wolves**."

The image "sheepskins / hearts of wolves" echoes **Matthew 7:15**. By "كتاب الله المنزل" Nawf may be designating the Gospel (which is a "revealed Book of God" in the Islamic theological sense), but the formulation is ambiguous and the content is absorbed into Quranic commentary without any marker of exteriority. Al-Quraẓī then seeks to find this text in the Quran and links it to the verses on hypocrites — a *post hoc* connection. See [[Concepts/Formula - in certain Books|the formula "in certain Books"]].

## Chronology

- Active under Muṣ'ab ibn al-Zubayr in **Kufa** (67-72 H) — presence in Zubayrid territory
- Imam and qāṣṣ in **Damascus** and **Homs**
- Died at **al-Ṭawāna** (~83 H) in an Umayyad military campaign
