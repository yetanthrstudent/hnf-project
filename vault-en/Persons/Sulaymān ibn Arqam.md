# Sulaymān ibn Arqam — the transmitter who invalidates al-Zuhrī

**The textbook case of a unanimously rejected transmitter** whose presence in al-Zuhrī's isnād was sufficient for al-Shāfiʿī to bring down all of his mursals. Basran, mawlā, prolific transmitter of al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī and al-Zuhrī, he produced dozens of hadiths whose authenticity no critic confirms. His interest for the HNF project lies not in his person, but in what he reveals: **if al-Zuhrī transmitted from someone like Sulaymān ibn Arqam, what are his sources worth when he does not name them?**

## The indictment — absolute consensus of rejection

No transmitter in the Sunni heritage is subject to as total a consensus of rejection. Not a single scholar declared him reliable.

### Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal

Via Abū Bakr ibn Abī Khaythama:

> « أبو معاذ الذي روى عنه سفيان الثوري عن الحسن اسمه سليمان بن أرقم ليس بشيء »
>
> "Abū Muʿādh, the one from whom Sufyān al-Thawrī transmitted via al-Ḥasan, is named Sulaymān ibn Arqam: he is worthless."

Via ʿAbd Allāh ibn Aḥmad:

> « لا يسوى حديثه شيئا ولا يروى عنه الحديث »
>
> "His hadith is worth nothing and one does not transmit from him."

And when al-Ājurrī tells Abū Dāwud: "Sulaymān ibn Arqam transmitted from al-Zuhrī, from Anas, on the talbiya," Aḥmad responds:

> « لا نبالي روى أم لم يرو »
>
> "It matters not whether he transmitted or not."

### Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn

Via ʿAbbās al-Dūrī:

> « ليس بشيء ليس يسوى فلسا »
>
> "He is worthless, not worth a fals (copper coin)."

Via ʿUthmān ibn Saʿīd (al-Dārimī):

> « ليس بشيء »

### Al-Bukhārī

> « سليمان بن أرقم مولى قريظة أو النضير عن الحسن والزهري تركوه »
>
> "Sulaymān ibn Arqam, mawlā of Qurayẓa or Naḍīr, [transmitted] from al-Ḥasan and al-Zuhrī — **they abandoned him**."

### Al-Nasāʾī

> « سليمان بن أرقم أبو معاذ متروك الحديث »
>
> "matrūk al-ḥadīth." (al-Ḍuʿafāʾ wa-l-Matrūkīn, no. 246)

And in al-Tamyīz:

> « لا يكتب حديثه »
>
> "His hadith is not to be transcribed."

### Abū Ḥātim al-Rāzī

> « سليمان بن أرقم متروك الحديث »
>
> (al-Jarḥ wa-l-Taʿdīl 4/450)

### Abū Zurʿa al-Rāzī

> « بصري ضعيف الحديث ذاهب الحديث »
>
> "Basran, weak in hadith, **hadith lost/worthless**."

### Al-Jūzajānī (Ibrāhīm ibn Yaʿqūb)

> « سليمان بن أرقم ساقط »
>
> "sāqiṭ (disqualified)."

### ʿAmr ibn ʿAlī al-Fallās

> « سليمان بن أرقم ليس بثقة روى أحاديث منكرة يُكنى أبا معاذ »
>
> "He is not reliable, he transmitted munkar (condemnable) hadiths."

### Muḥammad ibn ʿAbd Allāh al-Anṣārī

> « كنا ننهى عن مجالسة سليمان بن أرقم فذكر منه أمرا عظيما »
>
> "We were forbidden, when young, from frequenting Sulaymān ibn Arqam — and he mentioned about him **a terrible thing**."

### Muslim ibn al-Ḥajjāj

In al-Kunā:

> « منكر الحديث »
>
> "munkar al-ḥadīth."

### Al-Tirmidhī

> « وهو ضعيف عند أهل الحديث »
>
> "He is weak according to the people of hadith."

And in another passage:

> « متروك الحديث »

### Al-Dāraquṭnī

> « متروك الحديث »

### Abū Aḥmad al-Ḥākim

> « متروك الحديث »

### Ibn Khirāsh (ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Yūsuf)

> « متروك الحديث »

### Ibn Ḥibbān

> « سكن اليمامة ومولده بالبصرة وكان ممن يقلب الأخبار ويروي عن الثقات الموضوعات »
>
> "He lived in al-Yamāma, born in Basra. He was among those who **invert traditions** (yuqallib al-akhbār) and transmit forgeries (mawḍūʿāt) in the name of reliable transmitters."

### Ibn ʿAdī — the final verdict of the Kāmil

After citing more than twenty hadiths over ten pages (al-Kāmil, [no. 734](https://shamela.ws/book/12579/1804)):

> « ولسليمان بن أرقم غير ما ذكرت من الحديث أحاديث صالحة وعامة ما يرويه لا يتابع عليه »
>
> "Sulaymān ibn Arqam has, beyond what I have mentioned, other acceptable hadiths, but **the majority of what he transmits, no one corroborates**."

### Al-Dhahabī (Mīzān al-Iʿtidāl, [no. 3427](https://shamela.ws/book/1692/863))

After citing several of his hadiths:

> « قلت: له في الكامل نيف وعشرون حديثا »
>
> "He has in the Kāmil some twenty-odd hadiths."

The implicit verdict: not a word in his favor. Al-Dhahabī considers him a settled case.

### Ibn Ḥajar (Taqrīb al-Tahdhīb no. 2471)

Classifies him as **matrūk** (abandoned). His Tahdhīb al-Tahdhīb ([p. 773](https://shamela.ws/book/1293/773)) recapitulates all the condemnations above and adds:

> « ذكره يعقوب بن سفيان في باب من يرغب عن الرواية عنهم »
>
> "Yaʿqūb ibn Sufyān mentioned him in the chapter: 'those from whom one shuns transmitting.'"

## His relationship with al-Zuhrī — the crux of the problem

### The structural fact

The crucial point is not that Sulaymān ibn Arqam transmitted **from** al-Zuhrī — many weak transmitters did. It is that **al-Zuhrī transmitted from him**. Al-Mizzī notes this in the Tahdhīb al-Kamāl ([no. 2491](https://shamela.ws/book/3722/5672)):

> « محمد بن مسلم بن شهاب الزُّهْرِيّ — وهو من شيوخه »
>
> Among those who transmitted from Sulaymān: "Muḥammad ibn Muslim ibn Shihāb al-Zuhrī — **and he is (also) among his teachers**."

This is a bidirectional relationship. Al-Zuhrī was both the teacher **and** the student of Sulaymān ibn Arqam. This means that al-Zuhrī considered him a source worth citing — at least in some cases.

### Al-Khaṭīb al-Baghdādī (al-Sābiq wa-l-Lāḥiq, p. 214)

> « سليمان بن أرقم أبو معاذ البصري: حدث عنه ابن شهاب الزهري وعامر بن سيار الحلبي نحو من مائة وست عشرة سنة »
>
> "Sulaymān ibn Arqam: al-Zuhrī and ʿĀmir ibn Sayyār al-Ḥalabī transmitted from him, with a gap of approximately **116 years** between them."

### The ṣadaqāt hadith — the most famous case

The ṣadaqāt hadith (prescriptions of zakāt) attributed to al-Zuhrī was actually transmitted **by** Sulaymān ibn Arqam from al-Zuhrī. Abū Dāwud refuses to transmit it (Tahdhīb al-Tahdhīb, [p. 773](https://shamela.ws/book/1293/773)):

> « سألت أبا داود عن حديث الصدقات ... قال: لا أحدث به، حدثني أبو هبيرة محمد بن الوليد الدمشقي، قال: قرأت هذا الحديث في أصل يحيى بن حمزة عن سليمان بن أرقم عن الزهري »
>
> "I asked Abū Dāwud about the ṣadaqāt hadith... He said: 'I do not transmit it. Abū Hubayra told me he had read this hadith in the original manuscript (aṣl) of Yaḥyā ibn Ḥamza: **from Sulaymān ibn Arqam, from al-Zuhrī.**'"

This hadith sometimes circulated under the name "Sulaymān ibn Dāwud" but Abū Zurʿa clarified:

> « صوابه: سليمان بن أرقم »
>
> "The correct version is: Sulaymān ibn Arqam."

### The hadiths he transmitted from al-Zuhrī

Ibn ʿAdī cites in the Kāmil (pp. 1804-1814) some twenty hadiths transmitted by Sulaymān from al-Zuhrī, including:

1. **Zuhrī > Saʿīd ibn al-Musayyab > Abū Hurayra**: "The Jews envy you three things: the salām, prayer, and the āmīn"
2. **Zuhrī > Saʿīd > Abū Hurayra**: "No marriage without a walī and two witnesses"
3. **Zuhrī > ʿUbayd Allāh > Ibn ʿAbbās**: the Prophet's wuḍūʾ, once only
4. **Zuhrī > ʿUrwa > ʿĀʾisha**: the siwāk and the mirror of the Prophet
5. **Zuhrī > ʿUrwa > ʿĀʾisha**: the cloth for drying after wuḍūʾ
6. **Zuhrī > Saʿīd and al-Aʿraj > Abū Hurayra**: "Whoever wishes to taste the sweetness of faith, let him wear wool"
7. **Zuhrī > Abū Salama > Abū Hurayra**: "No qiṣāṣ except by the sword"
8. **Zuhrī > ʿUbayd Allāh and Saʿīd > Ibn ʿAbbās**: the Prophet took a different path returning from the ʿĪd
9. **Zuhrī > Mālik ibn Aws > ʿUmar, al-Zubayr, Ṭalḥa**: the division of spoils (fay')
10. **Zuhrī > Abū Salama > Ibn ʿAbbās**: day of ḥijāma

And a remarkable hadith where **al-Zuhrī transmits from Sulaymān** (not the reverse):

> « أخبرنا الشافعي أنا عبد المجيد عن ابن جريج عن الزهري عن سليمان بن أرقم عن الحسن أن عائشة وحفصة أصبحتا صائمتين »
>
> Al-Shāfiʿī < ʿAbd al-Majīd < Ibn Jurayj < **al-Zuhrī < Sulaymān ibn Arqam** < al-Ḥasan: "ʿĀʾisha and Ḥafṣa found themselves fasting in the morning."

## Al-Shāfiʿī's citation — the decisive argument against al-Zuhrī's mursals

This is **the** reason why Sulaymān ibn Arqam matters to the HNF project. Al-Shāfiʿī uses him as proof that al-Zuhrī's mursals are worthless.

### The text (al-Bayhaqī, al-Madkhal ilā l-Sunan al-Kubrā, [no. 868](https://shamela.ws/book/39268/486)):

> أخبرنا أبو عبد الله الحافظ أخبرني أبو أحمد ابن أبي الحسن أخبرنا عبد الرحمن بن محمد يعني ابن إدريس الرازي أخبرني أبي حدثنا أحمد ابن أبي سُريج قال: سمعت **الشافعي** يقول:
>
> **يقولون: نُحابي! ولو حابينا لحابينا الزهري، وإرسالُ الزهري ليس بشيء، وذاك أنا نجدُه يروي عن سليمان بن أرقم.**
>
> "They say: 'You show favoritism!' If we showed favoritism, it would be toward al-Zuhrī. But al-Zuhrī's mursal **is worthless** (laysa bi-shayʾ). For we find him transmitting from **Sulaymān ibn Arqam**."

### Yaḥyā ibn Saʿīd al-Qaṭṭān's explanation (al-Madkhal, no. 869):

> « مرسل الزهري شر من مرسل غيره لأنه حافظ وكل ما قدر أن يسمي سمى وإنما يترك من لا يستجيز أن يسميه »
>
> "Al-Zuhrī's mursal is **worse** than that of others, for he is a ḥāfiẓ: every time he can name [his source], he does. He only leaves anonymous the one **he cannot permit himself to name**."

### Al-Dhahabī's synthesis (Siyar Aʿlām al-Nubalāʾ, [5/339](https://shamela.ws/book/10906/4111)):

> « مراسيل الزهري كالمعضل لأنه يكون قد سقط منه اثنان ولا يسوغ أن نظن به أنه أسقط الصحابي فقط ولو كان عنده عن صحابي لأوضحه ولما عجز عن وصله »
>
> "Al-Zuhrī's mursals are like muʿḍal (where two links are missing), for it is likely that **two** (transmitters) were omitted, and one cannot presume he only omitted the Companion — if he held (the hadith) from a Companion, he would have mentioned him and would not have been unable to complete the chain."

### The syllogism

```
Premise 1: Al-Zuhrī transmits from Sulaymān ibn Arqam (a matrūk)
Premise 2: Al-Zuhrī, being a ḥāfiẓ, names his sources when he can
Premise 3: When al-Zuhrī does not name his source (mursal), it is because
           he cannot permit himself to name it
Conclusion: Al-Zuhrī's mursals potentially hide sources as weak as
            Sulaymān ibn Arqam — or worse
```

## Connection to tafsīr

Ibn Abī Ḥātim reports a tradition from Sulaymān ibn Arqam in his Tafsīr, regarding the verse "when you have eaten, disperse" (al-Aḥzāb 33:53):

> « نزلت هذه في الثقلاء، ومن ثم قيل: هي آية الثقلاء »
>
> "This verse was revealed concerning the bothersome — hence the name *āyat al-thuqalāʾ* (the verse of the burdensome)."

## Specifically condemned hadiths (munkar)

Among the munkar hadiths he transmitted that critics flag:

1. **Ḥadīth al-ṣadaqāt** (zakāt prescriptions) — from al-Zuhrī. Refused by Abū Dāwud.
2. **"Lā nadhr fī maʿṣiya wa-kaffāratuhu kaffārat yamīn"** — "No vow in disobedience, and its expiation is that of an oath." Via Yaḥyā ibn Abī Kathīr > Abū Salama > ʿĀʾisha. Ibn ʿAdī: "al-ḥadīth lā yuktab."
3. The hadith of the children of Nūḥ (Sām, Ḥām, Yāfith) — from al-Zuhrī > Saʿīd > the Prophet.
4. The hadith of ḥijāma on Wednesday and Saturday causing leprosy — from al-Zuhrī > Abū Salama > Abū Hurayra.
5. **"Idhā tawaḍḍaʾa aḥadukum fa-lā yaghsilanna asfala rijlayhi bi-yadihi l-yumnā"** — from al-Ḥasan > Abū Hurayra.

Ibn ʿAdī summarizes: most of these hadiths, whether from al-Zuhrī, al-Ḥasan, or Ibn Sīrīn, **no one else transmits them** (lā yutābaʿu ʿalayhi).
