# Mu'āwiya ibn Abī Sufyān

Governor of Syria then 1st Umayyad caliph. **Protector and patron** of the network of [[Concepts/Quṣṣāṣ|quṣṣāṣ]] and [[Concepts/Isrā'īliyyāt|isrā'īliyyāt]].

## Relations with the network

- **Authorises** [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar]] to resume qaṣṣ after being challenged by 'Awf ibn Mālik
- **Certifies** Ka'b as "the most truthful of those who transmit from the Book" (p. 23281)
- **Pays** [[Persons/Abū Hurayra]] — "when he paid him, Abū Hurayra would fall silent" (al-Thiqāt p. 185)
- **Employer** of [[Persons/Dhū'l-Kilā' al-Ḥimyarī]] as military commander
- **Rejoices** at the death of Dhū'l-Kilā' at Ṣiffīn -- *secondary source*

## Structural role

Mu'āwiya is the **political protector** who transforms qaṣṣ into a state institution:
1. He **authorises** Ka'b to preach (legitimation of the qāṣṣ as "ma'mūr")
2. He **certifies** Ka'b publicly (creation of an authority)
3. He **pays** Abū Hurayra (financial control of the tradition)
4. He **commands** Dhū'l-Kilā' (armed wing of the same clan as Ka'b)
