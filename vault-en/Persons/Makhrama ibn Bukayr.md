# Makhrama ibn Bukayr ibn 'Abd Allāh ibn al-Ashaj

Medinan transmitter, son of **Bukayr ibn 'Abd Allāh ibn al-Ashaj**. Makhrama is the bearer of a **book** (kitāb) attributed to his father — a written source that **Mālik ibn Anas** used as a major source for the Muwaṭṭa'. The problem: Makhrama **did not hear from his father**, the book is contaminated by traditions from [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar]], and Mālik accepted it at face value.

## The book, not the samā'

The testimonies converge:

**Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal** (al-Jarḥ wa al-Ta'dīl, [p. 3610](https://shamela.ws/book/2170/3610)):

> هو ثقة، لم يسمع من أبيه شيئا إنما يروي عن كتاب أبيه

> "He is trustworthy, he heard nothing from his father — he merely transmits the book of his father."

**Yaḥyā ibn Ma'īn** (ibid.) — two judgments, the second more severe:

> مخرمة بن بكير يقال إنه وقع إليه كتاب أبيه ولم يسمعه

> "It is said he obtained his father's book but did not hear it."

> مخرمة بن بكير ضعيف، وحديثه عن أبيه كتاب، ولم يسمعه من أبيه

> "Makhrama ibn Bukayr is **weak**, his hadiths from his father are from a book, he did not hear them from his father."

**Mūsā ibn Salama** (ibid.) — the most direct testimony:

> أتيت مخرمة بن بكير فقلت له حدثك أبوك؟ قال: لم أدرك أبي ولكن هذه كتبه

> "I went to see Makhrama and asked him: 'Did your father narrate to you?' He replied: '**I did not meet my father**, but here are his books.'"

**Ḥammād ibn Khālid al-Khayyāṭ** (al-'Ilal of Aḥmad, riwāyat 'Abd Allāh 2/490; al-Tārīkh al-Kabīr of al-Bukhārī 2/173):

> أخرج إلى مخرمة كتب أبيه فقال: هذه كتب أبي لم أسمع منها شيئا

> "Makhrama brought out his father's books and said: '**These are my father's books, I heard nothing from them.**'"

**Ibn al-Mubārak** (al-Marāsīl no. 832; al-'Ilal riwāyat 'Abd Allāh 1/325):

> حدثني مخرمة بن بكير قال: قرأت في كتاب أبي بكير

> "Makhrama narrated to me saying: '**I read in the book of my father Bukayr.**'"

**'Alī ibn al-Madīnī** (al-Kāmil of Ibn 'Adī 6/428):

> لا أظن مخرمة سمع من أبيه كتاب سليمان، لعله سمع منه الشيء اليسير، ولم أجد بالمدينة من يخبرني عن مخرمة بن بكير أنه كان يقول في شيء من حديثه سمعت أبي

> "I do not think Makhrama heard from his father the book of Sulaymān [ibn Yasār] — perhaps he heard very little from him. And **I found nobody in Medina** who could affirm to me that Makhrama would say 'I heard my father' for any of his hadiths."

## Makhrama's oath — and Mālik's naivety

On the back of Mālik's book, Ismā'īl ibn Abī Uways found this note (al-Jarḥ wa al-Ta'dīl, [p. 3610](https://shamela.ws/book/2170/3610)):

> سألت مخرمة عما يحدث به عن أبيه سمعها من أبيه؟ فحلف لي وقال: ورب هذه البنية — يعني المسجد — سمعت من أبي

> "I asked Makhrama whether what he narrates from his father, he heard from his father. He swore to me and said: '**By the Lord of this little building**' — meaning the mosque — 'I heard from my father.'"

The word **بُنَيَّة** (bunayya) is an **ism al-taṣghīr** (diminutive) — he literally says "this *little* building" when speaking of the mosque.

Mālik **took at face value** a man of whom Mūsā ibn Salama reports that he himself admitted not having met his father. Instead of a scientific verification, an oath — the same type of naivety that Mālik showed with 'Abd al-Karīm ibn Abī al-Mukhāriq, the other transmitter whose hadith is abandoned (al-Nasā'ī, [p. 202](https://shamela.ws/book/5950/202)).

## Makhrama in the Muwaṭṭa' — Mālik's two methods

**Ismā'īl ibn Abī Uways** confirms (ibid.): when Mālik says **« حدثني الثقة »** ("a trustworthy man narrated to me"), he means **Makhrama ibn Bukayr**.

**Al-Nasā'ī** goes further (al-Ḥākim/Dāraquṭnī, [p. 202](https://shamela.ws/book/5950/202)):

> الذي في موطأ مالك أنه عن القاسم وسالم وابن شهاب يشبه أحاديث مخرمة بن بكير

> "What is in Mālik's Muwaṭṭa' attributed to al-Qāsim, Sālim and Ibn Shihāb [al-Zuhrī] **resembles the hadiths of Makhrama ibn Bukayr**."

Furthermore, according to the Tahdhīb al-Kamāl: **everything Mālik reports "from Sulaymān ibn Yasār" comes from Makhrama's book**.

Mālik's three sources are: **books** (including Makhrama's), **Medinans**, and **outsiders**. Makhrama feeds the first category. Mālik uses him in **two ways**:
1. **Without naming him** — he attributes to al-Qāsim, Sālim or al-Zuhrī what actually comes from Makhrama's book
2. **By saying "al-thiqa"** — a formula that masks the transmitter's identity

Al-'Uqaylī (al-Ḍu'afā', [p. 2941](https://shamela.ws/book/13041/2941)) illustrates this for the gold hadith:

> ومالك يرويه في الموطأ أنه بلغه عن مالك بن أبي عامر، عن عثمان، **ولعله أخذه عن مخرمة**، ومخرمة يقال لم يسمع من أبيه شيئا

> "Mālik reports it in the Muwaṭṭa' saying 'it reached me from Mālik ibn Abī 'Āmir, from 'Uthmān' — **and he may have taken it from Makhrama**, and it is said that Makhrama heard nothing from his father."

## The extent of the contamination — the numbers

Makhrama's book feeds both canonical collections:

- **17 hadiths** in Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim pass through the chain Makhrama <-- Bukayr, all via **Ibn Wahb** (study from the University of Sharjah, *Aḥādīth Makhrama ibn Bukayr 'an abī-hi fī Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim*)
- **10** of these 17 are also in al-Bukhārī (muttafaq 'alayh)
- **13 hadiths** in the Muwaṭṭa' use the formula **"ḥaddathanī al-thiqa"**, of which Makhrama is identified in **8 cases** (study by Muṣṭafā Maḥmūd, *Qawl al-Imām Mālik fī Muwaṭṭa'i-hi*)

Muslim practised **tabyīḍ** (whitening): where Mālik said "balaghani" (it reached me) or "al-thiqa narrated to me" (incomplete chain), Muslim provided the **complete chain** via Ibn Wahb <-- Makhrama <-- Bukayr <-- [the source]. The result: Mālik's vague chains acquire an appearance of continuity through the same book whose audition is contested.

## The contaminated book — traditions from Ka'b al-Aḥbār

Bukayr's book (transmitted by Makhrama) contains three categories of material:
1. **Traditions from [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar]]** transformed into prophetic hadiths
2. **Political opinions**
3. **Genuine hadiths** actually transmitted by Bukayr

### Case 1: "The delegations of Allāh are three"

Al-'Ilal of Dāraquṭnī (no. 1913, [p. 3389](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/3389)):

> رواه **بكير بن عبد الله بن الأشج**، عن سهيل، عن أبيه، عن **أبي هريرة**، تفرد به عنه ابنه **مخرمة بن بكير**.
> وخالفه روح بن القاسم، وسليمان بن بلال، وعبد العزيز بن المختار، والدراوردي، وابن أبي حازم، ووهيب بن خالد رووه عن سهيل، عن أبيه، عن **مرداس الجندعي**، عن **كعب الأحبار** قوله، **وهو الصحيح**.

> "Bukayr ibn 'Abd Allāh ibn al-Ashaj reported it from Suhail, from his father, from **Abū Hurayra** — only his son **Makhrama** transmitted it from him.
> But **six transmitters** (Rawḥ ibn al-Qāsim, Sulaymān ibn Bilāl, 'Abd al-'Azīz ibn al-Mukhtār, al-Darāwardī, Ibn Abī Ḥāzim and Wuhayb ibn Khālid) reported it from Suhail, from his father, from **Mirdās al-Junda'ī**, from **Ka'b al-Aḥbār** as his own words — **and this is the correct version**."

The transformation before our eyes: **six transmitters** say Suhail <-- his father <-- Mirdās <-- **Ka'b al-Aḥbār** (Ka'b's own words). **One alone** (Makhrama, via his father's book) says Suhail <-- his father <-- **Abū Hurayra** <-- **the Prophet**. Makhrama's book has converted a saying of Ka'b into a prophetic hadith.

Despite Dāraquṭnī's verdict, **Ibn Khuzayma** (Ṣaḥīḥ no. 2511) and **Ibn Ḥibbān** (Ṣaḥīḥ no. 3692) included the prophetic version in their collections — implicit authentication —, and **al-Albānī** declared it ṣaḥīḥ (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Jāmi' no. 7112), without engaging with the 'illa documented by Dāraquṭnī.

### Case 2: "Prayer is like a river"

Al-Mustadrak of al-Ḥākim (no. 718, [p. 714](https://shamela.ws/book/2266/714)):

> مخرمة بن بكير، عن أبيه، عن عامر بن سعد بن أبي وقاص… إنما مثل الصلاة كمثل **نهر جار** ببابِ رجل… يقتحم فيه كل يوم **خمس مرات** فماذا ترون يبقى من درنه؟

> "Makhrama ibn Bukayr, from his father, from 'Āmir ibn Sa'd ibn Abī Waqqāṣ...: 'Prayer is like a **flowing river** at a man's door... in which he bathes **five times** a day. What do you think remains of his filth?'"

Al-Ḥākim himself notes the problem:

> فإنهما لم يخرجا مخرمة بن بكير **والعلة فيه** أن طائفة من أهل مصر ذكروا أنه **لم يسمع من أبيه** لصغر سنه

> "Bukhārī and Muslim did not include Makhrama, **and the reason** is that a group of Egyptians mentioned that he **did not hear from his father** due to his young age."

Yet in Tafsīr al-Ṭabarī (vol. 15, [p. 8671](https://shamela.ws/book/43/8671)), the **same tradition** appears attributed directly to **Ka'b al-Aḥbār**:

> حدثنا كعب في هذا المسجد، قال: والذي نفس كعب بيده، إن **الصلوات الخمس** لهن الحسنات التي يذهبن السيئات، **كما يغسل الماء الدرن**

> "Ka'b narrated to us in this mosque: 'By the One in whose hand is Ka'b's soul, the **five prayers** have good deeds that erase sins, **as water washes away filth**.'"

Same image (the river/water), same structure (five prayers, filth washed away), same concept — but with Ka'b it is **his own words**, and in Makhrama's book it has become a **prophetic hadith**.

Al-Ḥākim himself declared this chain **ṣaḥīḥ al-isnād** (Mustadrak no. 718) while acknowledging in the same passage that Bukhārī and Muslim had excluded Makhrama precisely because he had not heard from his father.

This river hadith is reported in prophetic form through the usual trio of Companions: **Abū Hurayra**, **Salmān al-Fārisī** and **Abū al-Dardā'** — three different channels for the same concept originating from Ka'b.

## Relations

- **Son of** Bukayr ibn 'Abd Allāh ibn al-Ashaj — whose book he transmits without having heard it
- **Hidden source of** [[Persons/Mālik ibn Anas]] — identified as "al-thiqa" in the Muwaṭṭa'
- **Transmitted by** Ibn Lahī'a, Ibn al-Mubārak, Ibn Wahb, Ma'n ibn 'Īsā, al-Qa'nabī
- **The book contains material from** [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar]] — transformed into prophetic hadiths

## The Mu'jam al-Mudallisīn — the author's conclusion

Entry no. 153 of the Mu'jam al-Mudallisīn ([p. 426-430](https://shamela.ws/book/794/426)) compiles the testimonies and concludes:

> والذي يظهر لي أن مخرمة بن بكير لم يسمع من أبيه شيئا، وحديثه عن أبيه من كتاب وجده لم يسمعه منه، فلا يصح وصف مخرمة بالتدليس عن أبيه، إنما يقال أنه أرسل عن أبيه

> "What seems clear to me is that Makhrama **heard nothing from his father**, and his hadiths from his father come from a found book that he did not hear from him. It is not correct to describe Makhrama as a mudallis regarding his father — one must say he performed **irsāl** (discontinuous transmission) from his father."

The text also notes al-Mu'allimī's comment on Abū Ḥātim's position: **« وجادة، فإذا احتيج إليها لم تغن، وإن أغنت لم يحتج إليها »** — "A wijāda: when you need it, it is insufficient, and when it suffices, you do not need it."

## Summary

Makhrama is the **written vector** through which traditions of Ka'b al-Aḥbār penetrated Mālik's Muwaṭṭa'. The mechanism: an inherited book (not heard) whose content mixes three types of material. Mālik, instead of verifying the medium, believed Makhrama's oath. Al-Nasā'ī identifies the contamination: what passes in the Muwaṭṭa' under the names of al-Qāsim, Sālim and al-Zuhrī "resembles the hadiths of Makhrama". The "wufd Allāh" case from Dāraquṭnī shows the transformation in action: six transmitters attribute the hadith to Ka'b, one alone (Makhrama via the book) attributes it to the Prophet — and it is Ka'b's version that is declared correct.
