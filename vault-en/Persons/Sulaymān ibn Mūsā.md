# Sulaymān ibn Mūsā

**Muftī of Damascus**, student of [[Persons/Makhūl al-Shāmī|Makhūl al-Shāmī]]. Important juridical figure of Umayyad Syria.

## Relationship with Makhūl

He once came to Makhūl with a **ṣaḥīfa** he had memorized entirely. Makhūl, impressed, said: « أتعجب ما سمعت شيئا قط فاستودعته صدري إلا وجدته حين أريد » — "You find this surprising? Everything I have ever heard, I have kept in my heart and find it when I want." (Tārīkh Dimashq p. 27811)

## The four regional scholars

It is Sulaymān who formulates the ranking of regional authorities (p. 27811-27812):

> "When knowledge comes from the Ḥijāz according to **al-Zuhrī**, we accept it. From Iraq according to **al-Ḥasan**, we accept it. From the Jazīra according to **[[Persons/Maymūn ibn Mahrān|Maymūn ibn Mahrān]]**, we accept it. From the Shām according to **[[Persons/Makhūl al-Shāmī|Makhūl]]**, we accept it."

## [[Concepts/Tafarrud|Tafarrud]]: the hadith of marriage without a walī

Sulaymān is the **only** one to report from al-Zuhrī, from 'Ā'isha, the hadith that "**any marriage without a guardian (walī) is invalid**" (أيما امرأة نكحت بغير إذن وليها فنكاحها باطل). This hadith, which became a pillar of matrimonial law, rests on a **tafarrud** by Sulaymān — a Syrian who is the only one to report from a Medinan (al-Zuhrī), the same pattern as the tafarrud of [[Persons/Al-Awzā'ī|Al-Awzā'ī]] for the Kharijites hadith.

Chain: Ibn Jurayj ← **Sulaymān ibn Mūsā** ← al-Zuhrī ← [[Persons/ʿUrwa ibn al-Zubayr|ʿUrwa]] ← ʿĀʾisha
