# ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār — the golden chain and the hidden mursal

**One of the most famous chains in hadith**: Mālik → ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār → Ibn ʿUmar → the Prophet. Considered **thiqa** by all critics, ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār is the pivot of a Medinan channel reputed to be unassailable. But al-Dāraquṭnī, in question 3086 of the ʿIlal, reveals a flaw: when ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz al-Mājishūn reports the same hadith from ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār, the source is not Ibn ʿUmar — it is **[[Persons/al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī|al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī]], in mursal**. And al-Dāraquṭnī rules: **والمرسل أشبه** — "the mursal is more likely."

## Profile

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| Full name | ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār al-ʿAdawī al-Madanī, Abū ʿAbd al-Raḥmān |
| Death | ~127 H in Medina |
| Status | Mawlā of ʿAbd Allāh ibn ʿUmar — lived in his house |
| Downstream transmitters | **Mālik ibn Anas** (golden chain), Sufyān al-Thawrī, Sufyān ibn ʿUyayna, Shuʿba, Yazīd ibn al-Hād, Yaḥyā ibn Saʿīd al-Anṣārī, ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz al-Mājishūn |
| Classical evaluation | Thiqa unanimously (Ibn Ḥajar, Ibn Maʿīn, al-Nasāʾī, Abū Zurʿa) |

## The golden chain — Mālik → Ibn Dīnār → Ibn ʿUmar

The chain **Mālik ← ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār ← Ibn ʿUmar** is the archetype of the short Medinan chain: three links, all Medinan, all thiqa. It is one of the **silsilāt al-dhahab** (golden chains) of hadith literature. When Mālik transmits from ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār, nobody questions it.

But the question is not Mālik's reliability. It is what ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār was **actually** receiving. If his source for certain hadiths is not Ibn ʿUmar but **al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī**, then the golden chain actually connects to a Basran transmitter whose **massive irsāl** is documented (Ibn Ḥajar: « كان يرسل كثيراً ويدلس ») — not to the Companion.

## The ʿilla 3086 — al-Dāraquṭnī rules

The hadith "yā maʿshar al-nisāʾ, taṣaddaqna..." (fitnat al-nisāʾ) is in Sunan Ibn Mājah (no. 4003, [ed. Arnaʾūṭ](https://shamela.ws/book/98138/2772)) through the chain:

```
Muḥammad ibn Rumḥ → al-Layth ibn Saʿd → Ibn al-Hād → ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār → Ibn ʿUmar → the Prophet
```

Al-Arnaʾūṭ judges the isnād **ṣaḥīḥ**. But al-Dāraquṭnī (ʿIlal, question 3086, [p. 5044](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/5044)) dissects this hadith and exposes the ʿilla:

> وسئل عن حديث عبد الله بن دينار، عن ابن عمر: قال رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم يا معشر النساء تصدقن، وأكثرن الاستغفار ...
>
> فقال: يرويه يزيد بن الهاد، ويحيى بن سعيد الأنصاري، عن عبد الله بن دينار، عن ابن عمر.
>
> **ورواه عبد العزيز بن أبي سلمة الماجشون، عن عبد الله بن دينار، عن الحسن مرسلا.**
>
> **والمرسل أشبه.**

Two versions of the same hadith pass through ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār:

| Version | Chain | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Musnad** | Ibn al-Hād / Yaḥyā → Ibn Dīnār → **Ibn ʿUmar** → the Prophet | Apparently ṣaḥīḥ |
| **Mursal** | al-Mājishūn → Ibn Dīnār → **al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī** → the Prophet | **والمرسل أشبه** |

Al-Dāraquṭnī adds a detail about manuscript contamination:

> **وقيل إن إبراهيم بن صدقة انقلبت عليه نسخة يزيد بن الهاد، فرواه عن يحيى بن سعيد، والله أعلم.**
>
> « It has been said that Ibrāhīm ibn Ṣadaqa had **his copy of [the nuskha of] Yazīd ibn al-Hād flip on him** (inqalabat ʿalayhi): he then transmitted it from Yaḥyā ibn Saʿīd [al-Anṣārī, instead of Ibn al-Hād]. God knows best. »

The verb **inqalaba** (انقلب) is a technical term: the nuskha "flips," meaning the transmitter names in the manuscript copy get mixed up. Ibrāhīm possessed a manuscript containing hadiths from Ibn al-Hād; the names got confused and he attributed the material to Yaḥyā ibn Saʿīd — another transmitter of Ibn Dīnār. Manuscript corruption adds to the attribution error: not only is the actual source (al-Ḥasan) hidden behind Ibn ʿUmar, but the intermediate transmitters themselves permute under the effect of flipped nusakh.

## The warning signal — Ibn al-Hād without Mālik

The ʿilla 3086 presents a characteristic signal. The chain passes through **Ibn al-Hād** (Yazīd ibn ʿAbd Allāh ibn Usāma ibn al-Hād al-Laythī) and **not** through Mālik. This is a clue: when a hadith from ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār appears through **secondary** channels — without Mālik or the other major transmitters reporting it — something is amiss. The hadith probably does not come from Ibn ʿUmar (whom Mālik knows perfectly through Ibn Dīnār), but from a **different source** that Ibn Dīnār possessed and that less vigilant transmitters misattributed.

Diagnostic rule: if ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār and Mālik is not among the reporters — look for al-Ḥasan.

## Ittibāʿ al-majarra — the reflex of the famous chain

The case is documented independently by al-Ḥākim (see [[Concepts/Ittibāʿ al-Majarra#Case 3]]). Al-Mundhir ibn ʿAbd Allāh sees the transmitter ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz ibn Abī Salama and automatically connects him to the chain **ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār → Ibn ʿUmar**, when the actual chain (longer, ending at ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib) passes through different intermediaries. Al-Ḥākim diagnoses:

> **والمنذر بن عبد الله أخذ طريق المجرة فيه**
>
> « Al-Mundhir ibn ʿAbd Allāh took the path of the majarra. »

The name "ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār" functions as an **attractor** toward the famous combination — "Ibn Dīnār = Ibn ʿUmar." This is the essence of [[Concepts/Ittibāʿ al-Majarra|ittibāʿ al-majarra]]: memory is not neutral, it is oriented by familiarity. The famous chain absorbs the material of the actual chain.

## Ibn Dīnār, shared node — proof from the ʿIlal

Research in the ʿilal books reveals an unexpected fact: **ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār does not only transmit from Ibn ʿUmar**. He also transmits from **Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān** — the father of [[Persons/Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ|Suhayl]]. And **Suhayl transmits from ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār**. The two networks (Ibn ʿUmar and Abū Hurayra) are not parallel — they share a common node.

### Q.1507 — Suhayl transmits from Ibn Dīnār

The hadith "al-Īmān bidʿ wa-sabʿūn shuʿba" (faith has seventy-odd branches). Al-Dāraquṭnī (ʿIlal, Q.1507, [pp. 2624-2626](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/2624)) identifies a massive ikhtilāf on Suhayl's isnād:

- **Ḥammād ibn Salama, Jarīr, Zuhayr, Abū ʿAwāna, al-Thawrī** and others: Suhayl → **ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār** → Abū Ṣāliḥ → Abū Hurayra
- **Maʿmar, Wuhayb** and others: Suhayl → Abū Ṣāliḥ → Abū Hurayra (without Ibn Dīnār)

Al-Dāraquṭnī rules:

> **والصحيح قول من قال: عن سهيل، عن عبد الله بن دينار، عن أبي صالح، عن أبي هريرة**
>
> « The correct version is that of those who say: from Suhayl, **from ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār**, from Abū Ṣāliḥ, from Abū Hurayra. »

ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār is a **shared link** between the two networks. Those who omitted him (Maʿmar, Wuhayb) committed a wahm.

### Q.2813/Q.1894 — the same hadith, two Companions

The hadith of the date given in charity (ʿIlal Q.2813, [p. 4684](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/4684)) appears through **both chains simultaneously**:

| Chain | Transmitter | Companion | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mūsā ibn ʿUbayda → Ibn Dīnār | **Ibn ʿUmar** | marfūʿ | Mūsā = ḍaʿīf |
| Abū Jaʿfar al-Rāzī → Ibn Dīnār → his father → Abū Ṣāliḥ | **Abū Hurayra** | marfūʿ | wahm |
| Warqāʾ → Ibn Dīnār → Saʿīd ibn Yasār | **Abū Hurayra** | marfūʿ | **maḥfūẓ** |

ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār transmits the **same matn** sometimes "from Ibn ʿUmar" sometimes "from Abū Ṣāliḥ from Abū Hurayra" — the profile of a transmitter who received the hadith from a **single source** and distributes it under multiple isnāds.

### Q.1525 — Mālik corrects his own transmitter

The hadith "a man utters a word without paying attention to it" (Q.1525, [p. 2647](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/2647)). ʿAbd al-Raḥmān, **the son** of ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār, transmits from his father → Abū Ṣāliḥ → Abū Hurayra (**marfūʿ**). But **Mālik** transmits the same hadith from the same Ibn Dīnār → Abū Ṣāliḥ → Abū Hurayra — **mawqūf**.

> **وخالفه مالك بن أنس رواه، عن عبد الله بن دينار، عن أبي صالح، عن أبي هريرة موقوفا، وهو المحفوظ**
>
> « Mālik contradicted him: he transmitted it mawqūf, and that is **al-maḥfūẓ** (the preserved). »

Mālik, the most reliable transmitter of Ibn Dīnār, **refuses to elevate to the Prophet** what Ibn Dīnār's son elevates. The material is the same; it is the **attribution** that varies — a classic sign of content whose status (prophetic or not) is not fixed at the source.

## The Suhayl section — Abū Ṣāliḥ → al-Salūlī → Kaʿb

The ʿilal books document a second circuit, parallel to the first: behind the chain **Suhayl → Abū Ṣāliḥ → Abū Hurayra** often lies **Suhayl → Abū Ṣāliḥ → ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra al-Salūlī → Kaʿb al-Aḥbār** (mawqūf). The material is **isrāʾīliyyāt**, not prophetic hadiths.

### Q.2022 (Ibn Abī Ḥātim) — Abū Zurʿa corrects: "this is an error"

ʿIlal Ibn Abī Ḥātim, Q.2022 ([p. 3002](https://shamela.ws/book/1350/3002)). The hadith "man kabbara wāḥida kutibat lahu ʿishrūn...":

> سألتُ أبا زرعة عن حديث رواه عبد الرحمن بن أبي الرجال، عن سهيل بن أبي صالح، عن أبيه، عن أبي هريرة ... قال أبو زرعة: **هذا خطأ؛ إنما هو: سهيل، عن أبيه، عن السلولي، عن كعب؛ قوله.**
>
> « I asked Abū Zurʿa [...]. Abū Zurʿa said: **This is an error (khaṭaʾ). The correct [chain] is: Suhayl, from his father, from al-Salūlī, from Kaʿb — his own words (qawluhu).** »

Al-Salūlī = **ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra al-Salūlī** (explicitly identified in al-Tamhīd of Ibn ʿAbd al-Barr, [p. 1833](https://shamela.ws/book/1719/1833)). The hadith is not from Abū Hurayra, not from the Prophet — it is a saying of **[[Ka'b al-Ahbar|Kaʿb al-Aḥbār]]**.

### Q.453 (Suʾālāt al-Bardhaʿī) — same correction

Suʾālāt al-Bardhaʿī, Q.453 ([p. 449](https://shamela.ws/book/812/449)). Another hadith attributed to Suhayl → Abū Ṣāliḥ → Abū Hurayra:

> **وإنما هو عن سهيل، عن أبيه، عن السلولي، عن كعب.**
>
> « The correct [chain] is: Suhayl, from his father, **from al-Salūlī, from Kaʿb.** »

### Q.1979 (ʿIlal al-Dāraquṭnī) — al-Dāraquṭnī confirms

ʿIlal Q.1979 ([p. 3490](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/3490)). The hadith on the takbīr during Ḥajj:

> **وخالفه سليمان بن بلال، رواه، عن سهيل، عن أبيه، عن مرداس، عن كعب، وهو الصحيح.**
>
> « Sulaymān ibn Bilāl contradicted him: he transmitted it from Suhayl, from his father, **from Mirdās, from Kaʿb — and that is the ṣaḥīḥ.** »

Mirdās is a transmitter from the same Salūl sphere as ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra. The material systematically traces back to **Kaʿb**.

### Q.1968 — ʿAlī ibn al-Madīnī prefers al-Ḥasan's mursal

ʿIlal Q.1968 ([p. 3474](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/3474)). The hadith "al-Imām ḍāmin wa-l-muʾadhdhin muʾtaman." Al-Dāraquṭnī notes the hadith is **muḍṭarib** (unstable) via the chain Abū Ṣāliḥ → Abū Hurayra. Then:

> **وزعم علي بن المديني أن حديث يونس، عن الحسن مرسلا، عن النبي صلى الله عليه وسلم بذلك أحبها إليه، وأحسنها إسنادا.**
>
> « **ʿAlī ibn al-Madīnī** stated that the hadith of Yūnus, **from al-Ḥasan, mursal**, from the Prophet, is **the version he prefers and the one with the best isnād.** »

The greatest isnād critic of his era says explicitly: for this hadith also transmitted via Abū Ṣāliḥ → Abū Hurayra (marfūʿ), the version from **[[Persons/al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī|al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī]] mursal** is superior.

### Who is ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra al-Salūlī?

Taqrīb al-Tahdhīb, no. 3396 ([p. 234](https://shamela.ws/book/8609/234)); Tahdhīb al-Kamāl, no. 3345 ([p. 7614](https://shamela.ws/book/3722/7614)):

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| Name | ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra al-Salūlī |
| Ṭabaqa | 3rd (major tābiʿīn) |
| Evaluation | Thiqa (al-ʿIjlī); mentioned in Ibn Ḥibbān's Thiqāt |
| **Transmits from** | **Kaʿb al-Aḥbār**, Abū Hurayra, Abū al-Dardāʾ |
| **Transmitted by** | **Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān** (Suhayl's father!), ʿAṭāʾ ibn Qurra, Mujāhid, Abū al-Zubayr |
| Brother | ʿĀṣim ibn Ḍamra al-Salūlī (uncertain according to al-Bukhārī) |

The link is direct in the rijāl: **Abū Ṣāliḥ transmits from ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra** (Tahdhīb al-Kamāl). When Suhayl says "my father, from Abū Hurayra," he sometimes shortens the chain which, according to Abū Zurʿa, is actually "my father, from al-Salūlī, from **Kaʿb**."

## The circuit — two sections, one convergence

```
         SECTION A (Ibn ʿUmar side)              SECTION B (Abū Hurayra side)

         AL-ḤASAN AL-BAṢRĪ (mursal)             KAʿB AL-AḤBĀR (isrāʾīliyyāt)
                   │                                       │
              [mursal]                            ʿABD ALLĀH IBN ḌAMRA
                   │                               al-Salūlī (thiqa)
                   │                                       │
         ʿABD ALLĀH IBN DĪNĀR ◄──────────────► ABŪ ṢĀLIḤ AL-SAMMĀN
          (Medina, d. ~127 H)                    (father of Suhayl)
                   │                                       │
          [majarra → "Ibn ʿUmar"]             [khaṭaʾ → "Abū Hurayra"]
                   │                                       │
          ┌────────┼────────┐                     SUHAYL IBN ABĪ ṢĀLIḤ
          │        │        │                    (ṣaḥīfa + ikhtilāṭ)
        MĀLIK   IBN AL-HĀD  AL-MĀJISHŪN                   │
       (golden   (the ʿilla   (reveals               ┌──────┤
        chain)   appears)    al-Ḥasan)          Mālik   Wuhayb
                                                 (before   (nuskha)
                                                 ikhtilāṭ)
```

Section A is documented by al-Dāraquṭnī (Q.3086: والمرسل أشبه).
Section B is documented by Abū Zurʿa (Q.2022: هذا خطأ) and al-Dāraquṭnī (Q.1979: وهو الصحيح).
The link between the two is ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār himself (Q.1507: Suhayl transmits from him).

**On the surface**: two independent Companions — Ibn ʿUmar and Abū Hurayra — give the same hadith from the Prophet. The plurality of routes (taʿaddud al-ṭuruq) apparently reinforces authenticity.

**In depth**: the Ibn ʿUmar channel leads to al-Ḥasan mursal (Q.3086); the Abū Hurayra channel leads to Kaʿb al-Aḥbār mawqūf (Q.2022). And both channels share a common node — ʿAbd Allāh ibn Dīnār (Q.1507). The plurality is a mirage.

## Relationships

- **Mawlā of** ʿAbd Allāh ibn ʿUmar (d. 73 H)
- **Transmits from** Ibn ʿUmar (partial samāʿ), **Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān** (confirmed by Q.1507, Q.1525, Q.2813), **[[Persons/al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī|al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī]]** (mursal — Q.3086)
- **Transmitted by** Mālik ibn Anas (golden chain), [[Persons/Sufyān al-Thawrī|Sufyān al-Thawrī]], Sufyān ibn ʿUyayna, Shuʿba, Yazīd ibn al-Hād, Yaḥyā ibn Saʿīd al-Anṣārī, ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz al-Mājishūn, **[[Persons/Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ|Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ]]** (Q.1507)
