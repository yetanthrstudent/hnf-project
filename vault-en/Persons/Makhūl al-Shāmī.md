# Makhūl al-Shāmī

**Faqīh of the Shām**, central juridical figure of Umayyad Syria. Mawlā (freedman), of non-Arab origin — a significant fact because after the death of the "'Abdullāhs" (Ibn 'Abbās, Ibn al-Zubayr, Ibn 'Umar, Ibn 'Amr), "fiqh in all countries passed to the mawālī" (p. 27812).

## Status

- **'Abd al-Malik ibn Marwān** asks who the faqīh of the Shām is, and is told: **Makhūl** (p. 27813)
- « مكحول أفقه أهل الشام » — "Makhūl is the most learned of the people of the Shām" (Abū Zur'a, p. 27813)
- He studied under Shurayḥ for **6 months** without asking questions, merely listening to his rulings (p. 27811)

## Relations

- **Teacher of** [[Persons/Al-Awzā'ī]] ("رأيت مكحولا" — Ibn Abī Shayba)
- **Teacher of** [[Persons/Sulaymān ibn Mūsā]] — who came to him with a memorised ṣaḥīfa (p. 27811)
- **Calls [[Persons/Rajā' ibn Ḥaywa]]** "my shaykh": « سل شيخي هذين » (ask these two shaykhs — Rajā' and 'Adī ibn 'Adī, p. 27815)
- **Transmits from** Sa'īd ibn al-Musayyab and al-Sha'bī (p. 27811)
- **Key chain**: [[Persons/Al-Awzā'ī]] <-- **Makhūl** <-- [[Persons/Rajā' ibn Ḥaywa]] <-- [[Persons/Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ|Ibn 'Amr]] (Ṭabarānī, shamela.ws/book/13162/2856)

## Classification of the four regional scholars

[[Persons/Sulaymān ibn Mūsā]] said (p. 27811-27812):

> « إذا جاء العلم من الحجاز عن الزهري قبلناه وإذا جاءنا من العراق عن الحسن قبلناه وإذا جاءنا من الجزيرة عن ميمون بن مهران قبلناه وإذا جاءنا من الشام عن مكحول قبلناه »
>
> "When knowledge comes from the Ḥijāz from al-Zuhrī, we accept it. From Iraq from al-Ḥasan, we accept it. From the Jazīra from Maymūn ibn Mahrān, we accept it. From the Shām from Makhūl, we accept it."

| Region | Reference scholar |
|--------|-------------------|
| Ḥijāz | al-Zuhrī |
| Iraq | al-Ḥasan [al-Baṣrī] |
| Jazīra | [[Persons/Maymūn ibn Mahrān]] |
| **Shām** | **Makhūl** |

Sa'īd [ibn 'Abd al-'Azīz] adds: « فكان هؤلاء الأربعة علماء الناس في خلافة هشام » — "These four were the scholars of the people under the caliphate of Hishām."

## The primacy of the Sunna over the Quran

Al-Awzā'ī transmits from Makhūl (al-Kifāya of al-Khaṭīb al-Baghdādī, [p. 29](https://shamela.ws/book/13055/29)):

> « الْقُرْآنُ أَحْوَجُ إِلَى السُّنَّةِ مِنَ السُّنَّةِ إِلَى الْقُرْآنِ »
>
> "**The Quran needs the Sunna more than the Sunna needs the Quran.**"

Full isnād: Abū Sa'īd al-Ṣayrafī <-- al-Aṣamm <-- al-Ṣaghānī <-- Rawḥ ibn 'Ubāda <-- **al-Awzā'ī** <-- **Makhūl**.

Also reported in al-Sunna of al-Marwazī (no. 104) and Sharḥ Madhāhib Ahl al-Sunna of Ibn Shāhīn.

In the same passage, **Yaḥyā ibn Abī Kathīr** asserts: « السنة قاضية على الكتاب وليس الكتاب قاضيا على السنة » — "The Sunna overrules the Book and the Book does not overrule the Sunna."

This doctrine finds a concrete illustration in the [[Traditions/Prohibition of carnivores|prohibition of carnivore meat]] — a ban with no Quranic basis, transmitted exclusively by Syrian jurists and unknown to the scholars of the Ḥijāz.

It fits within question 4 of the [[Concepts/Kūfa vs the Shām]] dossier: the Shām places the Sunna above the Quran, while the Kūfites ([[Persons/Abū Ḥanīfa|Abū Ḥanīfa]] foremost) reject this hierarchy and posit the Quran as the primary and sufficient source.

## Connection to the Ka'b network

Via the chain Makhūl <-- [[Persons/Rajā' ibn Ḥaywa]] <-- [[Persons/Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ|Ibn 'Amr]], Makhūl is connected to the possessor of the [[Traditions/Zamālatain of Yarmūk|zamālatain]] and to the network of [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar]]. Rajā' ibn Ḥaywa himself transmits from Ka'b (via his son 'Āṣim, shamela.ws/book/329/288).

## Qadarism

[[Persons/Al-Awzā'ī]] defends Makhūl against the accusation of qadarism: « لم يبلغنا أن أحدا من التابعين تكلم في القدر إلا هذين الرجلين: الحسن ومكحولا، فكشفنا عن ذلك، فإذا هو باطل » — "Only al-Ḥasan and Makhūl were said to have spoken about qadar among the Tābi'ūn; we investigated and it is false."
