# Abū Ḥanīfa — the chief of the Kūfites

**Founder of the largest living madhhab.** Of non-Arab origin — Persian from Kābul or Anbār depending on the sources. Dies in prison in Baghdād in 150H for refusing the position of qāḍī offered by the caliph al-Manṣūr — like [[Sufyān al-Thawrī]], he is among those who **refuse** power (see [[Concepts/ʿAmal al-Sulṭān]]).

His position in the [[Concepts/Kūfa contre le Chām|Kūfa vs. al-Shām]] dossier is central: he embodies the systematic rejection of the Sunna of al-Shām and the primacy of the Quran.

## The chief of the Kūfites against the Sunna of al-Shām

Al-Khaṭīb al-Baghdādī (Tārīkh Madīnat al-Salām) is the main source for these anecdotes. Important note: al-Khaṭīb is **hostile** to Abū Ḥanīfa — he reports these accounts to discredit him. But that is precisely what makes them valuable: a doctrinal enemy inadvertently documents the authentic Kūfite position.

### The hadith of obedience — "fables!"

When the hadith on obedience to the ruler (the one Muslim retains under no. 1848) is reported to Abū Ḥanīfa, he rejects it (Khaṭīb, Tārīkh, vol.15 p.532):

> « خرافة ! »
>
> « **Fables!** »

The word is violent: khurāfa, in the mouth of a scholar of the 2nd century, is a total disqualification. What Muslim canonizes, Abū Ḥanīfa treats as a children's tale.

### "Leave us alone with that!"

On the rafʿ al-yadayn (raising the hands) in jihād — a hadith propagated by the shāmis (Khaṭīb, vol.15 p.532):

> « دعنا من هذا ! »
>
> « **Leave us alone with that!** »

### "It's a sin" / "It's nonsense!"

On other hadiths from al-Shām (Khaṭīb, vol.15 p.533):

> « هذا إثم » / « هذا مُحال »
>
> « **It's a sin** » / « **It's nonsense!** »

### "Does he want to fly like a bird?"

On the hadith prescribing raising the hands during the bow in prayer (Khaṭīb, vol.15 p.535):

> « يريد أن يطير ؟ »
>
> « **Does he want to fly like a bird?** »

## The Quran as primary source

Abū Ḥanīfa's position is that of Kūfa (question 4 of the dossier): the Quran is the primary and sufficient source.

> « ما لم يحرّم عليك القرآن فهو حلال »
>
> « What the Quran does not forbid you is permissible. »

And:

> « بيني وبينهم كتاب الله »
>
> « Between me and them lies the Book of God. »

Practical consequence: dietary prohibitions are limited to the Quran strictly. What the Quran does not mention is not forbidden — contrary to the Syrian position that extends prohibitions through the Sunna (see the [[Traditions/Prohibition of carnivores|prohibition of carnivore meat]], a prohibition without Quranic basis, transmitted by Syrian jurists).

## The sources — al-Khaṭīb al-Baghdādī

Al-Khaṭīb is a Baghdādī muḥaddith of the 5th century (d. 463H), author of the monumental Tārīkh Madīnat al-Salām (History of Baghdad). He is **hostile** to Abū Ḥanīfa and the Ḥanafī madhhab — his Tārīkh methodically compiles criticisms against the Kūfite imam.

This is a classic paradox of Islamic historiography: the enemies of a figure are often the best preservers of his positions. Al-Khaṭīb reports Abū Ḥanīfa's words to condemn them — but he preserves them verbatim. The "Leave us alone!" and the "Fables!" have survived the centuries thanks to hostility, not despite it.

## Nabīdh and intoxication — only sukr is ḥarām

Abū Ḥanīfa's position on alcohol extends his doctrine of sources. His master Ibrāhīm al-Nakhaʿī teaches (Abū Yūsuf, Kitāb al-Āthār, vol.1 p.227): the saying "every intoxicant is ḥarām" is an error — only intoxication (sukr) is forbidden, not the drink itself. The Quran forbids khamr (5:90), not nabīdh. Extending the prohibition through hadith means giving the Sunna the power to add to the Quran — exactly what Kūfa refuses. See [[Concepts/Nabīdh, ṭilā', khamr]].

## The legacy — a domesticated madhhab

Abū Ḥanīfa dies in 150H. His school survives — it is the **largest living madhhab**, dominant in Turkey, the Indian subcontinent, Central Asia, and Iraq. But later Ḥanafism absorbed what Abū Ḥanīfa rejected: it accepts hadith, it accepts the Sunna as a source, it integrates into the "Ahl al-Sunna wal-Jamāʿa" system.

Kūfa's victory is a victory of form — the madhhab survives. The substantive victory goes to al-Shām: the epistemological framework (Sunna + Quran, not Quran alone) is that of al-Shām.
