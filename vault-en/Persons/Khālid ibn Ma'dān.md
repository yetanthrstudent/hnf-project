# Khālid ibn Ma'dān al-Kalā'ī

**Ascetic, ribāṭ warrior and chief of police of Yazīd ibn Mu'āwiya**, based in [[Lieux/Homs|Homs]]. Tribe **Kalā'** (Dhū al-Kilā', Ḥimyar). Third ṭabaqa of tābi'ūn of the Shām (Abū Zur'a, Dimashq [p. 7080](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7080)). Claims to have met **70 Companions** — a self-declaration transmitted by his daughter and his servant (Dimashq [p. 7081](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7081)-[7082](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7082), see below). Died at **Anṭarsūs** (a coastal ribāṭ fortress), while fasting (~103-104 H).

## The "70 Companions" — self-legitimation ([[Concepts/Tafarrud|tafarrud]])

The claim that he "met 70 Companions" is a **self-declaration** transmitted exclusively by his family circle:

- **Dimashq [p. 7081](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7081)**: al-Ḥākim cites Abū 'Amr al-Saksakī quoting Khālid: « **لقد لقيت سبعين رجلا من أصحاب النبي** » — it is Khālid himself who says this
- **Dimashq [p. 7082](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7082)**: three isnāds all passing through **Ismā'īl ibn 'Ayyāsh** --> **'Abda bint Khālid** (his daughter) and/or **Umm al-Ḍaḥḥāk** (his servant): « أدركت سبعين رجلا من أصحاب النبي »

No external source confirms these encounters. The Companions **explicitly** attested as his masters (Ibn Abī Ḥātim, p. 7081) are about **ten** (Abū Umāma, al-Miqdām, 'Utba ibn 'Abd, etc.) — far from 70. But even these nominal lists rest on no **independent testimony**: the later critics (al-Bukhārī, Muslim, Ibn Abī Ḥātim, al-Ḥākim) compile them from the existence of isnāds bearing Khālid's name — **circular** reasoning since the question is precisely whether these isnāds are authentic.

The scholars who declare him **ثقة** (al-'Ijlī, Ibn Kharrāsh, Ibn Sa'd) issue a **general** reliability judgment, without ever verifying his encounters with specific Companions. Even al-Awzā'ī, who venerated him, obtained his information about Khālid through **his daughter** 'Abda (Siyar [p. 46046](https://shamela.ws/book/8322/46046)).

## The chief of police of Yazīd

'Īsā ibn Yūnus quoting Thawr ibn Yazīd (Dimashq [p. 7083](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7083)): Khālid ibn Ma'dān was **صاحب شرطة يزيد بن معاوية** — the chief of the police/coercive force of caliph **Yazīd I**, under whose reign occurred the **Karbalā' massacre** (61 H) and the **sack of Medina** (Ḥarra, 63 H).

The sources make no comment on this function. No scholar criticises him for this role.

## The ribāṭ warrior

Ṣafwān ibn 'Amr via Abū Isḥāq al-Fazārī (Dimashq [p. 7085](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7085)):

> « كان خالد بن معدان إذا أمر الناس بالغزو كان فسطاطه أول فسطاط يضرب بدابق »

> "When Khālid ordered people to go on campaign (ghazw), his tent was the **first pitched at Dābiq**."

Dābiq: the great Umayyad military base on the Byzantine frontier, near Aleppo. Khālid himself dies at **Anṭarsūs**, a coastal ribāṭ fortress (Dimashq [p. 7089](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7089)).

Buḥayy ibn Sa'd quoting Khālid (Ḥilyat al-Awliyā' [p. 1745](https://shamela.ws/book/10495/1745)): « كانوا لا يفضلون على الرباط شيئا » — "They considered nothing superior to ribāṭ."

## The extreme ascetic

### The 40,000 daily tasbīḥ (Dimashq [p. 7089](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7089), Ḥilya [p. 1741](https://shamela.ws/book/10495/1741), Siyar [p. 46047](https://shamela.ws/book/8322/46047))

> « يسبح في اليوم أربعين ألف تسبيحة سوى ما يقرأ من القرآن — فلما مات وضع على سريره ليغسل جعل يشير بأصبعه كذا يحركها يعني بالتسبيح »

> "He performed **40,000 tasbīḥ per day**, in addition to Quranic recitation. When he died and was placed on his bed for washing, **his finger kept moving** — that is, performing the tasbīḥ."

Al-Dhahabī notes that the isnād is munqaṭi' (disconnected).

### The desire for death (Dimashq [p. 7085-7086](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7085), Ḥilya [p. 1741](https://shamela.ws/book/10495/1741), Siyar [p. 46046](https://shamela.ws/book/8322/46046))

Sufyān al-Thawrī quoting Thawr ibn Yazīd:

> « ما أحب أن دابة في بر ولا بحر تفديني الموت — لو كان الموت غاية يستبق إليها ما سبقني إليه أحد إلا من كان أقوى مني »

> "I would not want a single creature, on land or sea, to ransom me from death. If death were a goal toward which one races, nobody would reach it before me except one stronger than I."

Sufyān al-Thawrī loved Khālid from the moment he heard these words.

### Prostrations of gratitude (Dimashq [p. 7086](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7086))

> « ما أحدث الله لي نعمة قط إلا أحدثت له بها شكرا — حتى أن الرجل ليسلم علي أو يوسع لي في المجلس فأومئ للسجود لله شكرا »

> "Never has God granted me a grace without my rendering an act of gratitude in return — to the point that if a man greets me or makes room for me in an assembly, I make a **prostration of thanks** to God."

### Al-yaqīn (Dimashq [p. 7086](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7086))

> « تعلموا اليقين كما تعلموا القرآن حتى تعرفوه فإني أتعلمه »

> "Learn certainty (al-yaqīn) as you learn the Quran, until you know it — for I am still learning it."

### "Reading in the Books" (Ḥilya [p. 1741](https://shamela.ws/book/10495/1741))

> « أجع نفسك وأعرها لعلها ترى الله »

> "Starve your body and strip it bare, perhaps it will see God."

This passage is introduced by "he read in one of the Books" (في بعض الكتب) — the **same formula** used for Ka'b al-Ahbar, 'Abd Allāh ibn 'Amr and Wahb ibn Munabbih. In early Islamic sources, "reading in the Books" systematically designates the consultation of a **corpus foreign to Islam** (Torah, Gospel, biblical writings) — see [[Concepts/Formula - in certain Books|the formula "in certain Books"]].

### The terror he inspired (Siyar [p. 46045](https://shamela.ws/book/8322/46045))

Buqiyya quoting 'Umar ibn Ja'tham: "When Khālid sat down, nobody in the assembly dared mention worldly affairs in his presence."

Ḥabīb ibn Ṣāliḥ: "We feared nobody among the people as much as Khālid ibn Ma'dān."

Ṣafwān ibn 'Amr: "When his study circle grew large, he would stand up and leave — out of hatred for celebrity (كراهية الشهرة)." (Dimashq [p. 7085](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7085))

## The concept of the abdāl

At his death, a man in Homs heard horsemen (فوارس) at night who said they had attended the funeral of the **"badīl"** (substitute / abdāl) Khālid ibn Ma'dān, and that **Arṭa'a ibn al-Mundhir** would succeed him as an abdāl (Dimashq [p. 7089](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7089)).

This is one of the earliest attestations of the Sufi concept of the **abdāl** — the hidden saints who sustain the world — in the hadith sources.

## The hadith of the carnivores

Khālid is also in the chain of the hadith on the [[Traditions/Prohibition of carnivores|prohibition of carnivores]] — via **Jubayr ibn Nufayr** <-- **Abū Tha'laba al-Khushanī** (Musnad Aḥmad / Nasā'ī — [al-Musnad al-Jāmi'](https://shamela.ws/book/12748/8993)). Chain: Baqiyya <-- Buḥayr ibn Sa'd <-- **Khālid ibn Ma'dān** <-- Jubayr ibn Nufayr.

This same hadith is transmitted in parallel by other Umayyad officials from the Shām: **[[Persons/Abū Idrīs al-Khawlānī|Abū Idrīs al-Khawlānī]]** (qāḍī of 'Abd al-Malik) and **Muslim ibn Mishkam** (kātib of Abū al-Dardā'). Al-Zuhrī acknowledged he had **never heard** this prohibition in the Ḥijāz before Abū Idrīs transmitted it to him.

## Transmissions

### From whom he transmits (Siyar [p. 46045](https://shamela.ws/book/8322/46045))

Companions: Thawbān, Abū Umāma, Mu'āwiya, Abū Hurayra, al-Miqdām, Ibn 'Umar, 'Abd Allāh ibn 'Amr, 'Abd Allāh ibn Busr, Dhū Mikhbar, 'Utba ibn 'Abd

Mursal (without having met): Mu'ādh ibn Jabal, Abū al-Dardā', 'Ā'isha, 'Ubāda ibn al-Ṣāmit, Abū 'Ubayda (confirmed by Mūsā ibn Hārūn: « لم يلق أبا عبيدة », Ḥilya [p. 1747](https://shamela.ws/book/10495/1747))

Tābi'ūn: Jubayr ibn Nufayr, 'Abd al-Raḥmān ibn Ghanm, Kathīr ibn Murra, 'Amr ibn al-Aswad

### Who transmits from him

**Thawr ibn Yazīd** (principal, noted as **qadarī** — Dimashq [p. 7083](https://shamela.ws/book/71/7083)), Buḥayy ibn Sa'd, Ṣafwān ibn 'Amr, Ḥarīz ibn 'Uthmān, his daughter **'Abda bint Khālid**

**Al-Awzā'ī** venerated him and sent people to question his daughter about his conduct (hady) (Siyar [p. 46046](https://shamela.ws/book/8322/46046)).

## Summary

Chief of police of Yazīd — under whose reign Karbalā' and the sack of Medina occurred — and simultaneously one of the first **abdāl** of Sufism, an ascetic of 40,000 daily tasbīḥ, who died fasting in a ribāṭ fortress. Declared **ثقة** (reliable) by all critics, none of whom notes the tension.

But the central problem is **epistemic**, not moral. The "70 Companions" rest on a **self-declaration** transmitted by his daughter and his servant. The lists of masters compiled by later critics are drawn from the isnāds themselves — circular reasoning, since the question is precisely whether these isnāds are authentic. **The same chains that prove his existence as a transmitter ARE the ones that need his existence as a transmitter to be valid.** His status as thiqa rests on nothing external to his own network.

He transmits from 'Abd Allāh ibn 'Amr and from Mu'āwiya, and al-Awzā'ī venerates him (Siyar [p. 46046](https://shamela.ws/book/8322/46046)). The formula "he read in one of the Books" (في بعض الكتب) connects him to the corpus of "readers of the Books" — see [[Concepts/Formula - in certain Books|the formula "in certain Books"]].
