# Abū Hurayra — 3 years, 5,000 hadiths

A late Companion of the Prophet (~3 years of companionship, from Khaybar in 7 H), who became the **most prolific hadith transmitter** in history (> 5,000 traditions). His output surpasses that of all other Companions combined — a volume that his own contemporaries contested. Exit point from the [[Ka'b al-Ahbar]] network into the canonical prophetic tradition.

## Profile

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| Full name | Abū Hurayra al-Dawsī (given name disputed — ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Ṣakhr according to the most common version) |
| Tribe | Daws (Yemen) |
| Conversion | 7 H (year of Khaybar) — ~3 years of companionship |
| Death | ~59 H (Medina) |
| Volume | > 5,000 hadiths — the most prolific of the Companions |
| Before Islam | Worked as a **hired servant** (ajīr) of Bisra bint Ghazwān (Ibn Ḥajar, al-Iṣāba, [p. 3965](https://shamela.ws/book/9767/3965)) |

## Al-Tamyīz — Muslim's diagnosis

The most devastating passage on Abū Hurayra is found in **al-Tamyīz** by Imam **Muslim** himself (no. 10, p. 175) — the chapter entitled **"What is reported on caution in carrying and transmitting hadith and guarding against addition and diminution"** (باب ما جاء في التوقي في حمل الحديث وأدائه والتحفظ من الزيادة فيه والنقصان).

**Busr ibn Saʿīd** (tābiʿī thiqa, ascetic of Medina):

> « اتَّقوا اللهَ وتحفَّظوا في الحديثِ، فواللهِ لقد رأيتُنا نُجالسُ أبا هريرةَ، فيُحدِّثنا عن رسولِ اللهِ صلَّى اللهُ عليْهِ وسلَّمَ، ويُحدِّثنا عن كعبٍ، ثم يقومُ، فأسمعُ بعضَ من كان معنا يجعلُ حديثَ رسولِ اللهِ صلَّى اللهُ عليْهِ وسلَّمَ عن كعبٍ، ويجعلُ حديثَ كعبٍ عن رسولِ اللهِ صلَّى اللهُ عليْهِ وسلَّمَ »

> « **Fear God and beware of hadith!** By God, we used to sit with Abū Hurayra: he would relate to us from the Prophet, and relate to us from Kaʿb. Then he would stand up — and I would hear **some of those who were with us** turn the **hadith of the Prophet** into [a hadith] **of Kaʿb**, and the **hadith of Kaʿb** into [a hadith] **of the Prophet**. »

Chain: al-Dārimī ← Marwān al-Dimashqī ← **al-Layth ibn Saʿd** ← Bukayr ibn al-Ashajj ← **Busr ibn Saʿīd**. Isnād judged **ṣaḥīḥ**.

### The detailed version — Tārīkh Dimashq

Ibn ʿAsākir (67/359) gives a more explicit version via Ibn Lahīʿa ← Bukayr ← Busr:

> « كان يقوم فينا أبو هريرة فيقول سمعت النبي يقول كذا كذا سمعت كعبا يقول كذا كذا فعمد الناس إلى بعض ما روى عن كعب فجعلوه عن النبي وبعض ما روى عن النبي فجعلوه عن كعب »

> « Abū Hurayra would stand up among us and say: "I heard the Prophet say such-and-such," "I heard Kaʿb say such-and-such." Then **the people took part of what he reported from Kaʿb and turned it into [a hadith] of the Prophet, and part of what he reported from the Prophet and turned it into [a hadith] of Kaʿb**. »

Ibn Lahīʿa specifies: **« هو من الناس ليس من أبي هريرة »** — « It is the doing **of the people**, not of Abū Hurayra. »

### What the passage says

Muslim places this text in his own book of methodology. The diagnosis is clear:

1. Abū Hurayra **mixes** in his sessions prophetic material and material from [[Ka'b al-Ahbar]]
2. The **listeners** (the subsequent transmitters) confuse the two
3. Kaʿb's words end up attributed to the Prophet — and vice versa
4. The phenomenon is **structural**: it is the normal functioning of Abū Hurayra's sessions

Whether the confusion is "the doing of the people" or of Abū Hurayra changes nothing about the result: the canonical prophetic corpus contains an undetermined number of Kaʿb al-Ahbar's words — and nobody knows which ones. The documented cases (7 days, duʿāʾ of the mosque) are only those where the double version survived. For the rest, the Kaʿb version has disappeared and only the "prophetic" version remains.

## The documented contradictions

### "From Abū Hurayra's pouch" — Bukhārī 5040

Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī ([p. 8087](https://shamela.ws/book/735/8087)) — chain [[Persons/Al-Aʿmash|al-Aʿmash]] ← [[Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān|Abū Ṣāliḥ]] ← Abū Hurayra:

The marfūʿ hadith says: « The best charity is that which leaves the giver in comfort. The upper hand is better than the lower hand. Begin with those in your care. » Then Abū Hurayra **adds** on his own: « The wife says: feed me or divorce me. The slave says: feed me and put me to work. The son says: feed me, to whom will you leave me? »

He is asked: « Did you hear this from the Prophet? » He answers:

> « لا، هذا من كيس أبي هريرة »

> « No, **this is from the pouch** (kīs) of Abū Hurayra. »

The word **kīs** means both "pouch/purse" and "intelligence/sagacity." The admission is in the Ṣaḥīḥ itself: Abū Hurayra **mixes** his own commentary with prophetic words in the same stream of transmission. The boundary between marfūʿ and mawqūf is **porous** in his case.

### ʿAlī: "Since when was the Prophet your khalīl?"

Ibn Qutayba, Taʾwīl Mukhtalif al-Ḥadīth ([p. 61](https://shamela.ws/book/7292/61)):

Abū Hurayra had a habit of saying « **ḥaddathanī khalīlī** » (my intimate friend told me), « **qāla khalīlī** » (my khalīl said), « **raʾaytu khalīlī** » (I saw my khalīl) — claiming a status of intimacy with the Prophet.

ʿAlī confronts him:

> « متى كان النبي خليلك، يا أبا هريرة؟ »

> « **Since when was the Prophet your khalīl**, O Abū Hurayra? »

The term **khalīl** (intimate friend, in the Quranic sense — Ibrāhīm is the khalīl of Allāh) implies exclusive closeness. ʿAlī disputes that a late Companion, who arrived three years before the Prophet's death, could claim this status.

### The junub and fasting — the dead witness

Same source (Ibn Qutayba, [p. 61](https://shamela.ws/book/7292/61)):

Abū Hurayra transmits from the Prophet: « **من أصبح جنبا، فلا صيام له** » — « Whoever wakes up in a state of janāba cannot fast. »

Marwān sends someone to verify with **ʿĀʾisha and Ḥafṣa** (the Prophet's wives). Both formally contradict: the Prophet would wake up in a state of janāba without it preventing his fast.

When the messenger returns to confront Abū Hurayra, he **retracts**:

> « إنما حدثني بذلك الفضل بن العباس »

> « It was **al-Faḍl ibn al-ʿAbbās** who told me that. »

Ibn Qutayba comments: **« فاستشهد ميتا »** — « he invoked a dead man as witness. » Al-Faḍl had died at Ajnādīn in 13 H — a **witness who could neither confirm nor deny**. And Abū Hurayra had first let people believe it was a prophetic hadith: **« أوهم الناس أنه سمع الحديث من رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم، ولم يسمعه »** — « he made people think he had heard the hadith from the Messenger of God, when he had not heard it. »

### The world in 7 days — Muslim 2789

Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, hadith 2789: Abū Hurayra attributes to the Prophet a creation narrative in **seven days** (from Saturday to Friday) — in direct contradiction with **seven Quranic verses** that say **six days** (« خلق السماوات والأرض في ستة أيام »).

**Bukhārī** rejects this hadith. In al-Tārīkh al-Kabīr (1/413), he says:

> « قال بعضهم: عن أبي هريرة عن كعب. وهو أصح »

> « Some say: from Abū Hurayra, **from Kaʿb** [al-Ahbar]. And this is more correct. »

Ibn al-Qayyim reports from Bukhārī (Badāʾiʿ al-Fawāʾid, [p. 176](https://shamela.ws/book/169/176)):

> « إنه حديث معلول، وأن الصحيح: أنه قول كعب — يعني: كعب الأحبار »

> « It is a **defective** (maʿlūl) hadith, and the correct version is that it is a **saying of Kaʿb** — meaning [[Ka'b al-Ahbar]]. »

The pattern is transparent: a tradition from Kaʿb, drawn from Genesis (Bereishit), is attributed to the Prophet by Abū Hurayra — and **placed in Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim**, where it contradicts the Quran.

## The miracle of the ridāʾ — the justification

Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, hadith 119 ([p. 217](https://shamela.ws/book/735/217)):

> « قلت: يا رسول الله، إني أسمع منك حديثا كثيرا **أنساه**؟ قال: ابسط رداءك. فبسطته، قال: فغرف بيديه، ثم قال: ضمه. فضممته، **فما نسيت شيئا بعده**. »

> « I said: O Messenger of God, I hear from you **many hadiths that I forget**! He said: spread out your cloak. I spread it. He **scooped with his hands** [into the cloak], then said: gather it. I gathered it, and **I never forgot anything after that**. »

This miracle is the **canonical justification** for Abū Hurayra's prolixity. The initial admission is remarkable: he himself acknowledges that he **forgot** hadiths. The miracle of the ridāʾ solves the problem — retroactively, magically, and without any possibility of verification.

## ʿUmar and the enrichment — "enemy of God"

Abū Hurayra is appointed governor of Bahrayn by ʿUmar. Upon his return, ʿUmar confronts him (Ṭabaqāt Ibn Saʿd; Tārīkh al-Islām, [p. 82-83](https://shamela.ws/book/29967/82)):

> « استأثرت بهذه الأموال **يا عدو الله، وعدو كتابه**؟ »

> « You hoarded these riches, **O enemy of God and of His Book**? »

Abū Hurayra defends himself: « I am not God's enemy... these are horses that bred, slaves, successive gifts. » ʿUmar verifies, confiscates a portion, and dismisses him.

In another version, Abū Hurayra returns with **400,000** (dirhams). ʿUmar asks: « How much did you keep? » — « 20,000. » — « From where? » — « From trade. » ʿUmar confiscates the surplus for the bayt al-māl.

Later, ʿUmar offers him a new post. Abū Hurayra **refuses**: « I fear five things: speaking without knowledge, judging without discernment, being struck on the back, having my property seized, and having my honor insulted. » — An indirect admission that the first experience had gone badly.

## Bisra bint Ghazwān — from service to marriage

Ibn Ḥajar (al-Iṣāba, [p. 3965](https://shamela.ws/book/9767/3965)):

> « كانت قد استأجرته في العهد النبوي، ثم تزوجها بعد ذلك لما كان مروان يستخلفه في إمرة المدينة »

> « She had **hired him as a servant** (istaʾjarathu) in the Prophet's time, then he **married her** later, when Marwān was appointing him as interim governor of Medina. »

Bisra is the sister of ʿUtba ibn Ghazwān, a famous Companion and governor of Basra.

### The revenge

Fatḥ al-Bārī of Ibn Ḥajar (9/469) — isnād ṣaḥīḥ, via Muḥammad ibn Sīrīn:

> « ولقد رأيتُني وإنِّي لأجيرٌ لابنِ عفَّانَ وبنتِ غزوانَ بطعامِ بطني وعُقبَةِ رِجلي أسوقُ بِهِم إذا ارتحَلوا وأخدمُهُم إذا نزلوا، فقالَت لي يومًا: **لترِدَنَّ حافيًا ولتركبَنَّ قائمًا**. فزوَّجَنيها اللَّهُ تعالى، فقلتُ لها: **لتردِنَّ حافيةً ولتركبِنَّ قائمةً** »

> « I can still see myself, hired servant of Ibn ʿAffān and Bint Ghazwān, for the food in my belly and the use of my feet: I drove them when they traveled and served them when they stopped. One day she said to me: "**You will walk barefoot and ride standing!**" Then God gave her to me in marriage, and I said to her: "**It is you who will walk barefoot and ride standing!**" »

Another version (Ibn Ḥibbān 7150; Ḥilyat al-Awliyāʾ 1/380): a nocturnal traveler hears Abū Hurayra making the **takbīr** of gratitude along the road, and asks him why. Abū Hurayra explains the role reversal: « When people mounted, I led. When they stopped, I served. Now, when they mount, **I mount**. When they stop, **I am served**. »

Abū Hurayra goes from the status of **hired servant** to that of **husband** of his patroness — a social ascent made possible by his access to power under Marwān. He draws from it a **jubilation** that he does not hide.

## The Kaʿb → Abū Hurayra mechanism

### Kaʿb certifies Abū Hurayra (Dimashq [p. 30865](https://shamela.ws/book/71/30865))

> « I have not seen anyone who has not read the Torah know the content of the Torah better than Abū Hurayra. »

An extraordinary certification: a converted rabbi **validates** a Companion's knowledge of the Torah. This implies that Abū Hurayra was transmitting material that Kaʿb recognized as **drawn from the Torah** — that is, isrāʾīliyyāt, not prophetic hadiths.

### The duʿāʾ of the mosque — a Kaʿb saying turned prophetic

Al-Musnad al-muṣannaf ([p. 30873](https://shamela.ws/book/16953/30873)), hadith 13924:

**Prophetic version** (via al-Ḍaḥḥāk ← Saʿīd al-Maqburī ← Abū Hurayra ← the Prophet): « When one of you enters the mosque, let him greet the Prophet and say: "O Allāh, open for me the gates of Your mercy." And when he leaves: "O Allāh, protect me from Shayṭān." »

**Actual version** (via Ibn ʿAjlān ← Saʿīd al-Maqburī ← Abū Hurayra ← **Kaʿb al-Ahbar**):

> « يا أبا هريرة، احفظ مني اثنتين أوصيك بهما: إذا دخلت المسجد فصل على النبي وقل: اللهم افتح لي أبواب رحمتك؛ وإذا خرجت من المسجد فصل على النبي وقل: اللهم احفظني من الشيطان »

> « O Abū Hurayra, remember from me two [things] that I recommend to you: when you enter the mosque, pray upon the Prophet and say: "O Allāh, open for me the gates of Your mercy." And when you leave the mosque, pray upon the Prophet and say: "O Allāh, protect me from Shayṭān." »

Al-Nasāʾī notes the contradiction: **Kaʿb** gives this advice to Abū Hurayra — then Abū Hurayra attributes it to the Prophet. The same content, through the same chain (Saʿīd al-Maqburī ← Abū Hurayra), becomes sometimes a saying of Kaʿb (mawqūf), sometimes a prophetic hadith (marfūʿ).

In the Muṣannaf of Ibn Abī Shayba (3/176, no. 3434), **Kaʿb al-Ahbar** is even confused with **Kaʿb ibn ʿUjra** — a homonymous Companion — showing the manuscript confusion ([p. 3132](https://shamela.ws/book/50/3132)).

### Muʿāwiya paid him

Saʿīd ibn al-Musayyab (al-Thiqāt of al-ʿIjlī, [p. 185](https://shamela.ws/book/9170/185)) — chain via **[[Ḥammād ibn Salama]]** ← Yaḥyā ibn Saʿīd ← Saʿīd ibn al-Musayyab:

> « كان أبو هريرة إذا أعطاه معاوية سكت، وإذا أمسك عنه تكلم »

> « Abū Hurayra, when Muʿāwiya paid him, **he kept quiet**. And when Muʿāwiya stopped paying him, **he spoke**. »

Hadith transmission is **monetized**: Abū Hurayra's silence has a price. And the chain passes through [[Ḥammād ibn Salama]] — the same transmitter whose books were infiltrated by his zindīq stepson.

### ʿUmar threatens them together (Dimashq [p. 30865](https://shamela.ws/book/71/30865))

> To Abū Hurayra: « You will stop or I will send you to the **land of Daws**. »
> To Kaʿb: « You will stop or I will send you to the **land of the apes**. »

ʿUmar threatens both **together**, in the same episode. They are a **pair** of transmission — the rabbi and his relay.

## The documented tadlīs

[[Shu'ba ibn al-Ḥajjāj|Shuʿba]]: « **كان أبو هريرة يدلّس** » — « Abū Hurayra practiced tadlīs. » See [[Concepts/ʿAdālat al-Ṣaḥāba]].

## Relationships

- **Certified by** [[Ka'b al-Ahbar]]: « the best knower of the Torah among those who never read it »
- **Threatened by** ʿUmar together with Kaʿb
- **Paid by** [[Mu'āwiya ibn Abī Sufyān]] for his silence
- **Contested by** ʿAlī (khalīl), ʿĀʾisha (junub), ʿUmar (enrichment)
- **Tadlīs denounced by** [[Shu'ba ibn al-Ḥajjāj|Shuʿba]]
- **Employed then married by** Bisra bint Ghazwān
- **Transmits** the hadith of the [[Traditions/Hijra to al-Shām|hijra to al-Shām]] from [[Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ]]
- **Main chain to him**: [[Persons/Al-Aʿmash|al-Aʿmash]] ← [[Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān]] ← Abū Hurayra — most frequent chain of the Ṣaḥīḥayn
- **Competing chain**: [[Concepts/Silsilat al-Dhahab]] (Mālik → [[Nāfiʿ mawlā Ibn ʿUmar|Nāfiʿ]] → Ibn ʿUmar) — al-Bukhārī: « dībāj khusrawānī »
- **Transmits isrāʾīliyyāt from** [[Ka'b al-Ahbar]] elevating them to prophetic rank (7 days, duʿāʾ mosque)

## Synthesis

Abū Hurayra is the **most prolific and most contested transmitter** of early Islam. His own contemporaries challenged him: ʿUmar calls him enemy of God, ʿAlī disputes his claimed intimacy, ʿĀʾisha contradicts him on fasting, Shuʿba denounces his tadlīs. He himself admits mixing his own words with hadiths ("from Abū Hurayra's pouch") and retracts by invoking a dead witness. The miracle of the ridāʾ — the only justification for his infallible memory — is a narrative that solves the problem of his own admission of forgetfulness through an unverifiable supernatural act.

The link with [[Ka'b al-Ahbar]] is the central knot: Kaʿb certifies him as a knower of the Torah, transmits to him Jewish traditions that Abū Hurayra then elevates to prophetic rank (the duʿāʾ of the mosque, the 7 days of creation). ʿUmar threatens them together, Muʿāwiya protects them together. The system accepts the result: a massive corpus of isrāʾīliyyāt transformed into sunna by a man whom his contemporaries had judged problematic.
