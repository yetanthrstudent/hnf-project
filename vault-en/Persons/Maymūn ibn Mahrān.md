# Maymūn ibn Mahrān

**Wālī (governor) of Raqqa** and scholar of the **Jazīra**. Of Persian origin — captive from Istakhr (سبي اصطخر — Dimashq, [p. 27812](https://shamela.ws/book/71/27812)). Mawlā of Banī Naṣr (or al-Azd). Settled in Raqqa with his descendants (Tārīkh al-Raqqa of al-Qushayrī). One of the four regional scholars of the era of Hishām, according to [[Persons/Sulaymān ibn Mūsā]] (p. 27812).

## The four regional scholars

Cited as the authority of the **Jazīra**, alongside:
- **Al-Zuhrī** for the Ḥijāz
- **Al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī** for Iraq
- **[[Persons/Makhūl al-Shāmī|Makhūl]]** for the Shām

## The hadith of the carnivores

Maymūn transmits from **Ibn 'Abbās** the prohibition on eating carnivores (كل ذي ناب من السباع) and raptors (ذي مخلب من الطير) — [Musnad Aḥmad no. 2619](https://shamela.ws/book/25794/1887), via Shu'ba <-- al-Ḥakam <-- Maymūn <-- Ibn 'Abbās. Shu'ba himself says « أكره أن أحدث برفعه » ("I dislike reporting it as elevated [to the Prophet]").

This is the **same prohibition** transmitted in parallel by other Umayyad officials from the Shām: [[Persons/Abū Idrīs al-Khawlānī|Abū Idrīs al-Khawlānī]] (qāḍī of 'Abd al-Malik), [[Persons/Khālid ibn Ma'dān|Khālid ibn Ma'dān]] (chief of police of Yazīd) and Muslim ibn Mishkam (kātib of Abū al-Dardā'). Al-Zuhrī acknowledged he had **never heard** this prohibition in the Ḥijāz.

**Contradiction**: Ibn 'Abbās's documented methodological position is to **refuse any dietary prohibition absent from the Quran** (« قل لا أجد فيما أوحي إلي محرما » — Bukhārī 5529, [al-'Atīq p. 50](https://shamela.ws/book/103/13050)). Attributing to him the transmission of a prohibition on carnivores — absent from the Quran — contradicts his own doctrine. See the [[Traditions/Prohibition of carnivores|tradition page]].

## Dual role

Like [[Persons/Rajā' ibn Ḥaywa]] (adviser and judge) and [[Persons/Nawf al-Bikālī]] (qāṣṣ and soldier), Maymūn ibn Mahrān illustrates the **fusion** between religious knowledge and Umayyad administrative power. He combines the role of recognised scholar with that of a senior official in the state apparatus of 'Abd al-Malik ibn Marwān.
