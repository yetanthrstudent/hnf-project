# Al-Awzāʿī

Great jurist of al-Shām, founder of the Syrian legal school. Student of **Makhūl al-Shāmī** (« رأيت مكحولا » — Muṣannaf Ibn Abī Shayba, shamela.ws/book/333/7589). Principal transmitter: **al-Walīd ibn Muslim**.

## The [[Concepts/Tafarrud|tafarrud]] (isolation) in transmission

Significant case: he is the **sole** transmitter of the [[Traditions/Kharijites - Quran - throat|Kharijites]] hadith from [[Nāfiʿ mawlā Ibn ʿUmar|Nāfiʿ]] ← Ibn ʿUmar, even though Nāfiʿ had dozens of Medinan students.

If [[Nāfiʿ mawlā Ibn ʿUmar|Nāfiʿ]] had truly transmitted this hadith, **Mālik ibn Anas** and other Medinans would have also reported it. Yet only al-Awzāʿī, a **Syrian**, does. An entirely Damascene chain (Hishām ibn ʿAmmār ← Yaḥyā ibn Ḥamza ← al-Awzāʿī).

## Admission about the faḍāʾil of Ahl al-Bayt and the Syrians

**Al-Walīd ibn Muslim** from al-Awzāʿī (Jāmiʿ Bayān al-ʿIlm of Ibn ʿAbd al-Barr, shamela.ws/book/22367/1428):

> « كانوا يستحيون أن يتحدثوا بأحاديث فضائل أهل البيت ليردوا أهل الشام عما كانوا يأخذون فيه »

> « They were reluctant to transmit hadiths about the merits of Ahl al-Bayt [in order to] divert the people of al-Shām from what they were engaged in. »

**Analysis**: This admission reveals two things:
1. The **people of al-Shām** had "views" (hostility toward Ahl al-Bayt) that needed correction
2. Scholars **chose** which hadiths to transmit based on the audience — confirming that transmission was not neutral but **strategic**

## Ghaylān al-Dimashqī and the qadar in al-Shām

Al-Awzāʿī in al-Sharīʿa of al-Ājurrī (shamela.ws/book/1257/17):

> « أول من نطق بالقدر: رجل من أهل العراق، يقال له: سوسن، وكان نصرانيا فأسلم، ثم تنصر، فأخذ عنه معبد الجهني، وأخذ غيلان عن معبد »

> « The first to speak of the qadar was a man from Iraq named Sawsan — he was Christian, converted to Islam, then returned to Christianity. Maʿbad al-Juhanī took from him, and Ghaylān took from Maʿbad. »

Chain of transmission of the qadar: **Sawsan** (Iraqi Christian) → **Maʿbad al-Juhanī** (killed by al-Ḥajjāj) → **Ghaylān al-Dimashqī** (crucified by **Hishām ibn ʿAbd al-Malik**, ~106 H — al-Minḥa al-Ilāhiyya, shamela.ws/book/30017/152).

After Ghaylān's execution, the people of al-Shām were "at ease" (al-Minḥa al-Ilāhiyya, shamela.ws/book/30017/152). The Basrans remained more affected by this current.

## The four regional flaws

Al-Awzāʿī (Mukhtaṣar Tārīkh Dimashq, shamela.ws/book/3118/112):

> « لا نأخذ من قول أهل العراق خصلتين، ولا من قول أهل مكة خصلتين، ولا من قول أهل المدينة خصلتين، ولا من قول أهل الشام خصلتين »

| Region | Flaw 1 | Flaw 2 |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **Iraq** | Delaying the suḥūr | Drinking nabīdh |
| **Mecca** | Al-mutʿa (temporary marriage) | Al-ṣarf (ribā) |
| **Medina** | Rear intercourse | Singing (samāʿ) |
| **al-Shām** | Selling juice (ʿaṣīr = alcohol) | The dīwān (civil service register) |

Al-Awzāʿī adds that the two flaws of al-Shām "have disappeared" (قد ذهبا) (same passage, shamela.ws/book/3118/112).

## Chain al-Awzāʿī ← Makhūl ← Rajāʾ ← Ibn ʿAmr

Attested in the Musnad al-Shāmiyyīn of al-Ṭabarānī (shamela.ws/book/13162/2856):

> Maslama ibn ʿAlī ← **al-Awzāʿī** ← **Makhūl** ← **Rajāʾ ibn Ḥaywa** ← **[[Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ|ʿAbd Allāh ibn ʿAmr]]**

This chain is entirely **Syrian** and connects al-Awzāʿī to the Kaʿb network via:
- **Makhūl**: jurist of al-Shām, master of al-Awzāʿī
- **Rajāʾ ibn Ḥaywa**: adviser to the Umayyad caliphs (builder of the Dome of the Rock), key figure of the Umayyad administration
- **Ibn ʿAmr**: the possessor of the [[Traditions/Zamālatain of Yarmūk|zamālatain]] and student of [[Sarj al-Yarmūkī]]

## Relationships in the network

- **Master**: Makhūl al-Shāmī (رأيت مكحولا)
- **Principal transmitter**: al-Walīd ibn Muslim (from whom come the words about Ahl al-Bayt)
- **Connection to Ibn ʿAmr** via Makhūl ← Rajāʾ ibn Ḥaywa
- **Defends Makhūl** against the accusation of qadarism ("we verified, it is false")
