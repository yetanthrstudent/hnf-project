# Abū al-Zubayr al-Makkī — the ṣaḥīfa and the forger

**The most prolific transmitter of [[Jābir ibn 'Abd Allāh]]** — approximately 360 hadiths in Tuḥfat al-Ashrāf. But al-Layth ibn Saʿd demonstrates that his transmission is a **mixture**: part heard from Jābir, part received from unnamed intermediaries. And [[Persons/Ibn Jurayj|Ibn Jurayj]], who claims to transmit directly from him, actually goes through **Yāsīn al-Zayyāt** — a forger (kadhdhāb).

## Profile

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Full name | Muḥammad ibn Muslim ibn Tadrus al-Qurashī al-Asadī al-Makkī |
| Death | ~126 H in Mecca |
| Relationship to Jābir | **Mixed**: partial samāʿ + ṣaḥīfa (admission to al-Layth) |
| Volume | ~360 hadiths from Jābir in the collections |
| Market | Shuʿba accuses him of cheating with the scales — moral criticism |

## The admission to al-Layth ibn Saʿd — the founding document

Al-Layth ibn Saʿd recounts (via Saʿīd ibn Abī Maryam, in Tahdhīb al-Kamāl and Siyar Aʿlām al-Nubalāʾ vol. 5):

> « I came to Mecca and went to Abū al-Zubayr. He handed me **two books** (kitābayn). I left with them, then I said to myself: what if I went back and asked him whether he heard all of this from Jābir? I went back and asked. He said: »
>
> « **منه ما سمعت ومنه ما حُدِّثتُ عنه** »
>
> « **Part of it I heard from him; part of it was reported to me from him.** »

Al-Layth told him: « **Mark for me what you heard** (أعلم لي على ما سمعت). » Abū al-Zubayr marked certain hadiths. Al-Layth: « I only transmit those. »

### The importance of this admission

1. **The two books**: Abū al-Zubayr possesses a written collection, not an oral memory
2. **The unmarked mixture**: without al-Layth's question, the hadiths heard and the hadiths reported would be indistinguishable
3. **Only al-Layth sorted them**: all other transmitters of Abū al-Zubayr (including [[Persons/Ibn Jurayj|Ibn Jurayj]]) took the whole without verification
4. **But al-Layth himself is a victim**: his own books were **interpolated by [[Khālid al-Madā'inī]]** (insertion of names into mursal isnāds → musnad). The verifier is himself a victim of a forger. See [[Concepts/Falsification of nusakh]]

## The contamination via Yāsīn al-Zayyāt

Ibn Ḥibbān (al-Majrūḥīn 3/142, cited in the Muʿjam al-Mudallisīn [p. 309](https://shamela.ws/book/794/309)):

> « ياسين بن معاذ الزيات [...] كان ممن يروي الموضوعات عن الثقات، وينفرد بالمعضلات عن الأثبات [...] وكل ما وقع في نسخة ابن جريج عن أبي الزبير من المناكير كان ذلك مما سمعه ابن جريج عن ياسين الزيات عن أبي الزبير، فدلس عنه »
>
> « Yāsīn ibn Muʿādh al-Zayyāt [...] fabricated traditions (mawḍūʿāt) under the names of reliable transmitters. **All the munkar traditions** in [[Persons/Ibn Jurayj|Ibn Jurayj]]'s nuskha from Abū al-Zubayr — that is what Ibn Jurayj **heard from Yāsīn al-Zayyāt** from Abū al-Zubayr, and **he concealed it** (dallasa ʿanhu). »

### The actual circuit

```
Jābir ibn ʿAbd Allāh
        │
   ABŪ AL-ZUBAYR (samāʿ + ṣaḥīfa mixed)
        │
        ├── AL-LAYTH (sorted — transmits only the samāʿ)
        │       │
        │   KHĀLID AL-MADĀ'INĪ (forger — inserts names into al-Layth's books)
        │
        ├── YĀSĪN AL-ZAYYĀT (kadhdhāb — fabricates mawḍūʿāt)
        │       │
        │   IBN JURAYJ (tadlīs: "Abū al-Zubayr told me")
        │       │
        │   Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (contaminated route)
        │
        └── Zuhayr ibn Muʿāwiya (discovers the source is Ṣafwān, not Jābir)
```

## The fictitious samāʿ of Ibn Jurayj — a consensus ignored by Muslim

Al-Nasāʾī (al-Sunan al-Kubrā, no. 7461-7466, [Shamela](https://shamela.ws/book/8361)) settles it: **ما صنع شيئاً — ابن جريج لم يسمعه من أبي الزبير** — « Ibn Jurayj did not hear it from Abū al-Zubayr. He achieved nothing. » Aḥmad identifies the hidden intermediary: **إنما سمعهما ابن جريج من ياسين الزيات**. Abū Zurʿa and Abū Ḥātim confirm (al-ʿIlal 1/450).

The result: **all** of the channel "Ibn Jurayj — Abū al-Zubayr — Jābir" is contaminated by a forger whom four of the greatest critics identify by name. Yet it is through this channel that a significant portion of the ~360 hadiths of Jābir passes — and **Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim** draws from it with the formula "akhbaranī" which claims to guarantee direct samāʿ. Same paradox as for [[Persons/Ḥammād ibn Salama|Ḥammād]]: the system documents its own flaws without settling them.

The detail of the argument — the irony of Aḥmad's rule, the consensus of critics, the specific hadiths — is developed in [[Persons/Ibn Jurayj#The phantom samāʿ — al-Nasāʾī, Aḥmad, and the collapse of the rule|the Ibn Jurayj page]].

## The testimony of Zuhayr ibn Muʿāwiya

Zuhayr questioned Abū al-Zubayr about a hadith. Abū al-Zubayr admitted that the information actually came from **Ṣafwān**, not from Jābir directly. Another case of tadlīs: Jābir's name replaces that of the actual intermediary.

## Evaluations

| Critic | Judgment |
|----------|----------|
| **ʿAlī ibn al-Madīnī** | thiqa thabt |
| **Yaḥyā ibn Maʿīn** | thiqa |
| **al-Nasāʾī** | thiqa |
| **Aḥmad** | « More reliable than Abū Sufyān Ṭalḥa ibn Nāfiʿ » |
| **Al-Shāfiʿī** | « أبو الزبير يحتاج إلى دعامة » — needs support |
| **Shuʿba** | Accuses him of cheating with the scales at the market |
| **Ibn Ḥajar** | Classifies him among the mudallis |

The divergence is significant: the classical critics declare him thiqa, but al-Shāfiʿī demands corroboration, and the documentary evidence (al-Layth) shows that his corpus is an **unsorted mixture**.

## The link with al-Yashkurī's ṣaḥīfa

Sufyān reports that someone came to Abū al-Zubayr with the text of **Sulaymān ibn Qays al-Yashkurī**, questioning him about hadiths. Abū al-Zubayr would transmit certain traditions then say: **« unẓur kayfa hiya fī kitābika »** — « **look at how it is in your book** ».

This is an admission: the ṣaḥīfa serves as a cross-reference. The material circulates through written documents, not solely through memory.

## Relationships

- **Transmits from** [[Jābir ibn 'Abd Allāh]] (partial samāʿ + ṣaḥīfa), Ṣafwān (hidden under Jābir's name)
- **Transmitted by** al-Layth ibn Saʿd (the only one to sort samāʿ/non-samāʿ), [[Persons/Ibn Jurayj|Ibn Jurayj]] (contaminated by Yāsīn al-Zayyāt), Zuhayr ibn Muʿāwiya, Mālik ibn Anas
- **Hidden source**: Yāsīn al-Zayyāt (kadhdhāb, behind the munkarāt of Ibn Jurayj's nuskha)
- **Consults** the ṣaḥīfa of [[Sulaymān ibn Qays al-Yashkurī]] as reference
