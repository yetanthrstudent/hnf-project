# Ka'b al-Ahbar

Yemeni rabbi who converted to Islam under the caliphate of [[Omar ibn al-Khattab|'Umar]]. Central figure in the transmission of **isrā'īliyyāt** (Judeo-biblical traditions) in early Islam. Settled in [[Homs]], he combined the role of [[Concepts/Quṣṣāṣ|qāṣṣ]] (preacher-storyteller) and scholar of the "ancient books."

## Relationships

- **Cousin** of [[Dhū'l-Kilā' al-Ḥimyarī]] (same Himyarite clan in [[Homs]])
- **Father-in-law** of [[Nawf al-Bikālī]] (ابن امرأة كعب — son of Ka'b's wife)
- **Father-in-law** of [[Tubā'ī ibn 'Āmir]] (son-in-law, صهر كعب الأحبار)
- **Transmits to / meets** [[Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ]] (documented meeting at Mecca, Tabari p. 398)
- **Authorized by** [[Mu'āwiya ibn Abī Sufyān]] to resume qaṣṣ after being challenged
- **Certifies** [[Abū Hurayra]] as "the best expert on the Torah among those who have not read it"
- **His words elevated to prophetic status by** [[Abū Hurayra]] (7 days Muslim 2789, du'ā' of the mosque)
- **Confused with** Ka'b ibn ʿUjra in manuscripts (Ibn Abī Shayba, Muṣannaf 3/176)
- **Isrā'īliyyāt transmitted via** [[Ḥammād ibn Salama]] (Ḥilyat al-Awliyā', [p. 7988](https://shamela.ws/book/10495/7988))
- **Threatened by** [[Omar ibn al-Khattab|'Umar]] with exile "to the land of the apes"
- **Denounced (via Nawf) by** [[Ibn 'Abbās]]: "he lied, the enemy of God"
- **Contemporary of** [[Wahb ibn Munabbih]] (generational successor in the transmission of isrā'īliyyāt)
- **Material transmitted via** [[Persons/ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra al-Salūlī|ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra al-Salūlī]] → [[Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān]] (ṣaḥīfa) → [[Persons/Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ|Suhayl]] / [[Persons/Al-Aʿmash|al-Aʿmash]] → Ṣaḥīḥayn — erased link, restored by Abū Zurʿa (Q.2022: هذا خطأ — see [[Persons/ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra al-Salūlī]])
- **Material transmitted via** ʿAṭāʾ ibn Qurra al-Salūlī ← [[Persons/ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ḍamra al-Salūlī|al-Salūlī]] → Ibn Thawbān → Tirmidhī (no. 2322, al-dunyā malʿūna) and Ibn Mājah (no. 4112)
- **Material confused with** that of the Prophet in the chain [[Nāfiʿ mawlā Ibn ʿUmar]] → Ibn ʿUmar (3 divergences Nāfiʿ/Sālim)

## Key statements and traditions

### Ka'b certifies Abū Hurayra (Dimashq p. 30865)
> "I have not seen anyone who has not read the Torah know its contents better than Abū Hurayra."

### Mu'āwiya certifies Ka'b — then admits the lying (Dimashq [p. 23281](https://shamela.ws/book/71/23281))

First version (via al-Zuhrī ← Ḥumayd ibn ʿAbd al-Raḥmān):
> "Ka'b al-Ahbar was one of the scholars — he had knowledge like fruit." And: "He was the most truthful of those who transmit from the Book."

Second version (same chain, via Bukhārī, Tārīkh al-Ṣaghīr 1/62) — the **complete** statement:

> « إن كان لمن أصدق هؤلاء المحدثين الذين يحدثون عن الكتاب، **فإن كنا مع ذلك لنبلو عليه الكذب** »

> "He was among the most truthful of those who transmit from the Book — **even though despite this we would catch him lying**."

Both versions are on the **same page** of Ibn ʿAsākir. The second, transmitted by **Bukhārī himself**, contains the admission: Mu'āwiya certifies Ka'b as the most truthful of the Book's transmitters, then admits **in the same sentence** that they caught him lying. It is Mu'āwiya — Ka'b's protector, the one who authorizes him to preach — who testifies to his own lying.

### Ka'b challenges a Quranic reading (Tabari p. 6371)
Ka'b learns the Quran from a Companion. When he reaches the verse "know that God is Forgiving and Merciful," he refuses: "I do not know this in any of God's books!" The correct verse is "Mighty and Wise" — Ka'b was right, judging the Quran by the standard of the Torah.

### 'Umar threatens Ka'b and Abū Hurayra together (Dimashq p. 30865)
> To Abū Hurayra: "You will stop or I send you back to the land of Daws."
> To Ka'b: "You will stop or I send you to the land of the apes."

### Al-Falaq (Takhwīf min al-Nār p. 124)
Ka'b enters a church, admires its beauty, and declares: "Beautiful construction, but a misguided people. I accept for them al-Falaq — a house in Hell whose opening makes all its inhabitants cry out."

## Philosophy and approach — Ḥilyat al-Awliyā' (Abū Nu'aym, shamela.ws/book/10495/7969-8002)

The section devoted to Ka'b in the Ḥilyat al-Awliyā' (34 pages, 7969-8002) gives access to his **direct preaching**. It reveals a character with precise contours.

### 1. Self-definition (p. 7969)

Ka'b describes himself as: "the scholar (al-ḥibr), possessor of books and writings, he who reveals what is hidden and the secrets" (العالم الحبر صاحب الكتب والخطوط). He explicitly claims his status as a **holder of scriptural knowledge**.

### 2. The ascetic preacher (p. 7970-7976)

Ka'b preaches a **radical asceticism** (zuhd):
- Poverty is a divine election: "if this world were worth the wing of a mosquito, God would not have given anything to the unbelievers"
- The prophets "were happier in poverty and trials... one of them could be killed by lice"
- Moses says: "You wear the garments of monks, but your hearts are the hearts of tyrants"
- Weeping out of fear of God is worth "more than a mountain of gold in charity"

This register is **proto-Sufi** — the entry explicitly links him to Sufism (p. 7969). Ka'b thus provides the **scriptural material** that would feed Islamic spirituality in the early centuries.

### 3. The eschatological terrorist (p. 7984-8001)

The **core** of Ka'b's preaching is the **terror of the Last Day and of Hell**. This is his role as a [[Concepts/Quṣṣāṣ|qāṣṣ]]:
- Hell roars, the prophets fall to their knees, even Ibrāhīm says "myself, myself"
- Guards whose shoulders span a year's journey, armed with iron rods
- A bucket of ghasslīn poured in the East makes skulls boil in the West
- Brains flow through the nostrils from the heat
- Descriptions of furnaces, glacial cold (zamharīr), bridges over the fire

The recurring pattern with **'Umar**: he asks « خوّفنا يا كعب » ("frighten us, Ka'b") and Ka'b delivers escalating terrors until the audience weeps (p. 7985-7986, 7994).

### 4. The political theologian (p. 7982, 7992)

Ka'b links religion and governance:
- "The community improves through the ruler's integrity, and corrupts through his corruption" (p. 7982)
- The story of the just ruler (crowned in Paradise) vs. the unjust ruler (p. 7992) — a **political theology** that serves Umayyad power

### 5. The explicit sources

Ka'b regularly cites his sources:
- "I found in the Torah" (وجدت في التوراة, p. 7990)
- "It is mentioned in what Allah revealed in the Torah" (p. 7988)
- "I find in some of the Books" (أجد في بعض الكتب, p. 329/288)
- He swears "by Him who split the sea for Moses" (p. 7988) — a Judaic formula

### 6. The chains of transmission

Several traditions from this section pass through network members:
- p. 7979: [[Persons/Al-Aʿmash|al-Aʿmash]] ← Shamīr ← **[[Shahr ibn Ḥawshab]]** ← Ka'b ("I wish I were my family's ram")
- p. 7997: Qatāda ← Ka'b (the brain flowing through the nostrils)
- p. 7982: **al-Awzā'ī** ← al-Walīd ibn Hishām ← Ka'b (the community and the ruler)

### Summary

Ka'b is a **total preacher**: he simultaneously provides (1) ascetic/Sufi material, (2) eschatological terror, (3) political theology, all drawn from the "Books" that he alone can read. His audience is captive: even 'Umar asks him to "frighten us." This profile is that of a **qāṣṣ** in the full sense — not a mere storyteller, but a **master of sacred fear** who draws his authority from exclusive access to pre-Islamic scriptures.

## Ka'b's words become prophetic hadiths

The central mechanism: Ka'b transmits Jewish material → [[Abū Hurayra]] removes Ka'b from the chain → the material becomes a hadith of the Prophet in the canonical collections.

### Muslim's diagnosis — al-Tamyīz no. 10

Muslim places in his own methodological book (al-Tamyīz, [p. 175](https://shamela.ws/book/6048/50)) the testimony of **Busr ibn Sa'īd**:

> « **اتَّقوا اللهَ وتحفَّظوا في الحديثِ** فواللهِ لقد رأيتُنا نُجالسُ أبا هريرةَ، فيُحدِّثنا عن رسولِ اللهِ، ويُحدِّثنا عن كعبٍ، ثم يقومُ، فأسمعُ بعضَ من كان معنا يجعلُ حديثَ رسولِ اللهِ عن كعبٍ، ويجعلُ حديثَ كعبٍ عن رسولِ اللهِ »

> "Fear God and take care with hadith! By God, we would sit with Abū Hurayra: he would report from the Prophet and from Ka'b. Then he would stand up — and I would hear some of those with us turn the **hadith of the Prophet** into [a hadith] **of Ka'b**, and the **hadith of Ka'b** into [a hadith] **of the Prophet**."

See [[Abū Hurayra]] for the full analysis. The cases below (7 days, du'ā' of the mosque) are the examples where the double version has survived — for the others, only the "prophetic" version remains.

### The world in 7 days — Muslim 2789

Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, hadith 2789: the creation in **seven days** (Saturday to Friday), attributed to the Prophet via Abū Hurayra. The content corresponds to **Genesis** (Bereishit) — and contradicts **seven Quranic verses** that say six days.

Bukhārī settles the matter (Tārīkh al-Kabīr 1/413; Badā'iʿ al-Fawā'id, [p. 176](https://shamela.ws/book/169/176)):

> « قال بعضهم: عن أبي هريرة **عن كعب**. وهو أصح »

> "Some say: from Abū Hurayra, **from Ka'b**. And that is more correct."

And: "It is a **maʿlūl** (defective) hadith, and the correct version is that it is a **statement of Ka'b al-Ahbar**."

A hadith from **Genesis**, placed in **Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim**, that **contradicts the Quran** — and whose real author is Ka'b. The system knows it (Bukhārī says so) but has not removed it from the Ṣaḥīḥ.

### The du'ā' of the mosque — Ka'b advises Abū Hurayra

Al-Musnad al-muṣannaf ([p. 30873](https://shamela.ws/book/16953/30873)), hadith 13924:

**Prophetic version** (via al-Ḍaḥḥāk ← Sa'īd al-Maqburī ← Abū Hurayra ← the Prophet): invocations when entering and leaving the mosque.

**Real version** (via Ibn ʿAjlān ← Sa'īd al-Maqburī ← Abū Hurayra ← **Ka'b al-Ahbar**, mawqūf):

> « يا أبا هريرة، احفظ مني اثنتين أوصيك بهما: إذا دخلت المسجد فصل على النبي وقل: اللهم افتح لي أبواب رحمتك؛ وإذا خرجت من المسجد فصل على النبي وقل: اللهم احفظني من الشيطان »

Ka'b **gives personal advice** to Abū Hurayra. In another transmission route, Abū Hurayra removes Ka'b from the chain and attributes the same words to the Prophet. Al-Nasā'ī (Sunan al-Kubrā 9/40, no. 9839) notes the contradiction.

In the Muṣannaf of Ibn Abī Shayba (3/176, no. 3434), **Ka'b al-Ahbar** is even confused with **Ka'b ibn ʿUjra** — a homonymous Companion ([p. 3132](https://shamela.ws/book/50/3132)). A manuscript confusion that shows the fragility of the attribution.

### The recurring pattern

```
Ka'b al-Ahbar ("in the Books...")
        │
        └─→ ABŪ HURAYRA (receives the advice / tradition)
                │
                ├── Version A: "Ka'b told me" (mawqūf = Ka'b's words)
                │
                └── Version B: "The Prophet said" (marfūʿ = prophetic hadith)
                        │
                        └─→ ṢAḤĪḤAYN
```

The same content, the same transmitter, two contradictory attributions. The documented cases (7 days, du'ā') are probably only the visible tip of the phenomenon.
