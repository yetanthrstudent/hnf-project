# Shahr ibn Ḥawshab

Syrian transmitter, key link in the [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar|Ka'b al-Ahbar]] → [[Persons/Nawf al-Bikālī|Nawf al-Bikālī]] → Shahr network. Declared **ḍa'īf** (weak) by al-Wāqidī (Dimashq p. 10404). 142 mentions in Tarikh Dimashq.

## Relationships

- **Transmits from** [[Persons/Nawf al-Bikālī|Nawf al-Bikālī]] (listed among his transmitters in Nawf's biography, p. 28828)
- **Visited** Nawf in Damascus in 60 H (Dimashq [p. 28833](https://shamela.ws/book/71/28833)) — *ref. to verify*
- **Transmits to** [[Persons/Qatāda ibn Di'āma|Qatāda ibn Di'āma]] and others

## Role in the network

Shahr is the point of passage between Nawf (oral source for Ka'b's isrā'īliyyāt) and the classical transmitters. His recognized weakness by the critics is significant: he is the most vulnerable link in the chain.

```
Ka'b → Nawf → Shahr → attribution to Ibn 'Amr
                          (weak link)
```

The hadith of the [[Traditions/Hijra to al-Shām|hijra to al-Shām]] passes through Shahr → Nawf → Ibn 'Amr (Dimashq p. 28829).
