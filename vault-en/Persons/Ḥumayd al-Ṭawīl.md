# Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl

**Basran transmitter**, key figure in the Basra network under the governorship of [[Khālid ibn 'Abd Allāh al-Qasrī]]. Mawlā (freedman) of Ṭalḥa al-Ṭalaḥāt al-Khuzā'ī. Died in 142 or 143 H, at 75, while praying standing.

Classified as **thiqa** (reliable) in the collections of Bukhārī and Muslim, but a **notorious mudallis**: he systematically confuses what he heard from Anas with what he learned from [[Thābit al-Bunānī]] and [[Qatāda ibn Di'āma]].

## The tadlīs — confusion of sources

### The diagnosis (al-'Uqaylī, al-Ḍu'afā' al-Kabīr, [p. 770-772](https://shamela.ws/book/13041/770))

Several converging testimonies:

**[[Ḥammād ibn Salama]]** (Ḥumayd's nephew):

> « عامة ما يروي حميد عن أنس لم يسمعه منه، إنما سمعه من ثابت »

> "The majority of what Ḥumayd reports from Anas, **he did not hear from him** — he heard it from Thābit."

**Shu'ba**:

> « كل شيء سمع حميد عن أنس خمسة أحاديث »

> "Everything Ḥumayd actually heard from Anas amounts to **five hadiths**."

**Sufyān** (via Durust al-Baṣrī):

> "Ḥumayd **confused** (اختلط عليه) what he heard from Anas with what he heard from Thābit and Qatāda from Anas, except for a small number."

Sufyān would test Ḥumayd by asking him about traditions known through routes other than Anas — and Ḥumayd would reply: "I heard it from Anas."

**Ḥabīb ibn al-Shahīd** to Ḥumayd, while he was transmitting to Shu'ba:

> "Be careful what you report, for Shu'ba transmits from you. For Ḥumayd is a man who **forgets** (إن حميدا رجل نسي)."

### Ḥumayd's own confession

Ḥumayd himself acknowledges (al-'Uqaylī, [p. 772](https://shamela.ws/book/13041/772)):

> "Shu'ba would ask me about what I had heard from Anas, **and I obscured my sources from him** (فألبسته عليه)."

## The books of al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī

[[Ḥammād ibn Salama]] reports (al-'Ilal of Aḥmad, no. 3831; al-'Uqaylī [p. 770](https://shamela.ws/book/13041/770)):

> « أخذ حميد كتب الحسن فنسخها ثم ردها عليه »

> "Ḥumayd took the **books of al-Ḥasan [al-Baṣrī]**, copied them, then returned them to him."

Ḥumayd therefore had access to al-Ḥasan's written corpus — a reservoir of traditions he could then present as heard orally.

## Working for the ruling power (عمل السلطان)

Ḥumayd was criticized for his involvement in government work. **Zā'ida** stopped transmitting from him for this reason, and **Abū Ḥātim** confirms the motive:

> « لتدخله في عمل السلطان »

> "Because he was involved in **working for the ruling power**."

### Link with Khālid al-Qasrī

Al-Dhahabī lists Ḥumayd among the **direct transmitters** of Khālid al-Qasrī (Tārīkh al-Islām, [p. 2468](https://shamela.ws/book/22771/2468)):

> « وعنه **حميد الطويل** وإسماعيل بن أبي خالد وسيار أبو الحكم »

> "From him [Khālid al-Qasrī] transmitted **Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl**, Ismā'īl ibn Abī Khālid and Sayyār Abū al-Ḥakam."

Ḥumayd is therefore a man of the governor — the same pattern as [[Qatāda ibn Di'āma]], who dies at Khālid's place in Wāsiṭ, sustained by his provisions.

## Case of [[Concepts/Tafarrud|tafarrud]]: the hadith of the two festivals

Ḥumayd reports from Anas that the Prophet, arriving in Medina, found the people celebrating two festival days, and said:

> « قدم رسول الله ﷺ المدينة ولهم يومان يلعبون فيهما فقال: ما هذان اليومان؟ قالوا: كنا نلعب فيهما في الجاهلية. فقال: إن الله قد أبدلكم بهما خيرا منهما: يوم الأضحى ويوم الفطر »

> "The Prophet arrived in Medina while they had two days on which they used to play. He asked: 'What are these two days?' They replied: 'We used to play on them during the Jāhiliyya.' He said: 'God has replaced them for you with something better: the day of al-Aḍḥā and the day of al-Fiṭr.'"

### The tafarrud

**All reliable chains pass through Ḥumayd ← Anas.** No other transmitter of Anas (neither Qatāda, nor Thābit, nor al-Zuhrī) reports this hadith. No other Companion transmits it either. The hadith exists in at least 12 collections, but the entire body of evidence rests on a single point: Ḥumayd.

| Collection | No. | Chain (from Ḥumayd upward: identical) |
|---------|-----|----------------------------------------------|
| Abū Dāwūd | 1134 | Mūsā ibn Ismā'īl ← Ḥammād ← Ḥumayd ← Anas |
| al-Nasā'ī | 1556 | 'Alī ibn Ḥujr ← Ismā'īl ibn 'Ulayya ← Ḥumayd ← Anas |
| Aḥmad | 12006 | Ibn Abī 'Adī ← Ḥumayd ← Anas |
| Aḥmad | 13622 | 'Affān ← Ḥammād ← Ḥumayd (**سمعت أنسا**) ← Anas |
| al-Mustadrak | 1131 | via Ḥammād ← Ḥumayd ← Anas |
| Abū Ya'lā | 3820 | via Ḥumayd ← Anas |
| al-Mukhtāra (al-Ḍiyā') | 1908 | via Ḥumayd ← Anas |

**Only alternative route**: al-Bayhaqī (Shu'ab al-Īmān, 3710) adds **al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī** alongside Ḥumayd, via al-Rabī' ibn Ṣubayḥ. But al-Rabī' is **ḍa'īf** according to the majority of critics, and al-Ḥasan is known for his irsāl from Anas — this route does not break the tafarrud.

### The tadlīs problem

Some students report the first part as a statement of Anas (mawqūf), others as a statement of the Prophet (marfū') — a sign of **instability** in transmission (Awhām al-Muḥaddithīn, [p. 4223-4224](https://shamela.ws/book/1329/4223)).

In Aḥmad (13622), Ḥumayd explicitly says **« سمعت أنس بن مالك »** (I heard Anas ibn Mālik), which is supposed to lift the suspicion of tadlīs. But Shu'ba states that Ḥumayd actually heard from Anas only **five hadiths** (al-'Uqaylī, [p. 770](https://shamela.ws/book/13041/770)), and Ḥumayd himself confesses to having "obscured his sources" (ألبسته) from Shu'ba regarding what he had truly heard. The formula "سمعت" is therefore not necessarily conclusive from a mudallis of this profile.

### Authentication despite the tafarrud

This hadith enjoys a near-consensus of authentication. Salmān al-'Awda summarizes: « تواطأ الأئمة على تصحيح إسناده » ("the imams agreed on the authentication of its chain").

| Scholar | Judgment | Source |
|--------|----------|--------|
| al-Ḥākim (d. 405 H) | « صحيح على شرط مسلم ولم يخرجاه » | al-Mustadrak, 1131 |
| al-Dhahabī (d. 748 H) | Confirms al-Ḥākim's judgment | Talkhīṣ al-Mustadrak |
| Ibn Ḥajar (d. 852 H) | « رواه أبو داود والنسائي بإسناد صحيح » | Bulūgh al-Marām |
| al-Nawawī (d. 676 H) | Authenticated | al-Majmū', Khulāṣat al-Aḥkām |
| Ibn Ḥazm (d. 456 H) | Authenticated | — |
| al-Bayhaqī (d. 458 H) | Authenticated | al-Sunan al-Kubrā |
| Ibn al-Mundhir (d. 319 H) | Authenticated | — |
| Ibn Taymiyya (d. 728 H) | « على شرط مسلم » | Iqtiḍā' al-Ṣirāṭ al-Mustaqīm |
| al-Albānī (d. 1999) | « إسناده صحيح » | al-Silsila al-Ṣaḥīḥa, 2021 |
| Shu'ayb al-Arna'ūṭ | « إسناده صحيح، رجاله ثقات رجال الشيخين » | Taḥqīq of the Musnad |

No major scholar weakened it.

### Observation

The entire body of evidence for this hadith rests on a single transmitter — a confirmed mudallis of whom Shu'ba states he "actually heard from Anas only five hadiths," who confesses to having obscured his sources from his interlocutors, and whose students diverge on the mawqūf/marfū' status. Ḥumayd's tafarrud is treated as a non-issue because his transmitters *downstream* (Ḥammād, Ibn 'Ulayya, Ibn Abī 'Adī) are reliable. The reasoning evaluates the quality of the chain *below* Ḥumayd without weighing the structural fact that a single point of transmission — regardless of how many branches it produces downstream — remains a single point.

## Relationships

- **Transmits from** [[Khālid ibn 'Abd Allāh al-Qasrī]] — direct transmitter of the governor
- **Transmits from** [[Anas ibn Mālik]] — but the majority actually comes from [[Thābit al-Bunānī]] and [[Qatāda ibn Di'āma]]
- **Copies the books of** [[Persons/al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī|al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī]]
- **Washes the body of** [[Thābit al-Bunānī]] after his death (personal request by Thābit)
- **Criticized by** Zā'ida, [[Shu'ba ibn al-Ḥajjāj|Shuʿba]], [[Ḥammād ibn Salama]] for his tadlīs and his عمل السلطان
- **Contemporary of** [[Qatāda ibn Di'āma]] (d. 117 H) and [[Thābit al-Bunānī]] (d. ~118 H)
