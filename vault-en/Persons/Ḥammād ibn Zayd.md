# Ḥammād ibn Zayd — the blind man who never met Qatāda

**Imam of Basra**, unanimously thiqa, considered the most reliable transmitter of **Ayyūb al-Sakhtiyānī** (20 years of association). Blind (ḍarīr), without books — he transmitted from memory. Any chain "Ḥammād ibn Zayd ← [[Qatāda ibn Di'āma|Qatāda]]" is **munqaṭiʿa**: he never met him.

## The non-samāʿ from Qatāda — the proof

### The direct testimony

Al-Muqaddimī, Tārīkh (no. 1017), via Sulaymān ibn Ḥarb, who says he heard Ḥammād ibn Zayd say:

> « كنت هيأت الصحف لقدوم قتادة من واسط، من عند خالد بن عبد الله القسري، لأكتب عنه، فمات بواسط، وذلك في سنة سبع عشرة ومائة »
>
> "I had prepared the pages (ṣuḥuf) for Qatāda's arrival from Wāsiṭ, from [[Khālid ibn 'Abd Allāh al-Qasrī|Khālid ibn ʿAbd Allāh al-Qasrī]]'s place, in order to write from him, but he died at Wāsiṭ, and that was in the year 117."

Ṭāriq ibn ʿAwaḍ Allāh comments (al-Irshādāt, [p. 160](https://shamela.ws/book/9938/160)):

> « وهذا من أدل دليل على أن حماد بن زيد لم يسمع عن قتادة ولم يلتق به أصلا »
>
> "This is the clearest proof that Ḥammād ibn Zayd **did not hear from Qatāda and never met him**."

### The argument from silence

> « حماد ابن زيد ليست له رواية عن قتادة أصلاً، ولم يذكروا ذلك في ترجمته [...] قتادة وحماد بن زيد، فلو كان ابن زيد يروي عن قتادة لما أهملوا ذكر ذلك »
>
> "Ḥammād ibn Zayd has no riwāya from Qatāda, and [the biographers] did not mention it in his entry. Qatāda and Ḥammād ibn Zayd are two famous imams — if Ibn Zayd had transmitted from Qatāda, they **would not have omitted to mention it**."

A remarkable argument from silence: when two major imams are contemporary in the same city, the absence of any mention of samāʿ in the biographical dictionaries is itself proof.

### The chronology

- Qatāda dies in **117 H** at Wāsiṭ (during a ṭāʿūn), returning from [[Khālid ibn 'Abd Allāh al-Qasrī|Khālid al-Qasrī]]'s place
- Ḥammād ibn Zayd was born in **98 H**
- At Qatāda's death, Ḥammād was ~19 years old — the age is **sufficient** for samāʿ
- But he himself testifies that he had only **planned** to meet him, without succeeding

The samāʿ was chronologically possible. This is what makes the proof so strong: there is no impossibility of dates — there is a **confession** of absence of meeting.

## The confusion of the two Ḥammāds

A classic trap in hadith criticism: **Ḥammād ibn Zayd** and **[[Ḥammād ibn Salama]]** are both Basran, both from the same generation, both thiqāt. But only **Ibn Salama** transmitted from Qatāda.

The case study: Ibn Ḥazm (Muḥallā 1/90) cites a hadith through the chain ʿAffān ← **Ḥammād ibn Zayd** ← Qatāda ← Ibn Sīrīn ← Ṣafiyya ← ʿĀʾisha (hadith of the khimār). Ṭāriq ibn ʿAwaḍ Allāh demonstrates in three arguments that this is an **error** — the true transmitter is Ibn Salama, not Ibn Zayd:
1. The hadith is unanimously known as being from Ibn Salama
2. ʿAffān transmits this hadith from Ibn Salama in the Musnad of Aḥmad (6/150)
3. Ibn Zayd **never met** Qatāda (proof above)

## The iḍṭirāb of tark al-ṣalāt — Dāraquṭnī

Dāraquṭnī ('Ilal, [p. 397](https://shamela.ws/book/9082/397)) documents a major **iḍṭirāb** in the chain Ḥammād ← 'Amr ibn Dīnār ← [[Jābir ibn 'Abd Allāh|Jābir]] for the hadith of [[Traditions/Tark al-Ṣalāt|tark al-ṣalāt]]:

| Version | Transmitter from Ḥammād | Status |
|---------|----------------------|--------|
| 1 | Abū al-Rabī' al-Zahrānī | **Marfū'** (attributed to the Prophet) |
| 2 | Al-Qawārīrī | **Mawqūf** — "'Amr told me, **or one of my companions from 'Amr**" |
| 3 | Aḥmad al-Mawṣilī | **Marfū'** — "'Amr told me, **or it reached me from him**" |
| 4 | Ibn Ḥasāb | **Mawqūf** — "I heard 'Amr, **or it was reported to me from him**" |

Ḥammād hesitates about his own source: did he hear from 'Amr directly, or through an intermediary? The status oscillates between marfū' and mawqūf. And 'Amr ibn Dīnār himself did not hear from Jābir (confession to Shu'ba).

This hesitation is consistent with Ḥammād's scrupulous profile: « يقصر في الأسانيد ويوقف المرفوع ويشك كثيرا بتوقفه ». He doubts because he genuinely does not know where he got this hadith.

## Relationships

- **Transmits from** Ayyūb al-Sakhtiyānī (20 years), [[Thābit al-Bunānī]], [[Ḥumayd al-Ṭawīl]], [[Hishām ibn ʿUrwa]], Yaḥyā ibn Saʿīd al-Anṣārī (~90 shuyūkh)
- **Does NOT transmit from** [[Qatāda ibn Di'āma]] — died before the meeting
- **Transmitted by** ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Mahdī, Sulaymān ibn Ḥarb, Wakīʿ, Ibn al-Mubārak, Yazīd ibn Hārūn, ʿAlī ibn al-Madīnī
- **Confused with** [[Ḥammād ibn Salama]] (who does transmit from Qatāda)
- **Link with** [[Khālid ibn 'Abd Allāh al-Qasrī]] — Qatāda was returning from his patron's place when he died
