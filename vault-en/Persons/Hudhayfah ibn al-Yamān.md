# Hudhayfah ibn al-Yamān — the Companion with two voices

**Trusted Companion**, "keeper of the secret" (ṣāḥib al-sirr) of the Prophet — the one to whom Muḥammad allegedly confided the names of the hypocrites. Governor of al-Madā'in (Iraq) under 'Umar. Dies in Kūfa shortly after the assassination of 'Uthmān in 36H.

His case is the most spectacular in the [[Concepts/Kūfa contre le Chām|Kūfa vs. Shām]] dossier: the **same Companion**, the **same question**, two **contradictory** answers — one for the Shām, the other for Kūfa.

## The same Companion, two contradictory answers

The scenario is identical in both versions: Hudhayfah asks the Prophet what to do in case of fitna (strife, civil war). The two camps deliver two irreconcilable answers.

### Shām version — obey the ruler

Muslim 1847. Shāmī isnād:

> « الزم جماعة المسلمين وإمامهم [...] فاسمع وأطع الأمير وإن ضرب ظهرك وأخذ مالك »
>
> "Stay with the body of Muslims and their leader. [...] **Listen and obey the ruler, even if he strikes your back and takes your property.**"

### Kūfa version — study the Quran

Bazzār 2799, Ibn Ḥibbān 117. Kūfī isnād:

> « تعلّم كتاب الله واعمل بما فيه »
>
> "**Study the Book of God and act upon what is in it.**"

The two versions cannot both be authentic. The question they raise is not "which one is true?" but "why is the version that serves the ruling power in Muslim, and the one that weakens it in minor sources?"

## The question of Witr

Hudhayfah, as a Kūfī figure, is mobilized in the ritual debate between Shām and Kūfa on the Witr:

> « لا وتر لمن لم يقرأ بأم القرآن »
>
> "No Witr for one who does not recite the Mother of the Book."

This hadith excludes the Shāmīs from the Witr — those who do not recite the Quran correctly according to Kūfī standards have no right to this prayer (al-Bayhaqī, Sunan, no. 4660).

## The refusal of al-Quds

Hudhayfah categorically refuses pilgrimage to Jerusalem (Ibn Abī Shayba, Muṣannaf, no. 7617):

> « لو أعملت ناقتي حتى أنيخها عند باب بيت المقدس ما دخلته »
>
> "If I rode my camel all the way to the gate of Bayt al-Maqdis, **I would not enter it.**"

This refusal falls under question 3 of the [[Concepts/Kūfa contre le Chām|Kūfa vs. Shām]] dossier: the Kūfites reject the sacralization of al-Quds as a product of Umayyad propaganda.

## Analysis — the same figure, two camps

Hudhayfah is the textbook case of **contradictory fabrication**. The mechanism is transparent:

1. **The Shām party** selects the pro-obedience version and brings it into the canonical corpus (Muslim). The transmission circuit passes through Shāmī transmitters.

2. **The Kūfa party** selects the pro-Quran version and preserves it in its own collections. The circuit passes through Kūfī transmitters.

3. **The two circuits diverge** from the second link of the chain. The same Companion serves as authority for both camps, but the versions are incompatible.

The fact that Muslim retained the Shāmī version and not the Kūfī one is no accident: Muslim writes in the 3rd century, in an Islam where the Shām has already won. The version that serves the ruling power was selected, canonized, and taught. The Kūfī version survives — but in collections that no one reads.
