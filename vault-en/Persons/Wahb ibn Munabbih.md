# Wahb ibn Munabbih

Yemeni scholar of Persian origin (Abnā'), **generational successor** of [[Persons/Ka'b al-Ahbar|Ka'b al-Ahbar]] in the transmission of [[Concepts/Isrā'īliyyāt|isrāʾīliyyāt]]. Primary source for al-Ṭabarī on pre-Islamic universal history.

## Relationships

- **Brother**: [[Persons/Hammām ibn Munabbih|Hammām ibn Munabbih]] (principal transmitter of [[Persons/Abū Hurayra|Abū Hurayra]])
- **Met** [[Persons/Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ|Ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ]], Ibn 'Abbās, Abū Hurayra, Ibn 'Umar

## Wahb and the qadar — the champion of jabr

Wahb plays a central role in the debate on [[Concepts/Qadar|qadar]] — on the side of **absolute predestination** (jabr). His interventions systematically use the "ancient Books" to legitimize the Umayyad doctrine that everything — including evil — is predetermined by God.

### "72 books, and in each one..."

Al-Bayhaqī, al-Qaḍāʾ wa-l-qadar ([p. 150](https://shamela.ws/book/13053/150)):

> قَرَأْتُ اثْنَيْنِ وَسَبْعِينَ كِتَابًا وَأَرْبَعَةً وَعِشْرِينَ سِوَى ذَلِكَ فَمَا مِنْهَا كِتَابٌ إِلَّا فِيهِ: **إِذَا جَعَلَ الْعَبْدُ إِلَى نَفْسِهِ شَيْئًا مِنَ الْمَشِيئَةِ فَقَدْ كَفَرَ**

> "I have read **seventy-two books** and twenty-four others — and there is **not a single one** that does not contain: 'When the servant attributes to himself anything of the will, he has committed unbelief.'"

The number (72+24 = 96 books) is hyperbolic and unverifiable — but the argument is structural: the "ancient Books" (Torah etc.) would unanimously confirm that free will is kufr. This is a mobilization of [[Concepts/Isrā'īliyyāt|isrāʾīliyyāt]] in direct service of the Umayyad jabriyya doctrine.

### Preaching from the minbar

Al-Bayhaqī, al-Qaḍāʾ wa-l-qadar ([p. 132](https://shamela.ws/book/13053/132)):

> سَمِعْتُ وَهْبَ بْنَ مُنَبِّهٍ وَهُوَ يَخْطُبُ عَلَى الْمِنْبَرِ فَقَالَ: إِنِّي وَجَدْتُ فِي كِتَابِ اللَّهِ أَنَّ اللَّهَ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ يَقُولُ: **إِنَّ مِنِّي الْخَيْرَ، وَأَنَا قَدَّرْتُهُ، وَقَدَّرْتُهُ لِخِيَارِ عِبَادِي** [...] **وَإِنَّ مِنِّي الشَّرَّ، وَأَنَا قَدَّرْتُهُ، وَقَدَّرْتُهُ لِشِرَارِ خَلْقِي، فَوَيْلٌ لِمَنْ قَدَّرْتُهُ لَهُ**

> "I found in the Book of God that God says: **'Good comes from Me, I decreed it and destined it for the best of My servants [...] and evil comes from Me, I decreed it and destined it for the worst of My creatures — woe to him for whom I destined it.'**"

Wahb preaches **from the minbar** — this is a public address, not a private transmission. He attributes to "God in His Books" the doctrine that **God is the author of both good AND evil**, and that the wicked are predestined to be so. This is the jabriyya position in its purest form.

The same doctrine appears via Kulthūm ibn Jubayr (Muṣannaf ʿAbd al-Razzāq, [p. 189](https://shamela.ws/book/11333/189)):

> أَجِدُ فِي التَّوْرَاةِ: أَنَا اللَّهُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنَا، خَالِقُ الْخَلْقِ، **خَلَقْتُ الْخَيْرَ وَخَلَقْتُ مِنْ يَكُونُ الْخَيْرُ عَلَى يَدَيْهِ** [...] **خَلَقْتُ الشَّرَّ وَخَلَقْتُ مَنْ يَكُونُ الشَّرُّ عَلَى يَدَيْهِ، فَوَيْلٌ لِمَنْ خَلَقْتَهُ لِيَكُونَ الشَّرُّ عَلَى يَدَيْهِ**

> "I find in the Torah: 'I am God, there is no deity but Me, creator of all creatures. **I created good and I created the one through whose hands good will come** [...] **I created evil and I created the one through whose hands evil will come — woe to him I created for evil to come through his hands.**'"

### The propaganda hadith — "Wahb the wisdom, Ghaylān the fitna"

Tārīkh Dimashq ([p. 22339](https://shamela.ws/book/71/22339)):

> عَنْ مَرْوَانَ بْنِ سَالِمٍ، عَنْ [[Persons/Khālid ibn Maʿdān|خَالِدِ بْنِ مَعْدَانَ]]، عَنْ عُبَادَةَ بْنِ الصَّامِتِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: **يَكُونُ فِي أُمَّتِي رَجُلَانِ أَحَدُهُمَا وَهْبٌ يَهَبُ اللَّهُ لَهُ الْحِكْمَةَ وَالآخَرُ غَيْلَانُ فِتْنَتُهُ عَلَى هَذِهِ الأُمَّةِ أَشَدُّ مِنْ فِتْنَةِ الشَّيْطَانِ**

> "There will be in my community two men: one is **Wahb**, to whom God will grant wisdom — and the other is **Ghaylān**, whose temptation upon this community will be worse than that of Satan."

This is a hadith of **pure propaganda**: the Prophet would have predicted by name the opposition between Wahb (the champion of jabr) and Ghaylān al-Dimashqī (the Qadarite who was crucified). The chain passes through **Marwān ibn Sālim** (weak) and **[[Persons/Khālid ibn Maʿdān|Khālid ibn Maʿdān]]** — chief of the Homs police, close to Umayyad power.

### The pattern

```
The "ancient Books" (Torah, etc.)
         │
    WAHB (preaching from the minbar)
         │
    "God is the author of both good AND evil"
    "He who attributes will to himself = kāfir"
         │
    → Umayyad power is willed by God
    → To contest it = to contest the qadar = kufr
```

Wahb is not a mere "transmitter of isrāʾīliyyāt." He is the **theological arm** of the jabriyya doctrine — the one who provides the Umayyads with a corpus of unverifiable "scriptural proofs" (the "72 books") to sacralize predestination. His named counterpart and adversary in the tradition is Ghaylān — the Qadarite whom Hishām would have crucified.
