# Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān — the silent pivot

**Unanimously thiqa**, never contested, never questioned. And yet: he is the link through whom the most frequent chain of the Ṣaḥīḥayn to [[Abū Hurayra]] passes, it is in him that the material of [[Ka'b al-Ahbar|Kaʿb al-Aḥbār]] converges, and it is in his transmission that the problem of the **ṣaḥīfa** is hidden.

## The 1,000 hadiths of al-Aʿmash

[[Persons/Al-Aʿmash|Al-Aʿmash]] declares: « كتبت عن أبي صالح ألف حديث » — "I wrote from Abū Ṣāliḥ one thousand hadiths." The verb **kataba** (to write) — not **samiʿa** (to hear) — is ambiguous: writing **from** someone, or **copying** someone's writings?

## The Kaʿb al-Aḥbār connection

Abū Ṣāliḥ stands at the intersection of two streams:
- What [[Abū Hurayra]] transmits from the Prophet
- What Abū Hurayra reports **from Kaʿb** (and vice versa)

Muslim 1666a shows both streams in action: Abū Hurayra reports a hadith, then says: « فحدثتها كعبا فقال كعب... » — "I told it to Kaʿb, and Kaʿb said..." The traditions circulate in both directions. Abū Ṣāliḥ receives it all, without distinguishing.

## The son — Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ

[[Persons/Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ|Suhayl]] transmits from his father. But: « تُكلِّم في سماعه من أبيه فقيل صحيفة » — his audition from his father was challenged; it was said to be a **ṣaḥīfa**. Al-Bukhārī avoids him in the Ṣaḥīḥ. Muslim uses him — and Suhayl is the **only** one to elevate the hadith "tuʿraḍ al-aʿmāl" to the Prophet (Muslim 2565a), whereas four other transmitters from Abū Ṣāliḥ keep it mawqūf. → detailed entry: [[Persons/Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ]]

## The structural problem

Dhakwān is irreproachable in jarḥ wa-l-taʿdīl. Nobody contests him. But it is precisely because he is irreproachable that the chain holds — and that nobody asks the question: **where do these thousand hadiths come from?** Genuine audition from Abū Hurayra, or a copy of a manuscript that blends Abū Hurayra and Kaʿb?

The hadith no. 21 of the [[Persons/Al-Aʿmash|Nuskhat Wakīʿ]] — where al-Aʿmash no longer knows whether it is Abū Hurayra or Kaʿb — suggests that the confusion already existed **upstream**, at Dhakwān's level.
