# Al-Aʿmash — the mudallis at the center of the laundering

**Imam of the muḥaddithīn of Kūfa** and pivot of Kūfite transmission in the [[Concepts/Kūfa contre le Chām|Kūfa vs. al-Shām]] opposition, qāriʾ of the Quran, reputed for his prodigious memory and his legendary bad temper. One of the three mudallis from whom [[Shu'ba ibn al-Ḥajjāj|Shuʿba]] claims to have "protected" the corpus ([[Concepts/Kafaytukum tadlīs thalātha]]). **Pivot of the most frequent chain of the Ṣaḥīḥayn** to [[Abū Hurayra]] via [[Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān]]. Yet this chain, examined closely, leads straight to the **ṣaḥīfa of [[Ka'b al-Ahbar|Kaʿb al-Aḥbār]]**.

| Dimension | Detail |

| Death | Rabīʿ al-Awwal 148 H (Kūfa) — same year as Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq |
| Titles | "ʿAllāmat al-Islām" (Yaḥyā al-Qaṭṭān), "Sayyid al-muḥaddithīn" (Ibn ʿAyyāsh), "al-Muṣḥaf" for his truthfulness |
| Corpus | ~1,300 hadiths (Ibn al-Madīnī), 4,000 according to al-ʿIjlī |
| Qirāʾa | Read under Yaḥyā ibn Waththāb; readers: Ḥamza al-Zayyāt, Zāʾida |
| Piety | ~70 years without missing the first takbīr (Wakīʿ) |
| Character | "ʿAsir, sayyiʾ al-khuluq" — difficult, ill-tempered (al-ʿIjlī) |
| Tashayyu' | « كان فيه تشيع » — "there was Shiism in him" (al-ʿIjlī) |

## Shuyūkh — heard, ṣaḥīfa, tadlīs

### Heard (confirmed)

- **Abū Wāʾil** (Shaqīq ibn Salama) — close relationship, abundant contact
- **Ibrāhīm al-Nakhaʿī** — one of his main Kūfite masters
- **Zayd ibn Wahb**
- Khaythama, al-Maʿrūr ibn Sūwayd, etc.

### Ṣaḥīfa (written, not heard?)

- **[[Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān]]** — al-Aʿmash says: « كتبت عن أبي صالح ألف حديث » — "I **wrote** from Abū Ṣāliḥ 1,000 hadiths." The verb is **kataba** (to write), not **samiʿa** (to hear). Al-Dhahabī classifies him among the shuyūkh "abundantly frequented" — but the proof of this frequentation is the volume (1,000 hadiths), and the volume can be explained by a **ṣaḥīfa**. The parallel with Abū Sufyān is explicit: al-Bazzār says the ~100 hadiths via Abū Sufyān are not audition but "a known ṣaḥīfa." Three converging clues: (1) the verb kataba, (2) hadith no. 21 of the Nuskhat Wakīʿ where al-Aʿmash no longer knows if it is Abū Hurayra or Kaʿb — a confusion incompatible with direct samāʿ, (3) the hadith [[Traditions/Hadith Abnāʾ al-Mulūk|Abnāʾ al-Mulūk]], unanimously mawḍūʿ transmitted by this chain. Al-Dhahabī's concession (ʿanʿana "presumed connected") is the only thing keeping the chain standing — and the very fact that a concession is needed shows that the samāʿ was not obvious.

### Tadlīs (not heard or contested)

- **[[Anas ibn Mālik]]** — seen but never heard:

> « رأيت أنسا وما منعني أن أسمع منه إلا استغنائي بأصحابي »
>
> « I saw Anas, and nothing prevented me from hearing from him except that I was content with my companions. »

And more bluntly:

> « كان أنس يمر بي فأقول لا أسمع منك حديثا »
>
> « Anas would pass by me and I would say: I will not hear a single hadith from you. »

- **Mujāhid** — Ibn al-Madīnī:

> « ليس يصح للأعمش عن مجاهد إلا أحاديث يسيرة نحو من عشرة »
>
> « What is authentic from al-Aʿmash via Mujāhid is only a small number of hadiths — **approximately ten**. »

Far more circulated under his name.

- **Abū Ṣāliḥ mawlā Umm Hāniʾ** — Aḥmad: « منقطع » (interrupted) — tadlīs via al-Kalbī

- **Abū Sufyān** (Ṭalḥa ibn Nāfiʿ) — al-Bazzār:

> « لم يسمع منه شيئا وقد روى عنه نحو مائة حديث وإنما هي صحيفة عرفت »
>
> « He heard nothing from him, and he transmitted from him approximately **one hundred hadiths** — it is merely a **known ṣaḥīfa**, not audition. »

The keyword: **ṣaḥīfa**. One hundred hadiths attributed to a shaykh with no audition whatsoever, via a manuscript. The same mechanism is suspected for the chain to Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān.

## Principal students

- **[[Shu'ba ibn al-Ḥajjāj|Shuʿba]]** (~500 hadiths) — the anti-tadlīs "protector"
- **Sufyān al-Thawrī** — the most learned of his hadiths
- **Abū Muʿāwiya al-Ḍarīr** — the most precise. Al-Aʿmash himself: « أما أنت فقد ربطت رأس كبشك » — "You have well tied the head of your ram" (= you have locked your transmission)
- **Wakīʿ ibn al-Jarrāḥ** (~800 hadiths) — author of the **Nuskhat Wakīʿ**
- Sufyān ibn ʿUyayna, Jarīr ibn ʿAbd al-Ḥamīd, Ḥafṣ ibn Ghiyāth
- **Abū Ḥanīfa** — among those who transmit from him

## The tadlīs — unanimously recognized

### The rule — al-Dhahabī (Mīzān al-Iʿtidāl)

> « وهو يدلس وربما دلّس عن ضعيف ولا يُدرى به، فمتى قال حدثنا فلا كلام، ومتى قال عن تطرق إليه احتمال التدليس، إلا في شيوخ أكثر عنهم كإبراهيم وأبي وائل وأبي صالح السمان فإن روايته عن هذا الصنف محمولة على الاتصال »
>
> « He practices tadlīs and sometimes makes tadlīs from a weak transmitter without anyone knowing. When he says "ḥaddathanā," no discussion. When he says "ʿan" [from], tadlīs is possible — **except** for the shuyūkh he frequented abundantly, like Ibrāhīm, Abū Wāʾil, and Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān: his transmission from these is **presumed connected**. »

This is the exception — **al-Dhahabī's concession** — that saves the chain to [[Abū Hurayra]].

### The accusers

- **Al-Karābīsī**, **al-Nasāʾī**, **al-Dāraquṭnī** — classify him as mudallis
- **[[Shu'ba ibn al-Ḥajjāj|Shuʿba]]**: « كفيتكم تدليس ثلاثة » → see [[Concepts/Kafaytukum tadlīs thalātha]]
- **Mughīra ibn Miqsam** (Kūfite contemporary):

> « ما أفسد أحد حديث الكوفة إلا أبو إسحاق السبيعي وسليمان الأعمش »
>
> « Nobody corrupted the hadith of Kūfa more than **Abū Isḥāq al-Sabīʿī and al-Aʿmash**. »

- **Aḥmad**:

> « في حديث الأعمش اضطراب كثير »
>
> « In al-Aʿmash's hadiths there is **much confusion** (iḍṭirāb). »

And: « منصور أثبت أهل الكوفة » — "Manṣūr is more reliable than all the people of Kūfa" — al-Aʿmash included.

- **Ibn Ḥajar** (Taqrīb): « ثقة حافظ عارف بالقراءات ورع لكنه يدلس » — "thiqa, ḥāfiẓ, knowledgeable in readings, pious, **but he makes tadlīs**."

### The debate on the ṭabaqa — 2nd or 3rd?

**Ibn Ḥajar** classifies him in the **2nd ṭabaqa** (tolerable ʿanʿana) in his Ṭabaqāt al-Mudallisīn (entry no. 55, [p. 30](https://shamela.ws/book/1186/30)):

> « سليمان بن مهران الأعمش محدث الكوفة وقارؤها وكان يدلس وصفه بذلك الكرابيسي والنسائي والدارقطني وغيرهم »
>
> « Sulaymān ibn Mihrān al-Aʿmash, muḥaddith of Kūfa and its qāriʾ, practiced tadlīs — al-Karābīsī, al-Nasāʾī, al-Dāraquṭnī, and others described him as such. »

This classification — the 2nd, the "tolerated" one — is the one used in practice to save the corpus.

Yet in the critical edition of al-Maṭālib al-ʿāliya (commentary on hadith no. 2215, [p. 9918](https://shamela.ws/book/37618/9918)), the **muḥaqqiqīn** reclassify him in the **3rd ṭabaqa** (rejected ʿanʿana):

> « وأما بالنسبة لعنعنة الأعمش فإنه وإن كان في المرتبة الثالثة — والله أعلم — من المدلسين لكثرة تدليسه ولكونه يدلس عن الضعفاء والمجاهيل ولكن عنعنته هنا محمولة على الاتصال »
>
> « As for al-Aʿmash's ʿanʿana, he is in the **third category** — God knows best — of mudallis, due to the abundance of his tadlīs and the fact that he makes tadlīs from **weak and unknown transmitters** — but his ʿanʿana here is presumed connected. »

The editors themselves judge that Ibn Ḥajar's classification in the 2nd ṭabaqa is insufficient — then save the hadith in question through al-Dhahabī's concession anyway. The 3rd ṭabaqa is acknowledged in principle but circumvented in application. The 2nd "wins" in practice — because the 3rd would destroy too much material.

## The chain al-Aʿmash ← Abū Ṣāliḥ ← Abū Hurayra

**Central section.** This is the most prolific chain of the Ṣaḥīḥayn. It is also the one that leads to [[Ka'b al-Ahbar|Kaʿb al-Aḥbār]].

### [[Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān]] — the pivot

Dhakwān (d. 101 H), Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān — Aḥmad: « ثقة ثقة من أجل الناس وأوثقهم » — "thiqa thiqa, among the noblest and most reliable of people." Al-Aʿmash "wrote from him 1,000 hadiths." But Dhakwān possessed a **ṣaḥīfa** from Kaʿb al-Aḥbār, and traditions circulate in both directions between Abū Hurayra and Kaʿb, through him.

### The transmission circuit

```
Kaʿb al-Aḥbār (d. 32 H)
    │
    ├── [manuscript / ṣaḥīfa]
    │
    └── Abū Hurayra (d. ~59 H)
            │
            └── Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān (d. 101 H)
                    │                          │
                    │ [possesses the ṣaḥīfa    │ [direct audition?
                    │  of Kaʿb?]               │  or manuscript reading?]
                    │                          │
                    ├── al-Aʿmash (d. 148 H) — "1,000 written hadiths"
                    │       │
                    │       ├── Wakīʿ (800 h.) → Nuskhat Wakīʿ (41 h.)
                    │       ├── Abū Muʿāwiya
                    │       ├── Shuʿba (~500 h.)
                    │       └── Sufyān al-Thawrī
                    │
                    └── Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ (son)
                            │
                            └── → Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (sole to elevate to the Prophet)
```

### The ṣaḥīfa problem

Al-Bazzār on Abū Sufyān (Ṭalḥa ibn Nāfiʿ) — reminder: « لم يسمع منه شيئا وقد روى عنه نحو مائة حديث وإنما هي صحيفة عرفت ». One hundred hadiths = a known ṣaḥīfa, not audition. The **same mechanism** is suspected for Abū Ṣāliḥ: when al-Aʿmash says he "wrote 1,000 hadiths from him," is it samāʿ or **manuscript copying**?

For **Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ** (son of Dhakwān), the problem is explicit:

> « تُكلِّم في سماعه من أبيه فقيل صحيفة »
>
> « His audition from his father was disputed: **it was said to be a ṣaḥīfa**. »

**Al-Bukhārī avoids him** in the Ṣaḥīḥ for this reason. Muslim uses him anyway.

### The connection Abū Ṣāliḥ — Kaʿb al-Aḥbār

Muslim 1666a documents the circulation: via the chain al-Aʿmash ← Abū Ṣāliḥ ← Abū Hurayra, we see Abū Hurayra report a hadith then say:

> « فحدثتها كعبا فقال كعب... »
>
> « I told it to Kaʿb, and Kaʿb said... »

Traditions circulate in **both directions** between [[Abū Hurayra]] and [[Ka'b al-Ahbar|Kaʿb]], via [[Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān|Abū Ṣāliḥ]]. And when the transmitter no longer knows which direction it went, he puts a **shakk**.

## The Nuskhat Wakīʿ — the exhibit

### Identity

41 hadiths, single chain Wakīʿ ← al-Aʿmash ← Abū Ṣāliḥ ← Abū Hurayra. Shamela: book/23118.

### Hadith no. 21 — the doubt

Shamela [p. 23](https://shamela.ws/book/23118/23):

> عن الأعمش عن أبي صالح عن أبي هريرة **أو عن كعب شك الأعمش** قال: تُعرض أعمال العباد كل اثنين وخميس فيُغفر لكل عبد لا يشرك بالله شيئا إلا عبدا بينه وبين أخيه شحناء
>
> From al-Aʿmash, from Abū Ṣāliḥ, from Abū Hurayra **or from Kaʿb — al-Aʿmash doubts**: "The deeds of servants are presented every Monday and Thursday..."

The **shakk** is explicit: al-Aʿmash does not know if the source is [[Abū Hurayra]] or [[Ka'b al-Ahbar|Kaʿb al-Aḥbār]].

### The three variants of the same hadith

| Transmitter | Isnād | Attribution |
|---|---|---|
| **Wakīʿ** (Nuskha) | ← al-Aʿmash ← Abū Ṣāliḥ ← Abū Hurayra **aw** Kaʿb | **Doubt** |
| **Wakīʿ** (other riwāya, Ibn Ḥajar, Nuzhat al-sāmiʿīn) | ← al-Aʿmash ← Abū Ṣāliḥ ← Abū Hurayra **ʿan** Kaʿb | Abū Hurayra reporting **from Kaʿb** |
| **Abū Muʿāwiya** | ← al-Aʿmash ← Abū Ṣāliḥ **ʿan Kaʿb** | Directly **from Kaʿb**, without Abū Hurayra |

Three variants, three different attributions. The actual source converges on **Kaʿb al-Aḥbār**.

### The critics' verdict

**Al-Dāraquṭnī** (ʿIlal no. 1884) rules:

> « ومن وقفه أثبت ممن أسنده »
>
> « Those who stopped it [mawqūf] are **more reliable** than those who elevated it [marfūʿ]. »

**Ibn Rajab al-Ḥanbalī** (Fatḥ al-Bārī 3/410) broadens the observation:

> « ولو ذكرنا الأحاديث المرفوعة التي أُعلت بأنها موقوفة إما على عبد الله بن سلام أو على كعب واشتبهت على بعض الرواة فرفعها لطال الأمر »
>
> « If we listed the hadiths elevated [to the Prophet] that are actually stopped at ʿAbd Allāh ibn Salām or Kaʿb, and that some transmitters confused and elevated — **the list would be long**. »

## [[Persons/Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ|Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ]] — the son and the ṣaḥīfa

Suhayl is the **sole** one to elevate the hadith "tuʿraḍ al-aʿmāl" to the Prophet (marfūʿ) in Muslim 2565a. The four other transmitters from Abū Ṣāliḥ transmit it as mawqūf:

| Transmitter from Abū Ṣāliḥ | Hadith status | Marfūʿ or mawqūf? |
|---|---|---|
| **Al-Aʿmash** | Mudallis, shakk Abū Hurayra/Kaʿb | **Mawqūf** (at best) |
| **Muslim ibn Abī Maryam** | — | **Mawqūf** |
| **Al-Ḥakam** | — | **Mawqūf** |
| **Al-Musayyab** | — | **Mawqūf** |
| **Suhayl ibn Abī Ṣāliḥ** | Son, "it was said: ṣaḥīfa"; avoided by al-Bukhārī | **Marfūʿ** (sole) |

Five transmitters of the same shaykh. Four say mawqūf. Only one elevates to the Prophet — and it is the one al-Bukhārī distrusts. Muslim retains the marfūʿ version anyway.

## Nabīdh

Al-Aʿmash is a nabīdh drinker, like nearly all Kūfites. His reaction when told that a man does not drink (Tārīkh Ibn Maʿīn, vol.3 p.485): "May Allāh distance him — let him die of colic!" — see [[Concepts/Nabīdh, ṭilā', khamr]].

## Character — a misanthrope

Al-Aʿmash did not like people. The anecdotes are colorful:

**On common folk** — when people came to write from him:

> « هذا يعلق الدر على الخنازير »
>
> « This is hanging **pearls on swine**. »

**The barber** — one day he enters the barber's, who shaves half his head then starts talking. Al-Aʿmash gets up and leaves, **half his head shaved**: « ما كان في جملة الملائكة ولا الأنبياء ».

**Unwanted guests** — people come to his house, settle in, and talk too long. Al-Aʿmash serves them his **goats' hay** on a plate: « كلوا، فلو كان عندي شيء أطيب من هذا قدمته ».

**To Abū Ḥanīfa** — who came often and stayed long:

> « إني لأجدك ثقيلا ولو كنت في بيتك »
>
> « I find you **burdensome** even when you are at home. »

## Al-Aʿmash and power

Ibn Maʿīn draws the contrast with al-Zuhrī:

> « الزهري يرى العرض والإجازة ويعمل لبني أمية والأعمش فقير صبور مجانب للسلطان »
>
> « Al-Zuhrī accepts presentation and ijāza, and works for the Umayyads. Al-Aʿmash is poor, patient, **apart from power**. »

But the picture is not so pure. When caliph **al-Manṣūr** offers a bidra (10,000 dirhams) to the scholars of Kūfa, **Abū Ḥanīfa and Ibn Abī Laylā refuse**. Al-Aʿmash **accepts**. The relationship to power is pragmatic, not heroic.

## The methodological gap

Al-Dhahabī's concession — al-Aʿmash's ʿanʿana is "presumed connected" for his frequently attended shuyūkh (Ibrāhīm, Abū Wāʾil, Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān) — is the **only thing** preventing the chain al-Aʿmash ← Abū Ṣāliḥ ← Abū Hurayra from collapsing.

Yet this concession is an **ad hoc rule**. It exists only because the chain is too central to sacrifice. Same pattern as for the [[Traditions/Hadith of ʿUkl-ʿUraniyya|hadith of ʿUkl]]: when material is systemically important, the rules bend. When it is marginal, they apply with rigor.

If one applied the **3rd ṭabaqa** of Ibn Ḥajar (the one he defends in al-Maṭālib al-ʿāliya) instead of the 2nd (the one used in practice), all of al-Aʿmash's ʿanʿana would collapse. And with it, a considerable portion of the Ṣaḥīḥayn.

## Relationships

- **Transmits from** [[Dhakwān Abū Ṣāliḥ al-Sammān]] (1,000 hadiths), Abū Wāʾil, Ibrāhīm al-Nakhaʿī, Zayd ibn Wahb
- **Does not transmit from** [[Anas ibn Mālik]] — seen but refused to hear
- **Transmitted by** [[Shu'ba ibn al-Ḥajjāj]], Sufyān al-Thawrī, Wakīʿ, Abū Muʿāwiya, Sufyān ibn ʿUyayna, Abū Ḥanīfa
- **Mudallis of** the 2nd/3rd ṭabaqa — see [[Concepts/Kafaytukum tadlīs thalātha]]
- **Link with** [[Ka'b al-Ahbar]] via Abū Ṣāliḥ and the ṣaḥīfa
- **Entry point to** [[Abū Hurayra]] — most frequent chain of the Ṣaḥīḥayn
- **Contemporary of** Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq (died the same year, 148 H)

> [!note] Personal commentary
>
> The case of al-Aʿmash is structurally different from that of [[Ibrāhīm ibn Abī Yaḥyā]]. Ibrāhīm was ejected from the corpus — his content circulates under a pseudonym, but his name became toxic. Al-Aʿmash, for his part, is **at the center** of the corpus. Nobody rejects him. Everyone acknowledges his tadlīs. And yet the chain holds — thanks to al-Dhahabī's concession, which is less a rule than a **confession of helplessness**: if applied strictly, too much material would fall.
>
> Hadith no. 21 of the Nuskhat Wakīʿ is the piece that tips the reading. A single hadith where al-Aʿmash no longer knows if it is Abū Hurayra or Kaʿb al-Aḥbār. Just one — but it is in the most frequent chain of the corpus. If doubt is possible for one hadith, it is possible for the others. And when we see that Abū Muʿāwiya — described as the most precise of his students — transmits the same hadith directly from Kaʿb, without Abū Hurayra at all, the doubt becomes a convergent pattern.
>
> Ibn Rajab has the formula: "if we listed the hadiths elevated to the Prophet that are actually from Kaʿb or Ibn Salām, the list would be long." He does not list them. Nobody lists them. Because listing them would mean taking stock of the damage, and the inventory would be too heavy to bear.
