# Kitāb al-Aghānī (كتاب الأغاني)

Monumental anthology covering poetry, music, biographies, and court anecdotes from the pre-Islamic era through the early Abbasid centuries. A work of **adab** (belles-lettres), not hadith — reliability criteria are those of the literary genre, not hadith criticism.

## The author: Abū al-Faraj al-Iṣfahānī

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Full name | 'Alī ibn al-Ḥusayn ibn Muḥammad ibn Aḥmad al-Qurashī al-Umawī al-Iṣfahānī |
| Lineage | **Descendant of Marwān II** — the last Umayyad caliph |
| Confession | **Shiite** (Zaydite according to al-Ṭūsī) — despite his Umayyad ancestry |
| Death | 356 H (967 CE) |
| Milieu | Patronized by the **Buyids** and their vizier Abū Muḥammad al-Muhallabī |
| Doctrinal marker | Uses « عليه السلام » for 'Alī (Shiite formula), never « رضي الله عنه » |

Al-Dhahabī notes the anomaly: « شيعي وهذا نادر في أموي » — "Shiite, and this is rare for an Umayyad."

## Reception among Sunni scholars

| Scholar | Judgment |
|---------|----------|
| **Ibn al-Jawzī** (d. 597 H) | « مثله لا يوثق بروايته » — "someone like him cannot be trusted in his transmissions" |
| **Ibn Taymiyya** (d. 728 H) | « يضعفه ويتهمه في نقله ويستهول ما يأتي به » — "declared him weak, accused him in his transmissions, and found his reports scandalous" (reported by al-Dhahabī) |
| **Al-Dhahabī** (d. 748 H) | Acknowledges his expertise in adab, but judges him **layyīn** (weak) as a transmitter |
| **Ibn Khaldūn** (d. 808 H) | The definitive register of Arab culture — the only book al-Ṣāḥib ibn 'Abbād took on travels |

### Recurring criticisms

- **Broken isnāds** or ones passing through unknown transmitters
- **Promotion of loose morals** — wine, music, licentious anecdotes
- **Mathālib-type material** — scandalous anecdotes designed to discredit political rivals
- **Shiite bias** — oriented presentation of events touching the Alids and Umayyads

### What this means

The Kitāb al-Aghānī is not rejected *wholesale* by the Sunni tradition — it is too massive and too rich to be ignored. But the personal accusations it contains against Umayyad figures (sexual accusations, impiety, drinking) belong to the mathālib genre and must be treated as such: they are **hostile traditions** whose function is polemical, not documentary.

## Modern academic reception

Considered an **irreplaceable source** for Arab social, literary, and musical history. Key studies:

- **Hilary Kilpatrick**, *Making the Great Book of Songs* (RoutledgeCurzon, 2003) — first systematic study of the work's composition
- **George Dimitri Sawa**, *Musical and Socio-Cultural Anecdotes from Kitab al-Aghani* (Brill, 2019)
- **Zvikaite** (thesis, Edinburgh, 2017) — demonstrates through *redaction criticism* that the author deliberately shapes the material from a Shiite perspective, without however fabricating

## Relevance to our research — the case of [[Persons/Khālid ibn 'Abd Allāh al-Qasrī|Khālid ibn 'Abd Allāh al-Qasrī]]

Al-Aghānī devotes a long passage to Khālid al-Qasrī in **vol. 22** (Cairo ed.), belonging to the mathālib genre. Text verified via the OpenITI corpus (0362AbuFarajIsbahani.Aghani).

### Content of the passage

1. **Takhannuth** (section "تخنثه في حداثته"): Khālid described as effeminate in his youth in Medina, frequenting singers and mukhannathūn, serving as intermediary (خريت) between the poet 'Umar ibn Abī Rabī'a and women.

2. **Contested origins**: the ancestor Kurz presented as a runaway slave, possibly of Jewish origin from Taymā' — via al-Madā'inī and Abū 'Ubayda. Contradicts the Sunni claim of a Companion ancestor (Asad ibn Kurz).

3. **Final curse**: « انقضت أخبار خالد لعنة الله عليه أبدا » — explicit hostile closure, without equivalent in Sunni sources.

### What this means

This portrait is **diametrically opposed** to the Sunni construction that makes Khālid the "qaṣṣāb al-zanādiqa" (the butcher of the heretics). The gap between the two traditions does not reveal "the truth" about Khālid, but the **mechanism of constructing historical figures** through competing doctrinal traditions. See the full analysis in the entry [[Persons/Khālid ibn 'Abd Allāh al-Qasrī#The contrasted portrait — two traditions, two Khālids|Khālid ibn 'Abd Allāh al-Qasrī]].

### Note on terminology

The term used by al-Aghānī is **takhannuth** (تخنث — effeminacy), not "homosexuality." Wellhausen (1902) translates with the terminology of his era. The accusation concerns mores and social gender, within the framework of a mathālib narrative whose function is polemical.
